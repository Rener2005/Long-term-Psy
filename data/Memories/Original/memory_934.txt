# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young adult, possibly a student, who struggles with feelings of anxiety and self-doubt, particularly in relation to social interactions and perceptions of self-worth. They have faced issues of self-consciousness due to socioeconomic status and attire, which affect their interactions with peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem, though no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been indicated.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels down and anxious following an argument with a roommate, leading to feelings of isolation and self-doubt about their social standing.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user contends with negative self-perceptions based on socioeconomic factors but is open to examining and altering these thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative perceptions and affirm their self-worth beyond material appearance. Encourage the development of positive social interactions and self-confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports ongoing anxiety and self-doubt in classroom settings, specifically concerning participation and fear of judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a pattern of negative cognitive distortions, like catastrophizing about social interactions, but identifies the user's willingness to explore realistic outcomes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user to challenge and reframe distorted thoughts about social evaluations using CBT techniques. Develop strategies to enhance classroom engagement and reduce performance anxiety.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a growth in the user's self-awareness regarding anxious thoughts, with increased receptivity to rehearse potential positive interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to practice communication skills through role-playing to reduce anxiety about social interactions, utilizing CBT to foster affirming self-talk and realistic expectations.
