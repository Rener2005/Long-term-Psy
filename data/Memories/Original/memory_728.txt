# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, who identifies as female, is navigating life after a recent divorce and is struggling with emotions of failure and guilt. She is particularly concerned about how the divorce affects her daughter. A working individual, she finds it challenging to sustain motivation and focus on her job.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user demonstrates symptoms of low self-worth and anxiety, exacerbated by significant life changes. No formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling low and frustrated with herself, experiencing a persistent sense of failure related to her role in her marriage and family.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s self-critical thinking and highlights her strong emotional investment in her family, noting a reluctance to confront her daughter's feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Begin using cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user explore self-compassion and challenge negative self-beliefs. Encourage initial dialogues with her daughter to support mutual emotional processing.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious with the pressures from her recent life changes and job responsibilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's propensity for self-imposed high standards and pressures, but also her recognition of negative thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in identifying achievements to counteract self-doubt, and encourage practices like taking regular breaks to manage workplace stress and expectations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences heightened guilt and pressure from work, feeling inadequate after feedback from her boss.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes significant self-criticism tied to work performance and divorce, yet acknowledges the potential in her awareness of lacking evidence for perceived judgment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use CBT techniques to help the user recognize accomplishments and challenge the belief that perceived failure defines her. Encourage setting small, positive social engagement goals.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports initial nervousness but eventual satisfaction and a sense of accomplishment from participating in a volunteer event.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user’s growing confidence and willingness to engage in social activities, despite lingering fears of rejection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote further social activity engagement and help the user explore volunteering aligned with personal values to reinforce social connectivity and purpose.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a mix of warmth and anxiety from new friendships, struggling with trust due to past betrayals.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress in the user's ability to form connections but notes underlying fears from previous experiences impacting her trust.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive reframing techniques to encourage the user to focus on positive aspects of her new relationships. Set small social activity goals to strengthen bonds and tackle trust issues.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user notes progress in managing emotions and improving her relationship with her daughter, feeling better about personal interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes improved emotional awareness and relationship dynamics, alongside the user's drive to maintain a positive family environment.

[Consultation Goals]
