# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young individual experiencing loneliness and frustration due to familial dynamics, particularly their mother's frequent absences due to work. This feeling of isolation contributes to their anger and desire for acknowledgment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user feels symptoms of loneliness and anger, but there is no mention of a formal mental health disorder diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and angry, triggered by their mother's absence and a lack of perceived support.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user's cycle of loneliness leading to anger, which may cause further isolation. The user expresses a strong desire for connection and understanding.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user explore ways of expressing emotions safely and constructively, and consider the possibility of reaching out for support from others.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels embarrassed and angry following a conflict at school, coupled with a sense of being judged due to their appearance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's feelings of isolation and external judgment contribute to defensive behaviors and lashing out as a self-protective mechanism.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in developing strategies to manage anger and defensive reactions, such as cognitive restructuring and grounding techniques, while building positive interactions with peers.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's internalized self-doubt and negative thought patterns when faced with rejection and social stress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in challenging negative self-assessments through compassionate self-talk and positive affirmations, while encouraging engagement in stress-reduction activities.

# Fourth Consultation
