# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a university student, is struggling with feelings of isolation, vulnerability, and sentiment of being unwelcome at school after experiencing exclusion from classmates and damage to personal belongings by a roommate. The user is looking to rediscover motivation and engagement with school life despite these social setbacks.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user expresses symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, revolving around a lack of belonging and fear of social rejection, but no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been provided.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels isolated and hurt, experiencing a strong aversion towards attending school due to negative interactions with classmates, leading to feelings of hopelessness and demotivation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives the user's struggles with pervasive negative thoughts concerning social acceptance, highlighting vulnerability yet showing openness to exploring alternative perspectives and building connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to address and challenge the user's negative thoughts about social exclusion, fostering a gradual reconnection with peers and improving self-worth.
