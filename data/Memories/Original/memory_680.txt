# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Lin is a student who experiences heightened anxiety related to academic performance and social interactions. She is dedicated to improving her emotional and study habits and is working towards balancing expectations and achievements.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Lin is experiencing symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, although there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder mentioned in her self-reports.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Lin feels overwhelmed by academic pressure and fears judgment from peers and parents, reinforcing a belief in her inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Xiao Lin's struggles with cognitive distortions, particularly selective attention to perceived failures, yet sees a willingness to explore positive self-reassessments.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT techniques to help Xiao Lin identify and challenge negative thought patterns, promoting a more balanced view of her achievements and reducing academic-related anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Lin remains anxious about social interactions, driven by a need for perfection and fear of judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Lin's rigid perfectionism as a source of her social anxiety and notes her openness to role-playing exercises to build confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Xiao Lin in reframing her beliefs about social perfection using CBT, and encourage gradual exposure to social situations to alleviate anxiety.
