# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Dandan (pseudonym) is a university student who often feels lonely and self-doubtful, particularly regarding social relationships and friendships. Dandan is concerned about the perceived lack of connection with peers and is eager to cultivate a sense of belonging and improve social skills.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Dandan experiences symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem, reflected in feelings of loneliness and fear of rejection, though there is no formal mental health diagnosis disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Dandan feels lonely and anxious about friendships and perceives being excluded or distanced by peers, leading to feelings of unworthiness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Dandan's difficulty dealing with negative self-perception and perceived social rejection but notes a willingness to explore self-compassion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Dandan challenge negative beliefs and foster self-worth. Encourage small social engagements to gradually build confidence and connections.
