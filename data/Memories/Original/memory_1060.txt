# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a graduate student who struggles with feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, particularly regarding academic performance and social interactions. They recently transitioned to graduate school and have self-reported feelings of being overwhelmed and drained.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and self-doubt but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious about their academic performance and suffers from a persistent sense of inadequacy and fear of judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with negative self-comparison and a deep-seated fear of judgment, yet shows some readiness to adopt more balanced perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts and manage anxiety triggered by academic and social stressors. Encourage small achievable goals for building confidence in social interactions.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels depressed and inadequate, especially after struggling during an academic seminar, and questions their competence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user's harsh self-judgment is driven by comparisons and fear of judgment, but the user remains open to considering alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in identifying and challenging negative thought patterns. Encourage self-compassion and realistic self-assessment by breaking large tasks into smaller, manageable goals.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and overwhelmed in social situations, experiencing strong fears of judgment and embarrassment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency to catastrophize social interactions, though there's an awareness of the need to question these beliefs in the moment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to recognize and challenge extreme thoughts using CBT strategies during social interactions, focusing on manageable goals to prevent avoidance of social gatherings.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated and overwhelmed due to low energy and anxiety about group project contribution, leading to self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's struggle with critical inner dialogue and their focus on negatives, but there's recognition of past successes that contradict current negative beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Build the user's confidence by reinforcing their successes and incorporating anxiety management techniques like prioritization and exercise. Encourage a shift to a balanced perspective on performance.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more optimistic with implemented plans, though real-life issues still occasionally overwhelm them, particularly in expressing feelings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes progress in the user's confidence, with lingering anxiety about expressing feelings openly in friendships and study distractions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue strengthening stress management techniques and communication skills within friendships. Challenge negative self-talk during studies, focusing on achievements and incremental progress.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels better and is enjoying socialization but worries about maintaining progress amidst possible setbacks.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes significant improvement in the user's mood and social engagement, though there's fear about sustaining progress and guilt related to setting boundaries.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce the user's use of boundaries and self-care to maintain progress. Encourage hobby exploration as an extension of social and psychological engagement and balance.
