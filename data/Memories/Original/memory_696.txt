# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang is a university student who experiences significant social anxiety, particularly in public speaking and social settings. Wang recently had a nerve-wracking presentation, heightening these anxieties.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang shows symptoms of social anxiety, including excessive worry about perceived judgments during social interactions, though no formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels anxious and self-critical, especially regarding social interactions and fears of negative judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wang's anxiety stems from a fear of making mistakes in social contexts but shows a willingness to reframe these experiences with guidance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Wang in using cognitive behavioral techniques to challenge negative self-perceptions and develop coping strategies for public speaking and social interactions.
