# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who is experiencing significant anxiety related to social situations, particularly concerning misunderstandings at a cafeteria that have led to a fear of judgment from others. The user is reflective about personal experiences and is seeking to tackle these anxious feelings through proactive emotional management.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits signs of social anxiety and possibly generalized anxiety but has not presented any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels scared and overwhelmed about going to the cafeteria, experiencing racing thoughts and physical symptoms like a racing heart when anticipating social judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's pattern of strong negative anticipatory thoughts and anxiety responses, noting a willingness to explore alternative, rational perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify and challenge irrational thoughts about social judgment, and develop positive coping strategies through gradual exposure and self-talk.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to experience anxiety, particularly in crowded settings, although acknowledges some improvement since the previous session.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that the user recognizes their fears are often imagined or exaggerated but struggles with real-time anxiety experiences in social settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue to guide the user in challenging their distorted beliefs and practice relaxation techniques to manage anxiety. Encourage strategic exposure to social situations with peer support.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
