# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student struggling with feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, amplified by the recent death of their grandfather. They express a strong desire to meet family expectations and manage academic pressures.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed by upcoming exams and family expectations, which have intensified since their grandfather's passing.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user is experiencing significant pressure due to self-imposed and familial expectations but shows a willingness to consider alternatives to manage these feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user set manageable academic goals and explore emotional coping strategies related to their grandfather's loss.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and burdened by the recent tragic news of a classmate, leading to withdrawal from social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of self-isolation driven by the fear of being a burden and acknowledges the user's underlying need for connection and understanding.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to reach out to friends to rebuild social connections and suggest engaging in past enjoyable activities to alleviate feelings of isolation.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
