# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Megan (pseudonym) is a concerned mother worried about her son's safety and independence as he transitions to middle school and begins socializing more. She often feels overwhelmed by protective instincts and is keen on finding a balance between nurturing and allowing her son to grow independently.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Megan displays symptoms of anxiety primarily related to parenting and has strong protective inclinations concerning her son's well-being and social interactions.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Megan feels anxious and overwhelmed about her son's safety and independence, experiencing physical symptoms like stomach knots and dizziness when worrying about him.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Megan's strong protective instincts and the internal conflict she faces between ensuring her son's safety and supporting his need for independence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Megan challenge anxious thoughts and develop trust in her son's capabilities. Focus on strategies to create a balanced approach to her son's independence and reduce her anxiety.
