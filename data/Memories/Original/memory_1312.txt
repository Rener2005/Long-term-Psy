# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhao (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing anxiety, especially in social situations and struggles with feelings of inadequacy linked to past family dynamics. Zhao values self-improvement and is eager to foster better connections with peers by learning and applying better social skills.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhao exhibits symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem, partially stemming from earlier familial interactions, but there is no formal diagnosis disclosed during sessions.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhao feels anxious about social interactions, fearing judgment and experiencing physical symptoms like tightness in the chest around classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist noticed Zhao's anxiety is strongly tied to self-perception issues, but Zhao shows openness to self-compassion and exploring cognitive strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT techniques to help Zhao challenge negative perceptions and establish compassionate self-views. Introduce strategies for managing anxiety and nurturing friendships through positive affirmations.
