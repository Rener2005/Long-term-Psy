# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei (pseudonym) is an adult male who is struggling with confidence issues related to stuttering, especially in professional settings where he interacts with clients and presents in meetings. He is self-reflective and eager to improve his communication skills.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei experiences anxiety and self-doubt primarily driven by his stuttering, but there is no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious about his stuttering, particularly fearing judgment from clients during presentations, which affects his confidence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that Zhang Wei’s anxiety is fueled by negative self-perceptions and a fear of external judgment, but he is open to exploring positive reframes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use CBT techniques to help Zhang Wei reframe negative thoughts by focusing on the content of his presentations rather than the delivery. Implement relaxation techniques to prepare for speaking engagements.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels frustrated with his stuttering during meetings, leading to increased anxiety about future presentations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes that Zhang Wei is dealing with anticipatory anxiety and self-critical thoughts but sees potential in gradual exposure to speaking in smaller, less intimidating settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang Wei to practice speaking in smaller groups to build confidence and reinforce deep breathing as a pre-speech coping strategy. Challenge skewed perceptions with reminders of past communication successes.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
