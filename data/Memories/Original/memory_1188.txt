# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
H is a third-year college student who is facing inner conflicts related to relationships and a sense of loneliness. H is seeking security and is concerned about the ability to make meaningful connections with others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
H experiences anxiety and hesitance toward forming relationships, manifesting as a fear of being a burden or bothersome to others.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
H feels lost, with a significant sense of loneliness and anxiety about forming connections with others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that H struggles with anxious thoughts that hinder reaching out, yet exhibits openness and reflectiveness about emotional experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy techniques to help H identify and challenge negative thought patterns, fostering a mindset shift towards openness in forming relationships.
