# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a pseudonym for someone preferring anonymity, is dealing with emotional struggles surrounding substance use and the impact of these habits on their personal relationships. The user is navigating feelings of guilt, stress, and the desire to find healthier coping mechanisms.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user shows signs of substance dependency characterized by cravings and usage for temporary relief. However, they express motivation to change and acknowledge the negative impact of substances on their life, though no formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and confused, struggling with guilt over substance-induced conflicts and the inability to control emotions in stressful situations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's awareness of their dependency cycle and their drive to seek relief through alcohol. The user demonstrates a willingness to explore alternative coping mechanisms.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to identify and challenge negative thoughts tied to substance use. Develop small, manageable goals for stress management and establishing healthy self-care routines.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's critical struggle with cravings, noting how these moments are incited by stressful situations, underscoring the user's conflicted resolve between desire for change and compulsion.

[Consultation Goals]
