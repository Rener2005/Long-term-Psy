# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Jimmy (pseudonym) is a male in his mid-30s who has been experiencing homelessness and is struggling with securing stable housing. He expresses frustration and anger related to housing allowance issues. He has a history of alcohol use to cope with stress and feelings of inadequacy. Jimmy is determined to improve his circumstances despite past setbacks.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Jimmy experiences symptoms consistent with situational depression and anxiety due to his current living conditions and external stressors. He has not disclosed any formal diagnosis but showcases signs of low self-esteem and dependency on alcohol as a coping mechanism.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Jimmy feels overwhelmed and stuck due to his inability to secure stable housing, leading to a cycle of hopelessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that Jimmy struggles with feelings of inadequacy and self-comparison, which fuel his hopelessness. Despite these struggles, he shows willingness to try new strategies and seek help.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Jimmy identify and challenge negative thought patterns related to his living situation and self-worth. Encourage small, actionable steps towards stability and independence, such as reaching out to social services.
