# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, who remains unnamed in the sessions, identifies as a parent concerned with their health and its effects on their family. They are focused on the well-being and future of their daughter. The user has experienced health challenges recently, contributing to ongoing feelings of anxiety and sadness.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
No formal diagnosis is mentioned. However, the user reports pervasive anxiety and sadness linked to their health issues and the fear of not being present for or failing their family.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed by health-related anxieties and the perceived burden of providing for their daughter’s needs, experiencing significant sadness and occasional hopelessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user carries a heavy emotional burden tied to perfectionistic expectations and guilt, yet the user values family connection and shows resilience in difficult times.

[Consultation Goals]  
Using cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), assist the user in challenging negative thoughts around self-worth and capabilities as a parent. Develop strategies that balance self-care with family engagement, including the introduction of self-care routines and positive affirmations.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]
