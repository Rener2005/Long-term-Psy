# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who is deeply passionate about social issues but struggles with feelings of loneliness and frustration. The user feels often misunderstood by peers due to differences in social views.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user presents symptoms of social anxiety and mild depression, notably feeling isolated and disconnected from peers, but has not received a formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and frustrated, perceiving a disconnect between personal values and the surrounding environment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user is passionate about social issues, which can lead to feelings of isolation when others do not share the same level of concern.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user develop empathy and more open communication with peers, aiming to feel less isolated by initiating constructive conversations about shared interests.
