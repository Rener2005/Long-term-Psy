# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Martha is an individual who often struggles with memory lapses and worries about forgetting precious moments, especially as she navigates daily life with Alzheimer's. She cherishes spending time with her friend Ruth and engages in activities like Christmas shopping and gardening, which bring her joy and connection.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Martha has been navigating life with Alzheimer's, experiencing memory challenges but no specific diagnosis beyond self-reported concerns about memory loss impacting her daily activities.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Martha feels excited about upcoming activities but experiences moments of anxiety due to memory lapses. She expresses a willingness to capture joyful moments to reinforce them.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Martha's enthusiasm for shared activities and notes a mixture of joy and anxiety regarding her memories, highlighting her openness to using tools to aid memory.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Martha to employ cognitive behavioral techniques through journaling to solidify positive memories. Introduce methods to cope with anxiety about forgetting, focusing on enriching daily experiences with loved ones.
