# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Ming (pseudonym) is a student who is concerned about his handwriting, which he perceives as messy, despite achieving good grades. He is particularly stressed about his mother's emphasis on improving his handwriting, although he doesn't see it as a critical issue.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Ming exhibits signs of stress related to perceived inadequacies in handwriting but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ming feels frustrated and overwhelmed by his mother's focus on his handwriting, despite his academic performance not being affected by it.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Ming is hesitant to prioritize handwriting improvement, considering the effort it requires, yet he is open to exploring small, manageable steps.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Ming perceive handwriting practice as a manageable challenge. Set small daily goals to alleviate stress and foster parental understanding through shared progress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ming feels anxious and slightly hopeless about his handwriting affecting his performance, fearing teacher judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Ming's anxiety regarding his teacher's perceptions, indicating a lack of confidence in the value of handwriting practice.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT techniques to build Xiao Ming’s confidence through small, consistent handwriting practice and developing self-compassion. Encourage tracking progress to visualize improvements over time.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ming feels frustrated with perceived lack of progress in handwriting, experiencing impatience.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Ming's impatience with progress but acknowledges his willingness to consider setting smaller, achievable goals.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on setting achievable daily handwriting goals and incorporate rewards to reinforce efforts. Use CBT to reframe his perspective towards understanding that improvement takes time.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ming is motivated but occasionally overwhelmed by the effort required to maintain handwriting improvements.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Xiao Ming’s awareness of progress but also his struggle in maintaining consistency due to challenges of balancing with other activities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage establishing a structured practice schedule with set times for handwriting sessions. Use CBT to cultivate organizational skills and affirm the normality of making mistakes.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ming feels more confident due to role as recorder in group activities, pushing him to improve his handwriting.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that group responsibilities serve as motivation for Xiao Ming, who shows growth in self-assurance but still battles occasional anxiety over expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Ming by reinforcing the value of his writing improvements in group settings. Strengthen his use of positive affirmations to manage anxiety and motivate consistent practice.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ming feels proud of the recognition he has received for his improved handwriting, though he worries about maintaining progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Ming's increased confidence and willingness to implement structured practice, alongside a keen interest in enhancing his essay-writing skills.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop a personalized writing practice plan, focusing on essay skills and consistent reflection of progress. Encourage Xiao Ming to seek feedback to further boost his confidence in writing improvements.
