# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Ms. Zhang is an engineer who struggles with low energy, motivation, and feelings of inadequacy related to work performance. She is keen on integrating small lifestyle changes to improve her emotional state.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Ms. Zhang experiences anxiety, feelings of inadequacy, and occasional sadness; however, she has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ms. Zhang feels anxious and lacks confidence, especially concerning her work, but shows a sense of hope by initiating steps like morning walks.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Ms. Zhang grappling with negative self-talk and workplace anxiety but notes her willingness to challenge these thoughts and embrace gradual improvement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Ms. Zhang manage her anxiety by recognizing and challenging negative thoughts, while fostering achievable task-setting and self-reflection strategies to boost workplace confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ms. Zhang feels somewhat better due to her new routine but experiences anxiety over complex work tasks, resulting in a lack of confidence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Ms. Zhang's catastrophic thinking and its impact on her work confidence, yet she shows a readiness to explore alternative strategies to manage her workload.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use CBT to assist Ms. Zhang in breaking down tasks into manageable steps and developing constructive coping mechanisms for anxiety and doubt, enabling better work performance and self-assessment.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Ms. Zhang’s interest in exploring personal development and acknowledges her tendency to procrastinate without structured goals.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Ms. Zhang's exploration of new hobbies and personal interests by setting clear and structured goals under the SMART framework, to enhance her sense of fulfillment outside work.
