# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a middle school student, is currently preparing for high school entrance exams. They experience significant academic pressure, feeling overwhelmed by expectations from their parents and concerns about disappointing them.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user demonstrates symptoms of anxiety, primarily related to academic performance and parental expectations, without any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and overwhelmed by the pressure to perform well in upcoming exams, worrying about not meeting parental expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user is highly self-critical and experiences cycles of anxiety due to perceived academic underperformance but is open to reassessing these negative thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy techniques to help the user challenge perfectionistic thoughts and establish kinder self-assessment practices, fostering anxiety management and study balance.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and lacks motivation, stemming from recent unsatisfactory exam results and fear of not meeting expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices persistent self-doubt in the user's capabilities, compounded by the pressure to impress parents. However, the user is receptive to setting smaller, manageable study goals.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in identifying and cultivating positive study habits and self-talk techniques. Encourage awareness of incremental progress rather than perfection.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is overwhelmed and anxious, primarily concerned with disappointing academic performance and related parental expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that while the user continues to struggle with self-doubt, there is an emerging ability to acknowledge past academic improvements, indicating potential for positive change.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce strategies for setting achievable goals and acknowledging small successes. Employ CBT methods to address perfectionism and enhance self-compassion.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels increasingly confident and less anxious as they begin to prepare for exams, utilizing relaxation techniques effectively.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a positive shift in the user’s mindset toward exams, supported by visualization and relaxation strategies, suggesting growing resilience.

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance the user’s examination preparedness by integrating role-playing and visualizations. Strengthen coping mechanisms for unexpected challenges during exams.

# Fifth Consultation

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]
