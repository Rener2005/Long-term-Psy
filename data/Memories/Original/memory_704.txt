# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young individual who identifies as male and is currently dealing with feelings of exclusion and self-doubt, primarily influenced by interactions with classmates and perceived personal inadequacies. Recent conflicts and exclusion from peers at school have significantly impacted his mood and sense of self-worth.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user displays symptoms consistent with anxiety and low self-esteem but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels oppressed and anxious due to exclusion and conflicts with peers, leading to self-critical thoughts and avoidance behavior.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's tendency to withdraw from uncomfortable situations and anticipates rejection, yet recognizes a glimmer of hope and willingness to explore positivity.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative assumptions and explore balanced thinking patterns. Encourage gradual engagement with peers to reduce social anxiety and isolate feelings.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels conflicted and embarrassed after attempts to connect with classmates, leading to heightened fears of rejection and insecurity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's desire for acceptance is thwarted by overcompensation in social settings, leading to increased self-doubt and anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in developing a more relaxed approach to social interactions, using CBT methods to practice small engagement steps and manage anxiety through breathing exercises and self-affirmation.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and self-doubting after a failed social interaction, manifesting anxiety and intrusive negative self-assessments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees the user's self-criticism as fueled by past negative self-perceptions, but there is potential for growth through acknowledgment of personal strengths and pleasant social experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage recognition of personal achievements and positive traits in social contexts, guiding the user to break the cycle of self-doubt through CBT strategies and role-playing to enhance confidence.

# Fifth Consultation
