# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaoyu is an adult dealing with significant anxiety related to cleanliness and germ contamination. Recently, workplace and pandemic-related cleanliness requirements have intensified these feelings, impacting daily life and work performance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaoyu experiences symptoms consistent with obsessive-compulsive tendencies, characterized by excessive cleaning rituals and anxiety about germ exposure. There is no mention of a formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaoyu feels anxious and overwhelmed by the need to constantly clean and avoid germ exposure, leading to disruptions at work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiaoyu's heightened anxiety and irrational fears about contamination, yet recognizes his willingness to identify and challenge these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiaoyu confront and manage fears about germs and cleanliness, starting with small, gradual exposure tasks to build tolerance.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaoyu continues to feel intense anxiety related to germs, aggravated by shared spaces at work, leading to an increase in cleaning rituals.

[Observational Impressions]  
Xiaoyu's thought patterns appear exaggerated, with a tendency to imagine worst-case scenarios. However, he shows readiness to consider alternative viewpoints.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiaoyu in gradually breaking cleaning rituals through exposure techniques and positive affirmations, aiming to reduce anxiety and promote rationality in assessing cleanliness.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the association between cleanliness and control in Xiaoyu's thoughts which leads to heightened anxiety in messy settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing, to help Xiaoyu manage anxiety and discomfort related to perceived uncleanliness, fostering acceptance of imperfection.
