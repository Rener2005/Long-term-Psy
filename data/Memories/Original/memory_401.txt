# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a diligent professional in his 30s struggling with workplace anxiety, particularly regarding project management and team communication. He is driven and eager to improve his work-life balance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei exhibits symptoms of stress and anxiety primarily related to workplace pressures, although no formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious and overwhelmed by communication challenges within his team and fears the consequences of failing to meet project expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei as diligent but heavily self-critical, especially regarding leadership and team dynamics, yet willing to explore practical solutions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei identify and challenge negative thought patterns, improve team communication, and develop strategies to manage work-related anxiety effectively.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei is frustrated by a perceived lack of progress with newly tried communication strategies, doubting his abilities as a result.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei’s discouragement due to unresponsive team interactions but acknowledges his proactive approach to refining communication methods.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang Wei in enhancing communication skills through direct engagement and feedback solicitation; employ CBT to bolster confidence in his leadership capabilities.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory

The therapist observes Zhang Wei’s sensitivity to perfectionism-induced anxiety, recognizing a growing awareness of past accomplishments.

[Consultation Goals]

# Fourth Consultation
