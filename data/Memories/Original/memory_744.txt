# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a high school student experiencing significant stress and anxiety related to academic performance and familial expectations. Zhang feels pressure from her mother about grades and struggles with a cycle of doubt and procrastination when overwhelmed.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang exhibits symptoms of academic-related anxiety and stress. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder mentioned.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious and overwhelmed by upcoming exams, fearing disappointment and the inability to concentrate on studying.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's anxiety stems from the pressure to meet maternal expectations, leading to avoidance behaviors and procrastination.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang identify and challenge negative thought patterns. Encourage the development of practical strategies to manage exam-related stress, such as realistic self-assessment and relaxation techniques like deep breathing.
