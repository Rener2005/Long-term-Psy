# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Little A is a young child who often struggles with expressing emotions, specifically around feeling ignored or frustrated, and crying tends to be a common response to these situations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Little A exhibits symptoms of emotional distress, particularly when faced with situations perceived as unfair or lacking control but has not been diagnosed with any formal mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little A feels upset and cries over small inconveniences, attributing these emotions to a sense of unfairness and feeling misunderstood.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a tendency in Little A to feel overwhelmed by emotions, yet shows an openness to exploring new ways of expressing feelings constructively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Little A identify and express emotions in healthy ways and learn verbal communication strategies as alternatives to crying.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little A reports feeling anxious and overlooked, often leading to crying when attention is desired but not received.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Little A's sensitivity to perceived neglect and the strong emotional reactions this triggers, alongside a willingness to explore communication with family members as an alternative.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Little A's communication skills with family, encouraging verbal expression of feelings to mitigate feelings of neglect and anxiety using CBT techniques.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little A experiences anger and sadness, typically responding with tears when events do not go as expected.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives a habitual response to distress involving crying, yet sees potential in Little A's openness to learning alternative calming techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement CBT strategies to help Little A develop coping mechanisms such as deep breathing and positive self-talk to manage reactive emotions more effectively.

# Fourth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance Little A's skill in recognizing emotions and applying communication techniques to express desires without resorting to tears, using CBT-informed methods.

[Consultation Goals]
