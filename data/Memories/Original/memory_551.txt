# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Hao is a student at a boarding school who struggles with feelings of loneliness and separation anxiety, particularly missing his mother. Xiao Hao enjoys drawing and playing soccer but feels too shy to actively join peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Hao exhibits symptoms of separation anxiety and loneliness but has no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hao feels lonely and distracted, mainly due to missing his mother and struggling to concentrate on studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Hao's strong desire for connection and vulnerability in new environments, yet he displays openness to alternative coping mechanisms.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) methods to help Xiao Hao find healthy coping strategies for loneliness and gradually build confidence to engage in social activities.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hao continues to navigate feelings of loneliness, striving to balance his need for connection with his mother while seeking social support at school.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Hao’s willingness to express emotions and explore written self-reflection tools, showing potential for personal growth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Hao in building social connections by identifying small, achievable steps and reinforcing the use of a personal diary to process feelings and experiences.

Encourage Xiao Hao to focus on positive social interactions by adopting a mindset of exploration and curiosity. Utilize CBT techniques to reduce social anxiety and foster confidence.
