# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who struggles with feelings of anxiety and physical discomfort, often experiencing these symptoms in academic and social settings. They are working on better managing academic pressures and exploring self-expression in group settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety, particularly in situations involving academic performance and peer interactions. No formal mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious, particularly during class, and experiences physical symptoms of anxiety such as a racing heart and difficulty breathing when faced with academic challenges.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's struggle with negative thoughts about performance and peer perception, yet notes the user's willingness to try reframing these thoughts with encouragement and support.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user challenge and reframe negative thoughts through cognitive behavioral techniques, strengthen their self-compassion, and develop relaxation strategies to manage anxiety in academic settings.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels restless and anxious, particularly impacted by the noisy environment during study time, leading to concerns about academic performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's recognition of unrealistic thoughts and their capacity to identify past successful study experiences, showing potential for cognitive restructuring.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral therapy to assist the user in replacing distorted thoughts with balanced ones. Encourage the development of mindfulness and focus-enhancing techniques to manage academic stress.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and unsure about addressing disruptive behavior from classmates, which affects their focus and academic performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's hesitance in asserting boundaries yet highlights their readiness to explore assertiveness and improved coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Teach the user assertive communication skills and enhance their confidence in setting personal boundaries through role-playing and CBT techniques.

# Fourth Consultation

[Consultation Goals]

# Fifth Consultation
