# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, who has not disclosed their name, is a professional experiencing social anxiety, particularly when it comes to public speaking. The user often feels judged and nervous during these situations, leading to physical symptoms and avoidance. They seek to manage this anxiety and improve their performance in social and professional settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of social anxiety, manifested as a fear of public speaking and concern about judgment from others. There is no mention of a formal diagnosis, but the user is actively seeking strategies to cope with and reduce these symptoms.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about public speaking, reporting physical symptoms like dizziness and a racing heart, with a fear of being judged as inadequate by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with perfectionism and extreme self-pressure, leading to a heightened anxiety response, but shows a willingness to challenge and change these thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user manage and reframe negative thoughts related to public speaking. Use gradual exposure to speaking situations and implement coping strategies such as breathing techniques to alleviate anxiety.
