# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Cai is a young student dealing with quick-tempered reactions, finding it difficult to manage anger. Xiao Cai has recently been involved in conflicts with classmates and expresses frustration at an inability to control explosive emotional responses.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Cai experiences issues primarily related to anger management and impulse control but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Cai feels overwhelmed by sudden bursts of anger and is confused about handling these emotions, often acting impulsively during conflicts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Xiao Cai's tendency toward impulsive reactions driven by strong emotions, indicating a lack of effective emotional regulation strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Cai recognize the signs of building anger and develop alternative, calming strategies to manage impulsive responses.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Cai remains frustrated with her emotional responses, particularly when feeling criticized or unfairly treated, leading to confrontational behavior.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Cai's emotional reactions are often triggered by perceived criticisms and authority challenges, suggesting underlying sensitivity to feedback.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Xiao Cai through identifying thought patterns contributing to anger, focusing on recognizing personal triggers, and developing responses that promote calm communication.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Cai continues to feel entangled in conflicts, struggling with unjust situations and impulsive reactions during disagreements.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Cai’s desire to assertively express feelings yet struggles to balance emotion with calmness in confrontational settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Teach Xiao Cai the use of "I statements" to express discontent without confrontation, fostering constructive communication and emotional awareness in interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Cai is curious about improving peer relationships but still experiences minor conflicts during group activities due to frustration and feeling unheard.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees a developing awareness in Xiao Cai about how emotions affect interactions, with an openness to learning new communication strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the use of "I statements" to express thoughts in group settings, alongside strategies for active listening and collaborative engagement in team activities.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Cai enjoys leadership roles but worries about capability, balancing excitement with fear of underperformance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes Xiao Cai's burgeoning confidence in leadership, yet notes anxiety related to maintaining strong performance and handling feedback.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce Xiao Cai's leadership skills by encouraging initiative and feedback receptivity, promoting self-assurance through proactive engagement and support from peers.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Cai feels improvement in school behavior but acknowledges ongoing difficulty managing home dynamics and familial tension.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Cai’s progress in emotional regulation at school, yet highlights continued challenges with family interactions, marked by feelings of frustration and defensiveness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop strategies for Xiao Cai to manage emotional responses in familial settings, introducing deep breathing and counted pauses to facilitate calmer communication and understanding within the family.
