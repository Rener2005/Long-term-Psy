# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student dealing with feelings of fatigue and disinterest in activities that once brought joy, particularly in relation to academic pressure and unclear future plans. They express a need for guidance in managing these overwhelming emotions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of low mood and anxiety, primarily related to academic stress, though there is no indication of a formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels drained and disconnected from activities once enjoyed, struggling with concentration and a perceived loss of meaning in life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with pervasive negative self-talk and appears caught in a cycle of self-doubt, yet shows potential for reframing these thoughts through self-compassion and small, actionable steps.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user break the cycle of negative thoughts, focusing on self-compassion, and introduce manageable actions to rebuild interest in daily activities, particularly academic tasks.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels isolated due to conflicts with their roommate, grappling with embarrassment and a tendency to avoid confrontation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s internal conflict between addressing issues and fear of confrontation, suggesting prior experiences of conflict impacting current interactions, but also showing readiness to develop new communication skills.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT techniques to enhance the user’s conflict-resolution skills, encouraging open dialogue and self-expression through "I" statements, and explore the potential benefits of peer support.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to establish structured study routines and recognize personal achievements, using CBT techniques to shift perspective and reinforce self-compassionate affirmations.
