# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang is a university student who reports experiencing anxiety, particularly heightened by academic pressures and social interactions. Recent events, such as a roommate's stolen phone, have exacerbated these feelings. Zhang is committed to improving emotional well-being and coping with stressors effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang struggles with anxiety predominantly characterized by compulsive behaviors and social concerns, though no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels overwhelmed and anxious due to increased academic pressure and safety concerns, leading to compulsive checking behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang's compulsive checking stems from a fear of loss and security, influenced by past experiences, yet Zhang shows willingness to apply positive advice and insights.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Zhang in challenging compulsive thoughts and behaviors by utilizing evidence-based reasoning and reducing anxiety through structured mindfulness practices.
