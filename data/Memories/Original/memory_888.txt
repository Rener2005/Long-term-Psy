# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who struggles with feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, particularly concerning academic performance and meeting parental expectations. They are eager to develop better time management skills to reduce procrastination.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user reports symptoms indicative of anxiety and stress, but there is no mention of a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and overwhelmed by the pressure of upcoming exams and struggles with procrastination rooted in perfectionism.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that the user's pursuit of perfectionism leads to avoidance and anxiety, underlining the difficulty in aligning personal expectations with realistic outcomes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge perfectionistic thoughts, break down tasks into manageable goals, and establish a less pressured timetable for assignments.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences feelings of shame and stress, mainly due to self-blame and procrastination, further exacerbated by critical feedback from a professor.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant perceives that the user is caught in a negative cycle of self-blame and harsh self-criticism, yet there is recognition of past successful moments that could be leveraged for change.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in shifting from negative self-labeling to recognizing past successes, encouraging task breakdown into smaller, manageable parts, and fostering a more self-compassionate attitude.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains overwhelmed by academic pressures and paralyzed by a fear of not achieving perfection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notices deeply ingrained perfectionistic beliefs from upbringing, understanding that the user identifies their self-worth with academic performance but shows readiness to consider alternate viewpoints.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to adopt balanced thinking, viewing achievements through non-perfectionistic lenses, and practice breaking down assignments into smaller tasks to combat anxiety.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user expresses hope alongside anxiety, striving to change procrastination habits but feeling inconsistent with time management strategies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant recognizes that while the user attempts new strategies, inconsistency arises when under stress, reflecting a need for consistency in application.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on establishing consistent study routines, reinforce using short, achievable goals, and incorporate peer support as part of the coping strategy to maintain new habits.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling more confident and motivated after positive feedback from peers and achieving personal study goals.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes a constructive shift in the user's perception of study habits and motivation derived from peer interaction and goal achievement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce structured study planning and encourage sustainable motivation through consistent goal-setting and reward systems, addressing potential stress triggers.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports satisfaction with reduced procrastination and improved confidence in managing studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that the user is adopting effective task management strategies and benefits from self-reinforcement and increased motivation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in further embedding the routine of task segmentation and the Pomodoro technique, and review the techniques' efficacy, making adjustments as needed for sustained progress.
