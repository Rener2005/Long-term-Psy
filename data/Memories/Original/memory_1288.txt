# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang (pseudonym) is a young adult navigating significant life stressors, including the recent tragic accident of a cousin and interpersonal strains with a boyfriend. Yang feels overwhelmed, particularly in social situations and experiences a sense of not measuring up to peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang displays symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, manifesting as avoidance behaviors and pervasive thoughts of being judged unfavorably, but has not disclosed any formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels overwhelmed and anxious, particularly in social situations, where there is a persistent worry about being judged.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Yang appears trapped in a cycle of negative self-evaluation and avoidance, but is open to exploring these feelings and considering kinder self-perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang recognize and challenge negative thought patterns, fostering self-compassion and reducing avoidance in social situations.
