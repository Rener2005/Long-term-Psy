# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student who struggles with feelings of anxiety, especially concerning academic performance and efficiency in study habits. Li is experiencing pressure due to upcoming exams, marking it as a first experience with university-level assessments.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of anxiety related to academic pressures but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed and anxious about the upcoming university exams, with a persistent fear of not being adequately prepared.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li holds perfectionistic beliefs that lead to self-doubt and anxiety but shows openness to finding alternative, more compassionate perspectives based on self-guidance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li challenge perfectionistic thoughts and foster realistic study goals, emphasizing understanding over memorization and implementing relaxation techniques to manage anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious about the effectiveness of study plans and experiences self-doubt around academic preparedness, leading to stress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li has high self-imposed standards and engages in repetitive behaviors like note checking, which heightens anxiety but shows openness to structured study techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce the Pomodoro Technique to manage study time and integrate consistent relaxation practices. Use CBT to reduce perfectionism by highlighting adaptive strategies and supporting flexible thinking.
