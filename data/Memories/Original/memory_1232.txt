# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, referred to as C, is a young adult experiencing intense anxiety and obsessive thoughts, particularly related to cleanliness and hygiene. C's anxiety has been exacerbated by recent intimate contact with their partner, leading to increased compulsion around cleaning and frequent visits to the clinic for reassurance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
C exhibits symptoms consistent with anxiety and obsessive-compulsive tendencies but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
C feels exhausted and overwhelmed by pervasive thoughts about hygiene and fear of infection, which interfere with daily functioning.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that C struggles with constant anxiety and compulsions, yet shows insight into the irrational nature of their fears and a willingness to challenge them.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help C identify and challenge irrational thoughts about infection and hygiene. Begin to establish strategies for managing anxiety and obsessive behaviors.
