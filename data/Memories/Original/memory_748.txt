# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student who struggles with feelings of inadequacy and social anxiety, particularly in academic settings and peer interactions. Li is reflective about personal experiences and is trying to adjust to life in a new city, away from home.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly in social and academic environments, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels down and isolated, questioning the sense of belonging in the university after receiving average grades, leading to doubt and anxiety about academic competence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li is caught in a cycle of negative self-comparison, with anxiety inhibiting social and academic engagement, but shows openness to adopting more balanced perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Li in challenging negative automatic thoughts, focusing on the reframing of self-perception and enhancing engagement in class through manageable social interaction steps.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and frustrated after being unable to express thoughts during a group discussion, leading to feelings of isolation and self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li’s fear of judgment and pervasive belief of inadequacy in social interactions, stemming from an overemphasis on perceived negative evaluations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Li in recognizing and challenging cognitive distortions regarding social capabilities. Use small, attainable social goals to gradually rebuild confidence and connection within the university environment.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels down and anxious after unsuccessful attempts to socialize, leading to feelings of embarrassment and fear of rejection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes Li’s high self-awareness of automatic negative thoughts and a readiness to explore alternative, less judgmental approaches to social interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Li in developing confidence in social settings by employing prepared conversational strategies and utilizing self-reflection to integrate past positive interactions, promoting gradual social exposure.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li reflects on the difficulty of adapting to city life, expressing feelings of loneliness and homesickness, specifically connected to communication barriers like accent differences.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an emerging self-awareness and motivation in Li to overcome isolation, appreciating the importance of familiarity and cultural connection in social adaptation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li to participate in culturally relevant activities to share personal experiences and enhance social bonds. Utilize CBT techniques to reframe perceptions of difference as strengths in fostering new connections.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li experiences growing confidence and acceptance within university life, though moments of self-doubt persist when comparing to peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist identifies Li's appreciation of personal progress and a mindful awareness of intermittent challenges with self-comparison, alongside a willingness to set goals for further personal and academic growth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Build on Li's self-efficacy through structured goal-setting for class participation and club involvement, reinforcing the recognition of unique strengths and achievements to counter bouts of self-criticism.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels more confident and valued after a positive experience at a university exhibition, gaining motivation from peer feedback and participation in a study group.

[Observational Impressions]
