# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a high school student who has recently transitioned to living in dorms and is struggling with academic pressures. They have been feeling anxious and disappointed, especially after receiving unsatisfactory grades in their first English exam.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety linked with academic performance and self-worth, but there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder reported.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious, tense, and disappointed due to the stress of a new academic environment and a poor exam result. 

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user holds high expectations for themselves and experiences negative self-thoughts like feeling inadequate after a setback.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative self-perceptions and develop healthier coping mechanisms. Encourage the establishment of structured study routines and self-care activities to manage anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel overwhelmed by the pressure to excel in their studies, struggling with focus and the fear of not meeting parental expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user internalizes pressure, linking self-worth to academic performance, but shows an openness to shifting focus from grades to learning enjoyment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in reframing their perceptions of self-worth and academic pressure, using CBT techniques to foster a growth-focused mindset. Encourage realistic goal-setting and self-compassion.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences anxiety about future academic performance and feels inadequate due to recent disappointing exam results.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s tendency to equate academic setbacks with personal failure but notices growing awareness of alternative, healthier narratives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user disconnect self-worth from academic outcomes. Promote active learning techniques and relaxation practices to alleviate academic stress.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a sense of accomplishment after positive group interactions, leading to enhanced confidence and reduced social anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes significant progress in the user's self-expression and confidence levels. The user shows a willingness to engage more actively in social and academic life.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support continued development of social skills and bolster confidence by encouraging participation in collaborative activities. Foster strategies for balancing self-improvement with self-acceptance.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

The user feels proud and motivated following success in a speech competition, expressing eagerness to pursue more extracurricular activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s enhanced self-confidence and aspiration to expand interests. There's recognition of previous negative thought patterns and a shift toward constructive actions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to explore new interests and overcome initial social hesitations, leveraging the user's inherent motivation and CBT insights to manage negative self-talk.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels fulfilled and confident, particularly in their artistic pursuits and involvement in school activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes growth in the user's self-esteem and a healthy attitude towards personal goals and creative expression.
