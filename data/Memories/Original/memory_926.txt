# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student experiencing feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, primarily related to academic pressure and social isolation. The user is open to discussing emotional challenges and reflects on the influence of parental expectations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user demonstrates symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health condition.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels down and overwhelmed, with a fear of academic failure and a persistent sense of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's struggle with negative self-perceptions and a strong sense of pressure from unmet expectations, balanced by a willingness to explore kinder self-talk.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user reframe negative thoughts and promote self-compassion, while developing manageable study strategies to alleviate academic stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains overwhelmed and anxious due to increased academic pressures, experiencing guilt related to perceived performance shortcomings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's deeply ingrained beliefs about perfection and parental approval, recognizing the need to address cognitive distortions like all-or-nothing thinking.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in acknowledging and challenging unrealistic beliefs, fostering self-compassion, and building flexible study habits to reduce anxiety and improve academic focus.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and uncertain about future prospects due to declining grades, impacting motivation and increasing the fear of failure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes the cyclical nature of the user's anxiety and learned helplessness, with a clear opportunity to instill a sense of agency and planning.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user regain a sense of control with structured study plans and small, achievable tasks. Encourage mindfulness practices for stress management.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about fitting into social settings despite efforts to engage more, fearing judgment and social mistakes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's underlying fear of judgment which contributes to social anxiety, noting progress in user-initiated goals and openness to new strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in adopting perspective-shifting techniques to reduce fear of social judgment, while setting small, manageable social interaction goals.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports feeling better and more empowered after participating in social clubs, experiencing decreased fear and increased willingness to engage in conversation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's growing confidence and success in forming social connections, with an improvement in self-expression and reduced anxiety about judgment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in maintaining momentum in social engagement, practicing spontaneous conversation and group interaction strategies, while reflecting on social experiences to build self-awareness.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels good about recent social interactions and emotional management, expressing pride in improved communication with peers and greater emotional control.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges the user's significant progress in handling social situations, noting continued mild anxiety that is manageable with current strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage reflection on social interactions to build insight and resilience, prepare for possible setbacks with growth-oriented reframing, and reinforce strategies for managing social anxiety.
