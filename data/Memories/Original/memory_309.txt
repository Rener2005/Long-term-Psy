# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a professional experiencing increased work pressure and tensions in friendships. Recently, Zhang Wei has felt overwhelmed by looming deadlines and a sense of isolation, believing that friends do not understand his struggles.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei experiences symptoms of anxiety, particularly related to work performance and interpersonal connections, though no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious and overwhelmed by work deadlines and isolated due to a perceived lack of understanding from friends.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's tendency to doubt his abilities and internalize criticism, but he shows a willingness to explore proactive communication strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei reframe negative self-talk and improve self-perception. Encourage strategies such as open communication with colleagues and friends to manage workload and stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei continues to feel overwhelmed and inadequate, freezing up with pressure and worrying about his job compatibility.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei's persistent belief in inadequacy, yet identifies his openness to leverage past successes in overcoming current challenges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Zhang Wei in recognizing external pressures alongside personal capabilities. Encourage reaching out for support to enhance teamwork and alleviate anxiety.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Zhang Wei’s efforts to engage openly with friends to foster deeper connections, emphasizing the value of mutual support within relationships.
