# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student in their early twenties who has been dealing with anxiety and self-consciousness following a personal incident involving their sister. These feelings are particularly heightened in social contexts where the user fears judgment and struggles with confidence in interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences social anxiety characterized by self-doubt and fear of negative judgment from others. There is no mention of a formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling anxious and self-conscious, grappling with guilt and embarrassment, which hinders social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives the user as burdened by past experiences, yet notes the user’s insight into how these experiences affect current thought patterns and interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify and challenge negative thoughts, emphasizing self-acceptance and encouraging initial steps towards social engagement.
