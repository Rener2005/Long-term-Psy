# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a graduate from a prestigious university, currently enrolled in a master's program in chemistry but feeling disinterested in the field. They experience pressure from their family, who hold high expectations for them to succeed academically. The user has developed a keen interest in the cultural practice of cricket fighting and aspires to advocate for its recognition as cultural heritage.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences anxiety related to academic performance and family expectations but has not disclosed any formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels conflicted and frustrated about their academic disinterest and family expectations, expressing a lack of excitement toward their current studies in chemistry.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's struggle between family expectations and personal interests, noting their willingness to explore non-traditional passions like cricket fighting.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in exploring their interest in cricket fighting within an academic framework by employing cognitive behavioral techniques to align personal interests with academic pursuits. Encourage open communication with family regarding personal passions and interests.
