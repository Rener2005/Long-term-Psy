# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhao Lin is a young professional who experiences significant anxiety related to health and work performance. He is under increased stress due to work pressures and has developed compulsive health-checking behaviors as a coping mechanism.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhao Lin exhibits symptoms consistent with anxiety, particularly health anxiety, though there is no mention of a formal diagnosis of an anxiety disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhao Lin is feeling heightened anxiety primarily due to work-related stress and an ongoing fear of health issues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhao Lin's tendency towards compulsive health-checking behaviors, driven by fear and anxiety, but also observes a willingness to explore alternatives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhao Lin identify and challenge anxiety-driven thoughts and behaviors. Encourage the development of alternative coping strategies such as distraction techniques and gradual reduction of health-checking habits.

# Second Consultation
