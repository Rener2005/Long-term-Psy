# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Kiran is a student who is experiencing significant feelings of guilt and anxiety following a recent breakup. These feelings are impacting her motivation and social interactions. Kiran is reflective and eager to understand her emotional responses better.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Kiran shows symptoms of anxiety and self-doubt but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Kiran feels an ongoing sense of guilt about her breakup, which is affecting her motivation and social behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Kiran's tendency to blame herself for the breakup, reflecting cognitive distortions that could benefit from reframing.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT to help Kiran identify and challenge cognitive distortions regarding the breakup, promoting self-compassion and exploring healthy social engagement strategies.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue to use CBT to challenge and reframe Kiran's negative thoughts, promoting confidence in social settings and reducing social avoidance.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Kiran recognize intrinsic worth beyond achievements using CBT, implementing affirmations and self-kindness practices to support this reframing.
