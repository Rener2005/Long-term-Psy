# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wei (pseudonym) is a student struggling with anxiety related to public speaking and self-consciousness in social settings. Wei is determined to overcome these challenges and is actively seeking strategies to manage anxiety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wei experiences symptoms of social anxiety, particularly in performance situations, but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wei feels anxious about speaking in class and fears being judged by peers, resulting in physical symptoms like sweating and shaking.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wei's anxiety is driven by fear of negative judgment and self-doubt, yet Wei shows willingness to challenge these perceptions with supportive interventions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wei reframe negative thoughts and reduce anxiety in social situations. Encourage gradual exposure to public speaking in low-pressure contexts.
