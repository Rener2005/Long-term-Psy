# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is a hospital staff member who recently had surgery and is dealing with feelings of frustration and marginalization. She was moved to the logistics department post-surgery, which has impacted her sense of belonging and value within her workplace. Li seeks to regain a sense of self-worth and navigate her emotional responses more effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences feelings of anger, frustration, and anxiety, particularly concerning her employment and voice issues post-surgery. She has not disclosed any formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels angry and frustrated about being overlooked by her workplace post-surgery, leading to anxiety and self-doubt regarding her contributions and abilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's strong feelings of resentment and frustration, yet acknowledges her ability to recognize a lack of evidence supporting her negative self-perceptions and her openness to considering alternative views.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li reframe her perceptions of her role at work and shift her focus from feelings of inadequacy to identifying growth opportunities and resilience. Encourage her to manage negative emotions and support more constructive communication with colleagues and family.
