# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu Hua (pseudonym) is an adult who struggles with obsessive-compulsive tendencies, primarily manifested in compulsive checking behaviors like repeatedly checking if doors are locked or devices are turned off. Liu describes living alone, which heightens anxiety about safety and control.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu Hua exhibits symptoms consistent with obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD), focusing particularly on compulsive checking due to an overwhelming fear of potential disasters if things are left unchecked.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu Hua feels anxious and overwhelmed by compulsive thoughts, particularly related to safety checks, leading to a cycle of repetitive checking behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Liu's heightened anxiety around safety and a strong need for reassurance to calm fears related to control and responsibility, yet notes an openness to change through cognitive interventions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Liu identify and challenge compulsive thoughts and behaviors. Introduce strategies to trust initial decisions and reduce repetitive checking through alternative coping mechanisms, like journaling and mindfulness.
