# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Y is a student who has recently returned to school and is feeling overwhelmed by academic pressure. Y is worried about not being able to catch up with classmates and struggles with anxiety and feelings of not belonging.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Y experiences symptoms of anxiety, particularly in relation to academic performance and self-worth. No formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y is anxious and overwhelmed by the pressures of returning to academic life, leading to sleep difficulties and avoidance behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Y's tendency to avoid challenging situations due to fear of failure and disappointment, but notes Y's openness to trying small steps forward.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to manage academic anxiety by setting incremental study goals and fostering positive communication with family to alleviate stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels neglected and low, deeply affected by the parents' divorce and perceiving a lack of attention from the father.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Y's heavy feelings of being a burden are tied to changes in familial relations but sees potential in reframing these perceptions toward understanding and communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower Y to communicate feelings within family relationships using cognitive strategies to reframe perceptions and reduce feelings of neglect and burden.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y is experiencing anxiety related to social interactions with classmates, which stems from a fear of judgment and past misunderstandings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Y’s efforts to build social connections are hindered by intrusive negative thoughts but sees potential in Y’s desire to challenge and alter these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use CBT techniques to challenge negative thought patterns about social judgments, focusing on evidence-based assessments and fostering positive peer interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels overwhelmed by academic demands and is frustrated with setting high expectations, resulting in guilt and anxiety about falling behind.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes Y’s strong self-criticism and academic comparisons but also notes a willingness to develop a structured approach toward managing workload.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Y in creating a balanced study plan that incorporates acknowledgment of progress alongside realistic goal-setting, utilizing cognitive and visual study strategies.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y is experiencing improvements in self-satisfaction from academic results but continues to struggle with unexpected waves of anxiety and self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Y's capacity for positive reflection and recognition of success, and notes the positive impact of engaging in enjoyable activities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Y to apply cognitive techniques to remind themselves of past achievements during anxiety surges and incorporate relaxing activities to maintain balance.
