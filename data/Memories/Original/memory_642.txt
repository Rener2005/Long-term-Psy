# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Little Y is a young child who struggles with feelings of loneliness due to a busy family environment. He is interested in playing with peers and enjoys activities such as puzzles and building with blocks.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Little Y displays signs of social anxiety and loneliness, particularly related to interactions at school, but there is no formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little Y feels lonely and hesitant about approaching other children due to concerns about negative reception and not knowing what to say.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Little Y is eager to connect with peers but is hindered by self-doubt and uncertainty in social situations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Little Y gain confidence in initiating social interactions, focusing on practice and positive self-talk to ease social anxiety.
