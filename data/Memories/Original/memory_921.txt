# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wen (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with social anxiety and feelings of loneliness. Zhang describes feeling shy and worries about judgment from others, especially in social interactions and class activities.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences social anxiety, characterized by fear of negative evaluation and avoidance of social situations, although there's no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels low and anxious, particularly about social interactions and fitting in with classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang's tendency to avoid social engagement due to fear of judgment but notes Zhang's insightfulness and desire to improve.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang challenge negative thoughts and initiate small steps toward social engagement. Encourage the use of positive affirmations to build confidence.
