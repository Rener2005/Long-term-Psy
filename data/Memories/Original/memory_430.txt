# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a working parent, a teacher by profession, who is feeling overwhelmed due to a hectic work schedule and worries regarding their relationship with their son, James. The user is anxious about maintaining a strong connection with him amidst their busy life.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety related to work-life balance and concerns about parental adequacy. There is no formal diagnosis disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious about their inability to connect with their son due to a busy work schedule.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's persistent worry about losing connection with their son, along with feelings of guilt and apprehension about parental inadequacy.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help the user identify and challenge negative thoughts regarding their relationship with their son. Develop practical strategies to facilitate communication and alleviate anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels disappointed and exhausted, expressing frustration over their son's lack of engagement despite efforts to improve communication.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user experiences self-doubt and heightened anxiety when their efforts to communicate do not meet expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in recognizing and focusing on small positive interactions with their son while managing expectations. Encourage using interests to bridge communication gaps, fostering patience and understanding.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's willingness to connect through shared activities, yet there's a struggle with overthinking outcomes and fear of embarrassment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to focus on the experience of shared activities rather than outcomes. Reinforce confidence in communication attempts and reduce anxiety through realistic expectation setting.
