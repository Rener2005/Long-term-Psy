# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student struggling with social anxiety, particularly in adapting to dorm life and connecting with classmates. They have expressed feelings of inadequacy and fear of judgment in social settings and are reflective about their experiences and the desire to improve their social skills.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user displays symptoms of social anxiety, characterized by intense nervousness and avoidance behaviors in social interactions, though no formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious about social interactions, particularly in the context of dorm life, expressing a fear of being judged and a desire to withdraw.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with self-critical thoughts and anxiety in social scenarios, showing a readiness to reflect and experiment with new strategies despite apprehension.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative assumptions and build confidence in social interactions by setting small, attainable goals for engagement and practicing relaxation techniques to manage anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains anxious in social settings, though they have made efforts to interact more, feeling tense and unsure during group activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user's anxiety is still significant, yet they are starting to identify patterns of self-critical thinking and remain open to altering these perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to lower self-expectations in social scenarios and apply cognitive techniques to recognize and challenge automatic negative thoughts, progressing towards self-compassionate self-talk and confidence-building exercises.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel anxious in group settings, worried about saying something wrong, with social scenarios contributing to a sense of overwhelm impacting their studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of the user feeling unnoticed, though they are beginning to see the benefits of reframing thoughts and understanding that others may share similar insecurities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in creating opportunities for personal contribution in group discussions and employing cognitive techniques, such as thought reframing, to foster a more positive self-image and increased participation.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is attempting to participate in new social activities but remains anxious, fearing judgment and experiencing physical symptoms like a racing heart.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's tendency to withdraw in fear of judgment but also sees potential in exploring how they can shift focus to more positive social perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in challenging assumptions about being judged through cognitive restructuring and set intentions for small, achievable social engagements to incrementally build social confidence and acceptance.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user has made progress in connecting with classmates, experiencing less frequent but still present anxious episodes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user feels encouraged by positive interactions and is beginning to appreciate their ability to contribute socially, marking a shift towards greater self-esteem.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce positive social experiences and practice consistent cognitive behavioral strategies to maintain progress, with a focus on fostering genuine curiosity in interactions and establishing deeper social connections.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling better overall, noting a decrease in the intensity of anxiety and an ability to engage more comfortably in social situations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a significant improvement in the user's confidence, highlighted by proactive involvement in study groups and social discussions, pointing to effective implementation of coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Sustain momentum by exploring specific conversational openings that align with personal interests and rehearse cognitive exercises, including visualization, to enhance comfort during group presentations and continued social adjustment.
