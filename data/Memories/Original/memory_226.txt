# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who recently experienced disappointment after not being elected to the student council. This event has led to feelings of isolation and frustration due to perceived favoritism impacting the election results. The user has a history of being a class leader in high school but now feels a loss of identity in the current academic environment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user demonstrates signs of social anxiety and self-doubt but has not been formally diagnosed with any mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels isolated and frustrated after not being elected to the student council, with concerns about peers' perceptions and a sense of lost identity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's struggle with negative thoughts about self-worth and perceived external judgment, but also an openness to exploring these perceptions and reconnecting with peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to challenge negative thoughts and perceptions through cognitive behavioral techniques. Focus on initiating reconnection with peers to reduce isolation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels disappointed by superficial interactions with roommates and struggles with sustaining deeper connections.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's hesitations stem from past social experiences and a fear of being unliked, yet recognizes the user's desire to build meaningful relationships.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in exploring the origins of negative self-thoughts and develop strategies to engage in more authentic interactions, using evidence to counteract fears of rejection.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels nervous about participating in class activities due to fear of judgment and fitting in but expresses a desire to be more involved.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's positive interactions, highlighting moments of connection, but acknowledges ongoing anxiety about social acceptance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in recognizing positive social experiences and developing small, manageable goals for increasing participation, supported by CBT techniques.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more comfortable and included after forming new friendships, yet remains uncertain about leading group activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes growing self-assurance, particularly in making connections, but notes hesitation in leadership roles due to lingering self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to identify and reinforce self-affirmations that recognize past leadership successes. Support taking initiative in organizing activities to build confidence.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user enjoys organizing social gatherings and feels a sense of fulfillment but is cautious about ensuring everyone enjoys the events.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees the user's enhancement in confidence and social skills, recognizing the positive impact of their initiatives on the social environment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in refining event-planning strategies, managing anxiety related to participation, and emphasizing the importance of enjoyment over perfection.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences a significant boost in confidence after a successful team-building activity, leading to greater social engagement without anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's newfound liberation in social settings and willingness to lead discussions, resulting in positive peer reinforcement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to sustain confidence by setting realistic social goals and using past successes to bolster self-esteem in new interactions.
