# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young adult dealing with feelings of heartache and uncertainty following a recent breakup. They are also managing stress related to academic performance and social situations, seeking to rebuild self-confidence.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem following a breakup but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and preoccupied with thoughts of inadequacy following a breakup, impacting their focus on academic responsibilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency to internalize the breakup as reflecting personal inadequacy, yet notes their openness to recognizing supportive relationships as evidence of their worth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user explore and challenge negative self-talk, focusing on increasing outward engagement and developing positive self-affirmations.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels isolated and struggles with self-worth in social situations, believing they are not worthy of others' attention.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's deeply rooted negative self-perceptions, yet highlights their growing ability to recognize moments of positive social interaction.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user identify and challenge negative beliefs about their social worth, encouraging the use of positive past experiences as evidence to counteract anxiety and feelings of isolation.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences frustration over the superficiality of social connections, leading to feelings of loneliness and self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's difficulty in forming deep friendships, driven by fear of judgment, but recognizes their increasing willingness to challenge these fears.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to initiate meaningful social interactions and record positive experiences to boost confidence and counter habitual negative thoughts.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is anxious about upcoming exams, feeling a strong sense of obligation to succeed academically to meet parental expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist identifies a cycle wherein pressure leads to anxiety and then to procrastination, yet notices the user's potential to shift to a more positive academic mindset.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower the user by reframing negative academic thoughts, incorporating relaxation techniques, and setting achievable study goals to reduce anxiety.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels both excitement and overwhelm when considering future career paths, desiring guidance in aligning personal interests with potential job options.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives the user's blend of creativity and a helping nature, underscoring the user's openness to career exploration despite apprehensions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in exploring career options that merge creativity with social service, employing tools to assess interests and developing practical steps to gain clarity and confidence in decision-making.
