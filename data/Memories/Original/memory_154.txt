# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaomei is a student who struggles with managing smartphone usage, leading to anxiety and negative impacts on her studies and social life. She is motivated to gain better control over her phone habits and improve her overall well-being.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaomei exhibits symptoms of anxiety linked to excessive smartphone use, but she has not disclosed a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels overwhelmed and frustrated by her inability to control her smartphone usage, especially during study sessions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiaomei uses her phone as a distraction from study-related stress and boredom, but she is willing to consider alternative strategies for taking breaks.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiaomei identify triggers and develop healthier break routines, using physical activities as alternatives to phone usage.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels anxious and struggles with sticking to her phone usage goals, especially during study time.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Xiaomei experiences frustration due to a disconnect between her intentions and actions, particularly when triggered by boredom and overwhelm.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Xiaomei in establishing a structured routine, using techniques like the Pomodoro method, to balance study and break times while reducing phone usage.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels overwhelmed and anxious about social interactions, leading to increased phone usage for comfort.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Xiaomei's phone usage as a coping mechanism for social anxiety, noticing her guilt for missing opportunities to connect with others.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Xiaomei to practice deep breathing and mindfulness exercises to manage social anxiety, while setting boundaries for phone use during social activities.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels anxious and somewhat hopeless during weekends due to recurring smartphone habits that disrupt her study schedule and social life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiaomei's reliance on her phone for relaxation leads to regret, affecting her academic and social activities negatively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiaomei in setting clear time limits for smartphone use and incorporating mindfulness techniques to alleviate anxiety, promoting healthier study habits.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels satisfied and optimistic, having significantly reduced her smartphone usage which improves her sleep and focus on studies and social life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiaomei's successful implementation of time management strategies, leading to a sense of control and fulfillment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Xiaomei to maintain her progress with a structured weekly plan that balances studies, fitness, and social activities while adapting coping strategies for stress management.
