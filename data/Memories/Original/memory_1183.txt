# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
User is a university student who feels self-conscious and isolated due to observing financial disparities with classmates, impacting social interactions and self-esteem. The user is dealing with feelings of inadequacy and is focused on academic performance while struggling to connect socially.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
User experiences symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly triggered by comparisons with peers, though there is no formal diagnosis provided.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels low, disconnected, and self-conscious, particularly during class gatherings, resulting in avoidance of social activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s struggle with self-worth tied to financial status, noting the user’s self-imposed isolation despite a desire for connection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Challenge the belief that self-worth is linked to financial status using cognitive behavioral techniques. Encourage the user to identify personal strengths and initiate manageable social interactions to reduce isolation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels conflicted and disappointed, with heightened emotions about financial topics among peers, leading to a sense of helplessness and isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s negative self-perception driven by financial disparity but recognizes an openness to reconsider these beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to adopt alternative thoughts about self-worth that do not rely on financial comparisons. Suggest engaging friends in low-cost social activities to foster inclusion and belonging.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels out of place and inferior due to perceived financial constraints during group activities, resulting in anxiety and isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges the user’s habitual negative self-cognitions but sees progress in recognizing the lack of evidence for these beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user focus on enjoying group experiences and exploring low-cost activities. Encourage testing assumptions about classmates and reinforce self-acceptance.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user has experienced happiness and a sense of belonging after positively interacting with classmates during a club activity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s improved comfort in social situations, recognizing supportive peer interactions as valuable.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate recreating positive, supportive environments in other social contexts. Address the user’s concerns about initiating activities and support framing them as enjoyable shared experiences.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels encouraged and less tense due to improved communication and supportive interactions with roommates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees enhanced connection and friendship between the user and roommates, with a positive shift in the user’s perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue promoting open communication and shared activities in the dormitory. Help the user overcome doubts about being rejected, using cognitive reframing techniques.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lighter and more adjusted to dorm life, with reduced stress about financial discussions, leading to greater openness and connection with roommates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user’s increased comfort and willingness to address old patterns of isolation and financial inadequacy.

[Consultation Goals]
