# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhong (pseudonym) is a university student facing feelings of loneliness and overwhelm after recently starting university. Zhong finds it challenging to adapt to the new environment, often missing the comfort of home and struggling with self-doubt regarding their academic and social abilities.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhong experiences symptoms of anxiety and loneliness, with a tendency toward negative self-comparison. There's no formal diagnosis shared, beyond a significant adjustment disorder due to the transition.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhong feels lonely and overwhelmed, frequently contemplating leaving university and returning home due to self-doubt and a sense of not belonging.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhong is struggling with self-doubt and comparison with peers but shows a willingness to explore kinder self-perceptions and take small steps towards social connection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to support Zhong in recognizing self-worth and challenge negative thoughts by fostering small social connections. Encourage tracking of positive experiences to build a sense of belonging and manage anxiety.
