# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang Lin is an individual who experiences anxiety related to perfectionism and performance, often doubting their ability to effectively manage tasks. Recently, Wang Lin has been facing stress from an upcoming project, which exacerbates their compulsive checking behaviors.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang Lin experiences symptoms of anxiety primarily characterized by repetitive checking behaviors due to fears of unpreparedness. No formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang Lin feels overwhelmed and anxious due to an impending project and repetitive checking behaviors rooted in a fear of forgetting important tasks.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Wang Lin's cycle of self-doubt and anxiety, which manifests in compulsive checking, but notes Wang Lin's willingness to explore and implement new strategies for managing anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang Lin challenge anxiety-inducing thoughts and develop confidence in task management. Encourage the implementation of practical strategies, such as setting limits on checking behaviors and using relaxation techniques.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang Lin continues to experience anxiety, especially around the fear of forgetting essential documents, leading to persistent checking behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that Wang Lin's anxiety is tied to all-or-nothing thinking and a fear of being perceived as unprepared, indicating deeply ingrained beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Wang Lin in recognizing and reframing extreme thought patterns by using evidence-based reasoning. Encourage Wang Lin to adopt grounding techniques, such as mantras, to reduce anxiety before critical tasks.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]
