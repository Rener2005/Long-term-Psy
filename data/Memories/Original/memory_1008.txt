# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student experiencing anxiety and feelings of isolation since starting college. They struggle to connect with classmates, often feeling judged and inadequate, which hinders their ability to socialize and make friends.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of social anxiety characterized by negative self-perception and fear of social judgment. No formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and down, struggling with negative thoughts about not fitting in or being judged by classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency to anticipate social failure and notes a strong internal pressure to meet perfectionistic standards for social acceptance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive-behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative self-perceptions and encourage small steps toward engaging in social interactions to build confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is anxious and disappointed after a perceived social failure in class, leading to self-doubt and withdrawal from social activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes the deep-seated belief of personal inadequacy linked to performance and self-worth, which contributes to the user's avoidance of social engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in identifying and challenging unhelpful thought patterns, encouraging growth through exposure to social situations and fostering self-compassion.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user struggles with anxiety and sometimes feels hopeless about their capability to socialize due to persistent fears of rejection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's internal conflict between the desire to connect and fear that holds them back, yet detects excitement at the prospect of making social connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower the user to take small, manageable steps in social settings, shifting focus from self-doubt to active participation, and employing CBT techniques to manage anxiety.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling better about social activities, reporting some enjoyable interactions despite earlier overwhelming feelings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's focus on active listening and participating in conversations is beginning to reduce social anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue reinforcing positive social interactions through mindful engagement, setting intentions to reduce overthinking, and embracing gradual exposure to social settings.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more comfortable and included after successfully initiating conversations and contributing to group discussions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices progress in the user's social confidence, acknowledging their fear in larger groups but also celebrating their improved comfort in smaller settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen the user's self-assurance by reframing negative self-talk, encouraging participation in larger groups, and preparing discussion points for support.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences a boost in confidence following a successful suggestion in a class event, feeling more balanced between academic and social life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's acknowledgment of their strengths and conscious efforts to overcome self-doubt, contributing to their progress in social engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT techniques to manage presentation anxiety, encouraging the user to continue leveraging their support system and employing mindfulness to maintain balance amidst academic pressures.
