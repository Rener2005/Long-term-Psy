# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is a recent college student who is struggling with feelings of loneliness and homesickness. He finds it challenging to connect with his roommates and often feels isolated, despite a desire to engage socially.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang shows signs of social anxiety but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder. He experiences recurring thoughts of inadequacy in social settings.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels lonely and anxious about making new connections in college, expressing a sense of detachment and fear of rejection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Wang's pattern of self-imposed social isolation fueled by negative assumptions about others’ perceptions, yet there is a willingness to reframe these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang challenge assumptions that lead to social withdrawal, encouraging small, manageable interactions to build confidence.
