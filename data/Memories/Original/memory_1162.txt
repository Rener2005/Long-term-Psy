# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhou is a young adult currently preparing for entrance exams. Zhou expresses feelings of inadequacy and anxiety related to academic performance and family expectations, leading to social withdrawal and self-doubt.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhou experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, characterized by cognitive distortions like all-or-nothing thinking, yet no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhou feels overwhelmed and stressed about upcoming entrance exams, coupled with a deep fear of disappointing family and falling short of personal expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhou engages in negative self-comparisons and ruminative thought patterns, yet shows willingness to challenge these thoughts and seeks alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhou reframe negative thoughts and acknowledge past successes, developing strategies for better concentration and stress management.
