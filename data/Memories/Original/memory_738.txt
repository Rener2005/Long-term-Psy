# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang is a student grappling with anxiety related to academic performance and parental expectations. They express a strong desire to manage stress effectively to enhance both academic and personal life experiences.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences anxiety linked to exams and parental pressures, although there is no formal diagnosis of any mental health disorder disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang is feeling overwhelmed by exam anxiety, which has manifested in physical symptoms like headaches and nausea during exams, leading to frustration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang’s awareness of the disparity between their thoughts and reality, indicating potential for challenging negative thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang identify and challenge exaggerated thoughts about failure and parental disappointment, and to develop effective stress management strategies.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels somewhat improved concerning anxiety but remains confused about parental expectations, which continue to influence pre-exam stress.

[Observational Impressions]  
Zhang shows an ability to recognize the gap between self-worth and academic performance, though they still find it challenging to internalize this in personal situations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang to apply self-compassion and challenge the internalization of parental expectations using CBT. Foster a more balanced view of self-worth beyond academic achievements.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels relieved about improved family communications but continues to worry about future prospects and upcoming exams, experiencing stress and moments of hopelessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
Zhang starts recognizing personal achievements, such as improvements in math, yet struggles with self-comparison and the weight of perceived expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Zhang in celebrating personal achievements and identifying strengths. Apply cognitive behavioral strategies to bolster self-confidence and diminish anxiety through practical exercises such as speaking.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang is working on social interactions but experiences anxiety and self-doubt during group settings, which hinders active participation.

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Use CBT to help Zhang acknowledge and reformulate unrealistic fears about judgment. Encourage gradual exposure and participation in group settings to enhance social confidence.
