# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaozhang is a high school student experiencing difficulties with anger, particularly in social settings like school. Xiaozhang values respect and integrity highly and has a tendency to react strongly when these values are challenged by peers. He often feels misunderstood and wishes to improve his relationships both in school and at home, where he feels particularly sensitive during arguments with family members.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaozhang is experiencing heightened stress responses, particularly expressions of anger, which are likely linked to perceived violations of his core values and feelings of being misunderstood. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaozhang feels frustrated and overwhelmed by the behavior of some classmates and the resulting conflicts, leading to regret and concern about others' perceptions of him afterward.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiaozhang's strong moral compass and the emotional distress when these morals are breached by others, noting his openness to finding alternative, non-confrontational ways to express himself.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques such as pausing before responding, and reflection on emotional triggers to help reduce impulsive reactions. Guide Xiaozhang in expressing feelings in constructive ways to improve interactions both in school and familial contexts.
