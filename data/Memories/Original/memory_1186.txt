# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user (pseudonym: F) is an adult who recently started a new job and struggles with social anxiety, particularly in unfamiliar interactions. They aim to build confidence and form meaningful connections, expressing concerns about being judged or misunderstood by new acquaintances.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
F exhibits symptoms of social anxiety, which include fear of negative evaluation and avoidance of social situations. There's no indication of a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
F feels anxious and overwhelmed by social interactions at a new workplace, fearing judgment and inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes F's inclination towards perfectionism and fear of social rejection, yet notes F's willingness to confront these fears and explore techniques for improving confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
To assist F in managing social anxiety through cognitive behavioral techniques that challenge negative beliefs, encourage gradual exposure to social situations, and promote self-compassion.
