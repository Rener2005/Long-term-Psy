# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is married and has been experiencing anxiety and insecurity about her relationship with her husband, particularly regarding his interactions with a female colleague. She is motivated to improve communication with her husband and is exploring ways to articulate her feelings constructively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li has symptoms of anxiety and insecurity in her marital relationship, but there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and overwhelmed by jealousy concerning her husband's interactions with a colleague, leading to fear and insecurity about her self-worth and relationship stability.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li's jealousy results in emotional turmoil and reactive communication with her husband. However, Li is aware of her negative thoughts and is eager to address them.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li challenge cognitive distortions related to jealousy and build healthier communication strategies, aiming to foster trust in her relationship.
