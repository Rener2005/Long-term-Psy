# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is an adult male experiencing heightened anxiety following the death of his grandmother six months ago. He is also dealing with health-related concerns, notably acute pharyngitis, which exacerbates his health anxiety. Wang is particularly worried about his physical health, fearing serious ailments due to a family history of health issues.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang struggles with health anxiety exacerbated by recent family loss and personal health issues but has no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder. He exhibits patterns of catastrophic thinking and experiences anxiety-related physical symptoms.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels sad and anxious, particularly about his health, fearing that minor symptoms could indicate severe health problems.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Wang's tendency to link his health anxiety to past familial health struggles, noting a fixation on physical symptoms as potential signs of severe illness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang challenge catastrophic thoughts and focus on factual evidence. Encourage practical steps, such as seeing a doctor for reassurance, and stress management strategies like engaging in hobbies and journaling.

# Second Consultation

Wang remains anxious and preoccupied with physical discomfort, fearing serious health conditions without evidence.

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
