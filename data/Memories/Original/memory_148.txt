# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is a university student who has been navigating challenges related to a recent hospitalization for schizophrenia. Wang expresses concern about the impact of his health condition on his academic progress and future aspirations, as well as maintaining social connections.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang has been diagnosed with schizophrenia, which contributes to feelings of anxiety about academic performance and peer relationships.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels anxious and lost about his academic situation and social standing following hospitalization.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Wang's anxiety as stemming from real experiences with hospitalization, affecting his outlook towards both academic and social contexts. Wang shows signs of deep introspection and potential self-stigma but is open to exploring supportive perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang identify and challenge misconceptions about the opinions of his peers regarding his health. Establish small, achievable academic goals to rebuild confidence and manage anxiety. Encourage open communication with his social circle for support.
