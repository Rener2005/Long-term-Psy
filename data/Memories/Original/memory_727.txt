# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Han is an undergraduate student who experiences anxiety and frustration stemming from academic pressures and feelings of guilt regarding his sexual orientation. He is adjusting to university life, which presents significant changes and challenges in self-acceptance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Han struggles with symptoms of anxiety related to his academic performance and internal conflict about his sexual orientation. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Han feels anxious and frustrated, with substantial pressure from studies and guilt about sexual orientation affecting his concentration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Han's internal conflict about self-acceptance and a longing for validation, alongside potential embarrassment related to familial expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Han in acknowledging and accepting his feelings. Promote self-compassion and establish methods to better manage academic stress and guilt.
