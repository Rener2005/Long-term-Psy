# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a boarding school student who struggles with feelings of loneliness and social anxiety. Recent adjustments to a new environment have intensified these feelings, making it challenging to connect with peers. The user is introspective and determined to overcome these social barriers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of social anxiety and loneliness but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and isolated, struggling with a sense of invisibility in social settings and self-critical thoughts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user shows significant self-awareness of social anxiety patterns and is willing to challenge negative perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative self-perceptions and encourage practical strategies for engaging in social interactions.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about class participation, fearing negative judgment and self-worth in academic and peer contexts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's cognitive distortions regarding peer evaluations but notes a logical approach when considering alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in practicing self-compassion and identifying cognitive distortions in social perceptions, encouraging gradual participation and peer engagement.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed by missed social opportunities, coupled with anxiety about initiating interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees growth in the user’s understanding of social anxiety triggers and a readiness to implement small interaction steps.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in practicing small social goals, utilizing CBT techniques to promote incremental social engagement and reduce apprehension.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more open to social interactions yet experiences anxiety about sustaining conversations with peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes improved self-awareness and growth in the user’s social confidence, along with residual anxiety about self-expression.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to utilize humor and shared activities to deepen connections, reinforcing confidence through positive experience reflection.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more connected and motivated, experiencing increased confidence in suggesting and participating in group activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives a marked improvement in the user’s social initiative and confidence, with lingering hesitations about peer acceptance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen the user’s confidence in suggesting group activities, reinforcing the normalcy of sharing ideas using CBT techniques to challenge negative biases.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels good about social engagement, demonstrating increased confidence and proactivity in social settings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes significant growth in the user’s self-confidence and leadership abilities within group settings and a positive reflection on past successes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Maintain social momentum and encourage the user to join extracurricular activities, solidifying new social skills through positive visualization and self-affirmation techniques.
