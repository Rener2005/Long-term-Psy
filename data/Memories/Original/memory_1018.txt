# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaomei is a young individual struggling with feelings of anxiety and self-doubt, particularly concerning friendships and peer interactions. Xiaomei is eager to improve self-acceptance and navigate emotional challenges better.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaomei experiences symptoms related to anxiety and low self-esteem but has not received a formal diagnosis for a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels upset and insecure due to changes in friendships, primarily with friends A and B, and experiences difficulty in concentrating academically.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Xiaomei's tendency to blame herself for perceived relationship failings but also her willingness to explore compassionate perspectives towards herself.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to challenge negative self-talk and encourage Xiaomei to engage in creative activities, like doodling, as outlets for emotional expression and self-care.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei continues to feel anxious and confused about friendships, deeply troubled by perceived personal shortcomings and guilt over recent social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiaomei's persistent self-critical thoughts but notes an emerging awareness of alternative viewpoints that are less self-punitive.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the identification of distorted thinking regarding friendships and support the use of drawing and time with family as self-care practices to alleviate anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei experiences heightened anxiety related to peer perceptions and misinterpreted social interactions, which exacerbate academic stress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiaomei's struggle with ingrained negative thoughts yet acknowledges the progress made toward recognizing their irrational nature.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral strategies to help Xiaomei reframe negative perceptions and enhance self-confidence in social settings, using affirmations before anticipated social interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels confused and isolated due to perceived exclusion from a close friend, affecting her ability to concentrate academically.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Xiaomei's vulnerability and fear of rejection yet recognizes an openness to developing communication strategies with peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiaomei in initiating open conversations with friends to address relationship concerns while reinforcing academic focus through structured study routines.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels slightly more at ease expressing herself but continues to dwell on the impact of previous interactions on friendships, especially with friend A.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiaomei's continued struggle with guilt but also notices growth in her readiness to apply self-compassionate approaches.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower Xiaomei to prepare and practice constructive dialogues with friends using role-playing to alleviate anxiety. Encourage maintaining a journal to highlight personal achievements and bolster self-esteem.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaomei feels generally better with improved emotional stability but experiences residual anxiety over friendships and self-perceived missteps.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Xiaomei's progress in emotional regulation and coping but notes ongoing uncertainty about relationship dynamics.

[Consultation Goals]
