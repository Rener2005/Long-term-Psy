# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student facing challenges with adapting to a new environment, academic pressures, and social interactions. Li has expressed feelings of anxiety and overwhelm, particularly with adjusting to university life and managing time efficiently.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li displays symptoms indicative of anxiety, particularly related to academic performance and social situations, but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and overwhelmed by the pressures of university life, including academic performance and social integration, contributing to difficulties in concentration and an overall sense of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's tendency towards extreme thinking, particularly fearing failure and the subsequent consequences, but also notes Li's potential for self-compassion when encouraged to shift perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li reframe negative thoughts and focus on learning and self-kindness. Develop strategies for integrating enjoyable activities into routines for stress reduction and promote balanced scheduling to manage academic responsibilities and self-care.

# Second Consultation
