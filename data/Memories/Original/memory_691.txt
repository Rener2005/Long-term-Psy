# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young adult who struggles with feelings of inadequacy and tension in familial relationships. Recently, a distressing public incident involving parental criticism at a subway station has left them feeling exposed and overwhelmed. They express a desire to manage emotions effectively and improve social connections.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, with thoughts of self-harm during periods of emotional distress. No formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels humiliated and powerless following a public family confrontation, leading to feelings of worthlessness and pervasive thoughts of self-harm.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user carries a heavy burden of self-critical thoughts, yet they display a willingness to explore more compassionate self-perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative self-beliefs and establish coping strategies for familial conflict. Encourage self-compassion and initiation of social connections.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels discouraged and isolated after unsuccessful social interactions, exacerbating fears of rejection and inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's tendency to internalize social setbacks as personal failures despite a lack of evidence supporting these harsh self-assessments.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in reframing social experiences using cognitive restructuring and role-play exercises. Promote realistic self-appraisal and foster gradual peer engagement.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences a mix of pride and worry following positive social engagements, concerned about sustaining this progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that focusing on present-moment enjoyment fosters improved social interactions, marking potential for more confidence in social settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage mindfulness strategies to enhance present-moment focus and reduce anxiety about peer perceptions. Support preparatory techniques for upcoming group projects.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user expresses cautious optimism about improved family communication but remains wary of potential regression to previous conflict patterns.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s willingness to target communication barriers and cultivate a more empathetic dialogue with family members.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop the user’s use of "I" statements to foster effective family communication. Encourage a consistent routine for emotional check-ins and self-reflection.

# Fifth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]

# Sixth Consultation
