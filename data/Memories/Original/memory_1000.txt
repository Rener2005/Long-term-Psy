# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Mr. Li (pseudonym) is living in a nursing home and recently diagnosed with terminal cancer, which has increased his anxiety and fear of death. He expresses feelings of helplessness and worries about being a burden to his family. Mr. Li values self-reflection and desires emotional support to handle his vulnerability and guilt.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Mr. Li experiences anxiety and feelings of guilt related to his illness and perceived burden on family, without reporting a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li feels overwhelmed with anxiety and fears regarding death, struggling with guilt and helplessness related to his illness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes Mr. Li's difficulty in applying compassion to himself, as he's more accustomed to being self-critical due to his vulnerability.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to challenge Mr. Li's negative self-perceptions and promote self-compassion. Develop strategies to manage anxiety and utilize relaxation techniques to foster emotional relief.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li continues to grapple with anxiety over his illness and death, feeling lonely and fearful about the future without him.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Mr. Li's tendency to isolate himself emotionally, but acknowledges his openness to finding ways to share concerns with family.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Mr. Li to communicate his fears with loved ones and reframe his thoughts about death, using support and positive memories to combat anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Mr. Li's exploration of creative expression as a means to process emotions, while reinforcing connections made in group settings to combat loneliness.
