# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Martha is a mother who enjoys spending time with her daughter, Ruth. Recently, she has been planning activities with Ruth, including Christmas shopping and decorating the house.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Martha struggles with remembering everyday details and experiences occasional feelings of confusion, but has not reported a formal diagnosis of a mental disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Martha feels generally okay but is concerned about her memory, particularly with daily activities and plans.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Martha's positivity when discussing activities with her daughter, though there's an underlying frustration with memory lapses.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Martha develop memory strategies, such as using a notebook for planning and reinforcement of positive experiences.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Martha feels happy after attending a Christmas performance with her daughter, but worries about forgetting positive experiences and moments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Martha feels supported by her daughter and values shared experiences, which aids in managing her memory concerns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Martha to start a journal to record positive family interactions and personal reflections to strengthen memory and reinforce joyful experiences.
