# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Jimmy is a middle-aged man experiencing homelessness and struggling with alcohol dependence. He feels trapped in a cycle of living on the streets and is seeking hope and practical steps towards improving his situation, including stable housing and sobriety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Jimmy exhibits symptoms of depression, alcohol dependence, and stress related to instability in his life but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Jimmy feels frustrated and overwhelmed, experiencing moments of hope quickly dashed by setbacks, leading to feelings of anger and hopelessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Jimmy's candor about his struggles with alcohol and housing, noting his willingness to explore small steps toward stability and accountability.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on cognitive behavioral strategies to help Jimmy challenge negative beliefs about his situation and explore small, actionable steps for stability, such as writing plans and seeking resources.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Jimmy continues to struggle with guilt and doubt about his ability to quit drinking, feeling isolated and stressed.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Jimmy's capacity for supporting others but highlights his difficulty in applying self-compassion. He is open to exploring alternative perspectives and strategies for coping with cravings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Jimmy to recognize his sober achievements and support the development of alternative coping strategies for handling stress and cravings, fostering community support.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote Jimmy's use of creative expression and community activities to mitigate loneliness and stress, reinforcing his support network and positive coping strategies.
