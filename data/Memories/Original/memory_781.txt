# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a nursing student experiencing heightened anxiety, particularly with upcoming qualification exams. Zhang describes compulsive cleaning behaviors as a response to stress, which disrupt focus and productivity, especially related to study routines and interactions with colleagues.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms associated with anxiety and obsessive-compulsive behaviors, without a confirmed formal diagnosis. The compulsive behaviors are intensified by academic stress and fears of inadequacy.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious and overwhelmed about the nursing qualification exam, perceiving cleaning as necessary to feel prepared for study, despite recognizing it as a distraction.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang's compulsive cleaning as a coping mechanism for exam-related anxiety, coupled with underlying beliefs about the consequences of not adhering to high cleanliness standards.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang challenge the belief that cleanliness is directly linked to success. Establish structured study routines with designated cleaning times to balance habits and reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang continues to feel isolated and overwhelmed due to exam pressure and compulsive cleaning behaviors, exacerbated by a recent argument with a colleague.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Zhang's ongoing struggle with compulsion stemming from deep-seated beliefs about control, impacting social connections negatively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang in recognizing the impact of compulsive behaviors on interpersonal relationships. Encourage the development of mindfulness exercises to manage urges and foster a healthier balance between cleanliness and study.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang reports intensified stress and compulsive cleaning related to exam preparation but is open to exploring alternative coping methods.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the link between Zhang's family history of strict cleanliness and current compulsions, indicating a need for addressing underlying fears of chaos.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Zhang towards recognizing and challenging cognitive distortions about chaos and failure. Introduce relaxation techniques to develop alternative coping strategies in stressful situations.
