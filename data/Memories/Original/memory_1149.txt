# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang is an adult who experiences significant feelings of loneliness, inadequacy, and anxiety in social relationships, compounded by complex familial dynamics. Recent conflicts with friends and reflections on parental relationships have intensified these emotions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang struggles with anxiety and feelings of inadequacy, particularly in social and familial contexts, although there is no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels isolated and overwhelmed due to a recent argument with a friend and ongoing reflections about parental relationships, leading to a heightened sense of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's patterns of negative thoughts, particularly self-doubt and a tendency to withdraw both socially and emotionally, yet recognizes Zhang's insight and potential for personal growth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to help Zhang identify and challenge negative thought patterns, fostering improved self-perception and relationship dynamics. Encourage small actionable steps towards vulnerability and connection.
