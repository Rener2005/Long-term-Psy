# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student experiencing significant stress related to academic performance, which has affected their mental health, appetite, and study focus. They have a perfectionistic approach and worry about failing and disappointing their parents.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user shows symptoms indicative of anxiety and stress-related eating habits. There is no mention of a formal diagnosis at this time.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and overwhelmed by the upcoming exams, impacting their food choices and leading to avoidance of studying.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's anxiety stems from a fear of failure and a perceived need for perfection, coupled with physical stress responses.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to challenge perfectionistic thoughts and alleviate academic pressure. Develop a balanced study routine and healthy eating habits to reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is struggling with self-esteem issues, feeling unattractive after a recent social event, which led to unhealthy behavior like self-induced vomiting.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user tying self-worth to physical appearance and social comparisons, indicating a distorted self-image needing re-evaluation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user challenge beliefs linking self-worth to appearance, focusing on non-appearance-related qualities. Encourage journaling and engagement in positive, stress-relieving activities.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
