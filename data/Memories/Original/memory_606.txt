# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is an investor who has been feeling increasingly anxious about their financial decisions, particularly regarding recent stock market losses. They are questioning their decision-making skills and struggling to enjoy activities they once valued, such as trading.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences anxiety related to financial decisions but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and pressured, doubting their investment skills due to recent losses.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's anxiety stemming from financial loss and sees a fear of further loss and uncertainty impacting their decision-making.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts about their investing skills and confidence. Introduce mindfulness and breathing techniques to manage anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and isolated, facing self-doubt and criticism from peers about their investing capabilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's anxiety is exacerbated by group criticism, yet they show a willingness to explore their past successes and develop a more secure investment approach.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in identifying and restructuring skewed thought patterns, bolster confidence through evidence-based approaches, and develop strategies for secure investment decision-making.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels guilty and regretful after recent investment losses, fearing similar future mistakes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes regret linked to impulsive investment decisions based on unreliable sources, yet notes the user's openness to reframe their mindset towards learning.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to view setbacks as learning opportunities and establish a structured, evidence-based approach to investments to regain confidence.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels empowered, recognizing the need to verify investment information and avoid scams after attending a seminar.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes increased awareness and a proactive mindset in the user, alongside an inclination to involve family for support in decisions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Leverage new knowledge to foster a collaborative family environment for investment decisions, encouraging communication and shared learning experiences.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels positive and motivated, supported by their spouse's involvement in investment discussions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes boosted confidence and open communication with the user's spouse, which reinforces their decision-making process and reduces feelings of isolation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to maintain partner collaboration, create a systematic checklist for investments, and nurture ongoing open dialogue to reinforce confidence in decisions.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels confident and has noted significant improvements in their investment skills and decision-making processes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's increased confidence and more structured investment approach, fueled by ongoing learning and spousal support.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in maintaining their learning routine and set short-term investment goals for sustained motivation. Develop a list of trusted resources for verifying investment information.
