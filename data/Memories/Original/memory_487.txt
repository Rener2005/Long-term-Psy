# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a student experiencing tension and anxiety due to a conflict with classmates and the pressure of a new school term. Zhang Wei expresses feelings of exclusion and a desire to regain respect and belonging within the peer group.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei exhibits symptoms of social anxiety and stress but has not been formally diagnosed with any specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious and isolated due to conflicts with classmates and is overwhelmed by academic pressures.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's tendency to interpret classmates' behavior negatively, potentially exacerbating feelings of rejection and loneliness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei reframe negative interpretations of classmates' behavior and encourage proactive social engagement to rebuild peer relationships.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels slightly better but continues to experience anxiety from perceived rejection by classmates, affecting social confidence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes persistent but unfounded beliefs of being disliked are contributing to Zhang Wei's anxiety in social interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang Wei in identifying and reevaluating unsupported negative beliefs about classmates' perceptions. Encourage initiating light interactions to rebuild social connections.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels nervous about participation in club activities and making new friends but acknowledges relaxation in some interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of overthinking and negative self-assessment hindering Zhang Wei's social engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Zhang Wei in recognizing positive social feedback and reducing overthinking through rehearsal of positive interactions using CBT strategies.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei enjoys social interactions but is stressed about schoolwork, struggling to balance academics and social life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei's effective social progress but observes a pressing need for time management skills to balance responsibilities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop a structured schedule incorporating study and social time. Introduce relaxation techniques post-study to alleviate academic stress.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels confident from recent praise and seeks to deepen peer connections, though concerns about social engagement linger.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes Zhang Wei's strengthened self-esteem but identifies hesitation in initiating deeper interactions due to fear of awkwardness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang Wei to utilize successful past interactions to build deeper connections. Provide strategies for initiating conversations and managing potential awkwardness with confidence.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels positive and less anxious, having improved social relationships, but is cautious about potential setbacks.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's increased self-assurance and social engagement but notes underlying concerns about maintaining progress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce strategies for social and academic success, emphasizing preparation, relaxation techniques, and proactive engagement to sustain confidence and connections.
