# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with anxiety related to public speaking. Zhang has recently entered university and finds it challenging to gather confidence in speaking before classmates and teachers, fearing judgment and criticism.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms of anxiety specifically triggered by public speaking situations but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels extremely anxious and experiences physical symptoms like tension and sweaty palms during public speaking engagements, fearing embarrassment and judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang's anxiety is linked to negative predictions and judgments about public speaking, but Zhang is open to exploring more positive self-beliefs and strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to work with Zhang on recognizing and challenging negative self-talk and increasing confidence through relaxation and exposure to manageable public speaking situations.
