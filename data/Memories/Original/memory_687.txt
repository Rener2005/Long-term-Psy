# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young adult who has recently experienced a lack of direction and motivation after dropping out of school. The user's mother is often not home, leading to excessive time spent online without any real goals, resulting in anxiety about the future.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and a lack of motivation, though no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and directionless, with excessive time spent online leaving them feeling unfulfilled and anxious. 

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user feels overwhelmed by a lack of structure and is seeking ways to reconnect with past interests to foster motivation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify small steps towards re-engaging with activities like basketball and socializing. Work on creating a structured routine to reduce time spent online and consider exploring employment opportunities to improve motivation and self-worth.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels disheartened and questions their ability to succeed after struggling with the motivation for part-time work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a pattern of negative self-talk and anxiety around failure, yet the user shows awareness of these unhelpful thought patterns and is willing to challenge them.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to set smaller, attainable goals for part-time work to gradually build confidence. Use CBT techniques to reframe negative thoughts, fostering self-compassion and realistic self-assessment. Promote open communication within the family to seek emotional support.
