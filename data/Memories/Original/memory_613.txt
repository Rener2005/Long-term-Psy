# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Mr. Zhou is a middle-aged man who has recently experienced the traumatic loss of his son, which has deeply affected him and his family. He is currently struggling with overwhelming grief, feelings of injustice due to external circumstances, and difficulties in family communication.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Mr. Zhou is exhibiting signs of acute grief, including persistent sadness, anger, and familial communication issues, as well as concerns about financial stability and self-worth. There is no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder at this time.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Zhou is overwhelmed and consumed by grief, experiencing anger towards external situations and difficulty coping with family dynamics.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant perceives Mr. Zhou as deeply engrossed in his grief, which affects his interactions with family members, prompting emotional withdrawal from his wife and daughters.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Mr. Zhou to initiate open communication with his wife using cognitive behavioral techniques to share grief and extract mutual support. Suggest concrete actions to honor his son's memory, which might alleviate feelings of helplessness and injustice.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Zhou remains engulfed in sadness and frustration, resulting in tense family atmospheres and feelings of guilt over familial interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a communication barrier between Mr. Zhou and his family, amplifying misunderstandings and emotional isolation, yet observes openness to explore gentle avenues for family discussion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Mr. Zhou's journey towards opening dialogue within the family by employing CBT to address guilt and the weight of expectations. Introduce strategies for discussing emotions to promote familial empathy and shared grieving.
