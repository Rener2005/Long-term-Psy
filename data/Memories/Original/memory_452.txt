# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is an individual navigating challenges related to alcohol use, primarily as a means to unwind from work-related stress. Recently, a police incident has heightened familial concerns about Li's drinking habits.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li exhibits patterns of alcohol use as a coping mechanism for stress, yet no formal diagnosis of alcohol dependency is disclosed in the sessions.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels ambivalent about attending the session and denies the severity of the drinking issue, relying on alcohol to manage stress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li grappling with defensive attitudes about drinking and familial pressure, though open to exploring lifestyle changes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Explore cognitive behavioral techniques to identify alternative stress management strategies, encouraging Li to reflect on the effects of drinking and begin small lifestyle adaptations, such as incorporating physical activity.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels tense and overwhelmed by work, leading to reliance on alcohol to cope with the stress, resulting in guilt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's increasing awareness of the temporary relief provided by alcohol and a growing consideration for healthier coping mechanisms.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT to help Li identify new rewarding activities post-work, encourage reflections on the impact of drinking on relationships, and initiate small habit changes.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li to pursue manageable steps for incorporating exercise and mindfulness into daily routines, promoting a shift from isolation toward workplace engagement.
