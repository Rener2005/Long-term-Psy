# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li Ming (pseudonym) is an employee in a workplace setting experiencing feelings of overwhelm and disconnection from colleagues. Li Ming reports feeling pressured by increasing workloads and worries about colleagues' judgments affecting self-confidence and social interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li Ming displays symptoms consistent with anxiety related to work performance and social interactions, but no formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming feels overwhelmed and disconnected at work, burdened by thoughts of being judged as incompetent by colleagues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li Ming's tendency toward self-critical thoughts and withdrawal, though Li Ming demonstrates a willingness to adopt new perspectives on work interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li Ming challenge self-critical thoughts by shifting focus to personal achievements and positive work experiences. Encourage small, proactive steps to improve workplace connections.
