# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, referred to as G, is navigating the pressures of academic life, particularly the anxiety and self-doubt associated with exams and performance expectations from family. G is introspective and open to exploring emotional and cognitive strategies to manage these feelings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
G demonstrates symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly concerning academic performance. There is no formal diagnosis disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G feels anxious and overwhelmed by approaching exams and is caught in self-critical thoughts that affect mood and confidence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes G's tendency toward negative self-evaluation and acknowledges the openness to introduce alternative, more affirmative self-perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist G in challenging and rebalancing negative thoughts, reinforce self-confidence, and develop strategies for exam-related stress management.
