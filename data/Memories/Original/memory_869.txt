# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is an individual who has been experiencing feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, primarily triggered by academic performance and social interactions. Zhang tends to compare themselves unfavorably to others and struggles with feelings of isolation and self-doubt.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang shows signs of anxiety and low self-esteem, with prevalent perfectionistic tendencies. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been reported.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels significantly overwhelmed by their performance in class and social situations, resulting in increased anxiety and feelings of failure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang's perfectionistic beliefs are deeply rooted, contributing substantially to their feelings of inadequacy and anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to help Zhang recognize and reframe perfectionistic thoughts, gradually fostering more realistic and compassionate self-evaluations. Encourage exposure to anxiety-inducing situations to build resilience and decrease avoidance behaviors.
