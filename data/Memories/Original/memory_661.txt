# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is an individual experiencing significant anxiety, particularly related to public speaking situations, such as during English speaking classes. They report frustration and exhaustion due to difficulties in expressing themselves and fear of judgment from others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user shows symptoms of anxiety predominantly focused on social performance situations, although no formal mental health disorder diagnosis is disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels highly anxious and self-conscious during public speaking, leading to negative thoughts about being judged and feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user experiences intense anxiety related to perceptions of judgment but demonstrates the willingness to consider alternative, less critical perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy techniques to help the user identify and challenge their negative thought patterns, promoting self-compassion and confidence in public speaking scenarios. Encourage gradual exposure to speaking tasks to build comfort and reduce anxiety.
