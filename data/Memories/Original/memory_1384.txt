# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
B (pseudonym) is a junior high school student who struggles with academic and social pressures. B has recently developed an interest in a "gangster" lifestyle but recognizes the need to find meaningful engagement in school.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
B is experiencing challenges related to academic motivation and peer relationships but has not been formally diagnosed with a specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
B feels disconnected and frustrated with school, perceiving it as boring and irrelevant. There’s a strong interest in the "gangster" lifestyle, which contrasts with school demands.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes B's interest in seeking excitement and control through alternative lifestyles, underscoring the struggle with academic disengagement and peer pressures.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help B find engaging activities within school, such as integrating creative interests into studies. Encourage setting small, achievable goals to balance interests and school expectations.
