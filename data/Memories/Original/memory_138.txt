# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Yin (pseudonym) is a student who recently moved to a new school and feels lonely and overlooked. She misses her mother's attention amid her mother's focus on Xiao Yin's younger brother, and she struggles to make new friends.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Yin experiences feelings of loneliness and social anxiety, but there is no indication of a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder. Her feelings predominantly stem from recent life changes and social challenges.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Yin feels lonely and overlooked by her family, particularly her mother, and struggles with making friends at her new school.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Yin's feelings of isolation and lack of maternal attention; she is expressing a need for connection at home and among peers in a new environment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Yin articulate her feelings to her mother and develop strategies for initiating friendships, such as practicing positive communication and starting conversations with classmates.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Yin feels somewhat better but continues to experience loneliness at school, though she enjoys playing games during recess.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Yin shows improvement in recognizing social opportunities but remains hesitant to engage fully due to self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Yin in actively joining social activities by practicing simple conversation starters and addressing her social anxiety through role-playing and CBT techniques to build her confidence.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Yin feels better emotionally and has engaged in group activities, yet experiences occasional loneliness when not included in all interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Yin's progress in making friends and recognizes her growing awareness of internal negative thoughts about social acceptance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Xiao Yin to challenge thoughts of exclusion with evidence of inclusion by recalling positive interactions. Use breathing techniques and positive affirmations to reduce anxiety in social settings.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Yin reports feeling happy about making new friends but worries intermittently about acceptance and self-worth within her peer group.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Yin's improved social connections and confidence; however, she still battles doubts about being liked and accepted.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce the importance of reflecting on positive social experiences. Encourage Xiao Yin to suggest new activities to her friends as a way to strengthen bonds, and discuss strategies for trusting and communicating with peers effectively.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Yin feels accomplished and motivated following recognition in a class activity, which has significantly boosted her confidence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Yin’s growing eagerness to participate in social and academic settings, reflecting a significant enhancement in her self-esteem and openness to new experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Yin in pursuing her interest in joining the school’s art club by setting realistic steps toward goal achievement. Encourage her use of journaling to reflect on personal achievements and manage any lingering self-doubt with positive self-talk.
