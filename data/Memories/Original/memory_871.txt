# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student who struggles with speech-related anxiety, particularly stuttering. He recently moved into a dormitory where he shares a room with another student who stutters, exacerbating his own speech issues. He feels anxious about speaking in class and social interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences anxiety related to stuttering but has not mentioned any formal diagnosis of an anxiety disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and embarrassed about his speech, fearing judgment from classmates and avoiding speaking to prevent potential embarrassment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li's anxiety about stuttering is heightened by self-critical thoughts and fears of judgment, though he shows a willingness to explore more positive self-perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in developing relaxation techniques for managing anxiety before and during speaking. Encourage cognitive restructuring to challenge negative beliefs about stuttering and promote self-acceptance.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li continues to experience anxiety about stuttering in class and fears being judged as unintelligent by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's persistent focus on negative outcomes, despite recognizing some lack of evidence for his fears. Li is open to adopting alternative, constructive thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li replace fear-based thoughts with supportive, realistic affirmations. Practice role-playing scenarios to build confidence in speaking situations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li remains anxious about social interactions, feeling preoccupied with potential stuttering and how it’s perceived by friends.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li benefits from focusing on supportive peer reactions rather than imagined judgments, signaling an openness to practice socializing with less self-criticism.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li to view social interactions as opportunities to practice speaking without fear of error. Foster supportive self-talk and engage in low-pressure conversational settings to build confidence.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels both excited and fearful about an upcoming speech competition, worried about potential stuttering in a public setting.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a duality in Li's feelings, combining fear of public judgment with an existing foundation of self-compassion, motivating a positive shift in perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize visualization and self-regulation techniques, including breathing exercises, to manage speech anxiety. Reinforce the practice of positive affirmations to build self-assurance during public speaking.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li reports a boost in confidence after completing a speech competition, receiving positive feedback from peers and teachers which reinforces his self-worth beyond his speech patterns.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's significant progress in distinguishing his abilities from his stutter, resulting in increased participation and reduced fear in social and academic settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Maintain progress through consistent use of breathing and visualization techniques. Set goals for regular practice of public speaking to continue building self-confidence and reduce speech anxiety.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is feeling generally good and more comfortable with public speaking after joining the speech team, though he feels some anxiety about an upcoming formal presentation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's improved self-assurance and enjoyment in speaking, underpinned by peer support and effective anxiety management techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage continued practice and goal setting in preparation for formal presentations. Leverage Li’s support network to bolster confidence, utilizing visualization and structured practice to prepare effectively.
