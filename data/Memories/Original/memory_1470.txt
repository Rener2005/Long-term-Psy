# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Jun is a high school student experiencing intense emotions, particularly anger and confusion, which have resulted in incidents both at home and school. He struggles to understand these emotions and seeks guidance to manage them better.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Jun shows signs of emotional dysregulation, marked by frequent anger outbursts but does not have a formal diagnosis of any mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Jun feels confused and guilty about his anger incidents, especially after losing control during a football game and interactions with his parents.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Jun's awareness of his emotional triggers, particularly feelings of being overlooked or unfair treatment, and his willingness to explore alternative responses.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to identify triggers and develop coping strategies such as breathing exercises and positive self-talk to manage anger effectively.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Jun feels persistent confusion and heightened anxiety when facing his father's outbursts, impacting his emotional stability.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Jun’s difficulty in pausing before reacting, yet acknowledges his progress in increasing self-awareness regarding emotional triggers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage practice of CBT techniques, such as pausing and recognizing alternative interpretations of events, to manage emotional responses non-reactively.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Jun feels frustrated with managing his anger under his father's strictness and during pressures about schoolwork.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Jun’s negative perceptions arise from feeling misunderstood and unsupported, but notes his effort in applying breathing techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Xiao Jun’s ability to utilize breathing exercises and communication as tools to express feelings calmly and improve familial interactions using CBT.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Jun feels progress in classroom activities but still struggles with fast anger during competitions, leading to self-comparison and frustration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Jun’s inclination towards self-comparison, yet also his interest in adopting a more personal-progress-focused mindset.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Xiao Jun employ cognitive reframing techniques to diminish self-comparisons and focus on personal performance during competitions, using mental preparedness strategies.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Jun feels more positive about collaborative work with classmates, which boosts his confidence, yet remains slightly intimidated when addressing emotions with family.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes improved social interactions and openness to practicing new communication strategies with parents.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Jun in transferring positive communication skills from classroom to home, practicing the expression of emotions calmly and timing conversations effectively.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Jun feels a sense of improvement and validation from practicing anxiety-management techniques, reflected in his parents' supportive responses.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Jun’s growing confidence in handling anxiety and his receptiveness to further practice and consolidation of strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage continuous use of CBT techniques to sustain emotional management improvements, with emphasis on preparation and self-reward tactics to enhance public speaking confidence.
