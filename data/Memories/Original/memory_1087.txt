# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Fangfang (pseudonym) is a student at a teacher training school experiencing significant anxiety related to frequent restroom use, which impacts her focus and studies. Recently, she has been adjusting to the new school environment and is looking to better manage her anxiety and enhance her school experience.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Fangfang reports symptoms consistent with anxiety, primarily manifesting through an anticipatory fear of needing to use the bathroom. She has not been formally diagnosed with a specific anxiety disorder yet.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Fangfang feels overwhelmed by anxiety regarding her need to frequently use the restroom, which disrupts her ability to focus on her studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Fangfang's anxiety is driven by a fear of losing control, particularly in public settings like classrooms, but notes her willingness to explore strategies for managing this anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive-behavioral techniques to help Fangfang identify and challenge negative thought patterns. Develop a strategy integrating enjoyable activities into her study routine to mitigate anxiety.
