# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaofei (pseudonym) is a student dealing with significant academic pressure and compulsive thoughts. Recently, their focus has been disrupted by doubts regarding their completed tasks, causing anxiety and frustration.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaofei experiences symptoms of anxiety and compulsions related to academic performance, though no formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaofei feels overwhelmed by academic pressure and trapped in cycles of self-doubt and anxiety, particularly when unprepared.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiaofei's negative self-perceptions and anxiety arise from an intense need for assurance, yet there's a notable willingness to embrace change.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiaofei set manageable study goals, practice self-compassion, and cut back excessive checking behaviors. Encourage the use of structured break times to reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaofei feels anxious and overwhelmed by an unorganized study plan and an escalating cycle of compulsive checking.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiaofei's anxiety is intensified by chaotic planning and the urge to repeatedly check work, yet Xiaofei shows a strong interest in adopting more effective time management strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop a simplified study plan focusing on manageable goals and scheduled breaks, utilizing methods like the Pomodoro Technique. Encourage reflection to alleviate compulsive checking and build confidence.

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement role-playing and mindfulness to prepare Xiaofei for future social situations, reinforcing the practice of positive self-affirmations to boost confidence.
