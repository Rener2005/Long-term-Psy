# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing difficulties with social connections, leading to feelings of loneliness and a sense of not belonging. Liu is eager to develop meaningful relationships and improve self-esteem in social settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu has symptoms consistent with social anxiety and low self-esteem but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu feels lonely and disconnected, perceiving interactions with dorm mates as strained and forced, which reinforces feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Liu’s tendency to interpret others' behaviors negatively, revealing a cycle of self-consciousness and anxiety that affects Liu’s social interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Begin utilizing cognitive behavioral techniques to help Liu challenge negative perceptions and recognize that self-critical thoughts may not reflect reality. Encourage Liu to plan a social activity to practice engaging with dorm mates in a relaxed setting.
