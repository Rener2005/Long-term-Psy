# First Consultation

## User Background

C (pseudonym) is a university student who experiences anxiety and fear when it comes to relationships, particularly around intimacy and judgment from others. This anxiety is influenced by past relationship experiences and manifests as self-doubt and social caution.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
C struggles with anxiety and fear of intimacy, reinforced by self-doubt rooted in past relational experiences. No formal mental health disorder diagnosis is disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
C feels anxious and confused about starting new romantic relationships, with a recurring sense of guilt and fear over past experiences.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes heightened anxiety around social judgments and self-worth but recognizes C's openness to exploring these thoughts and feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help C identify and challenge negative thoughts, focusing on creating a more balanced view of self-worth and reducing anxiety about social judgment.
