# First Consultation

## User Background

[Personal Profile]  
The user is an individual experiencing significant anxiety related to electricity after a work-related incident. They express difficulties in focusing and sleeping due to persistent fears of electrocution and have a tendency to avoid electrical equipment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user demonstrates symptoms consistent with anxiety, particularly situational anxiety related to electricity, but there's no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder disclosed.

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
The user is extremely anxious and preoccupied with the potential danger of electricity, resulting in avoidance behavior and sleep disturbances.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's intense fear of electricity manifests through hypervigilance and avoidance, but also recognizes the user's openness to exploring educational approaches to alleviate fear.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive-behavioral therapy techniques to educate the user about electrical safety features and develop coping strategies for managing anxiety. Focus on practical steps and gradually expose the user to safe interactions with electricity.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel anxious about returning to work, with a vivid imagination of worst-case scenarios surrounding electricity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's willingness to employ observational learning and practical exposure to reduce fear, despite ongoing anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to observe safe handling of electrical equipment to lower anxiety, and apply self-soothing techniques, such as deep breathing, to manage panic attacks.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
The user experiences persistent anxious thoughts about potential mistakes in handling electricity, triggering physical symptoms of panic.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's acknowledgment of their anxiety and small steps taken towards facing their fears, bolstered by self-reflection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate gradual exposure to electrical tasks starting with non-threatening ones, while encouraging the user to monitor and challenge anxious thoughts throughout the process.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
The user is gaining confidence but still experiences significant anxiety when thinking about physically handling electrical devices.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes the user's growing willingness to engage with electrical tasks, acknowledging steps taken toward exposure despite recurring panic.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement gradual physical interactions with electrical equipment to build confidence and utilize relaxation techniques to manage anticipatory anxiety.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
The user reports feeling better overall, with lessening anxiety during work, yet still faces moments of intense fear in specific situations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices increased self-awareness in the user’s recognition of anxiety triggers and their effectiveness in handling recent electrical tasks.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen strategies for documenting achievements to counteract anxiety, and practice relaxation techniques to further enable confidence and skill development in complex tasks.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
The user expresses substantial improvement in managing anxiety related to electricity, with the ability to work without significant panic.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s self-efficacy and optimism regarding continued progress, indicating successful adaptation and application of CBT principles.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on reinforcing successful strategies and planning for potential stressors by journaling experiences, thus maintaining and enhancing resilience against anxiety.
