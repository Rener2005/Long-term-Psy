# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaohua is a high school student preparing for college entrance exams. She experiences anxiety and self-doubt related to academic performance and parental expectations. Recently, Xiaohua has struggled with feelings of being overwhelmed and has difficulty maintaining healthy sleep patterns due to exam stress.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaohua exhibits symptoms of anxiety, particularly related to academia, but has not received a formal diagnosis of an anxiety disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohua feels anxious and overwhelmed by exam preparations, leading to poor sleep and a sense of drowning in study materials.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiaohua experiences a cycle of pressure and avoidance regarding her studies but exhibits openness to exploring new coping mechanisms.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiaohua challenge negative thoughts about academic failure and to develop relaxation strategies, such as deep breathing, to manage study-related anxiety. Aim to improve parent-child communication dynamics.
