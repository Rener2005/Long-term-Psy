# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li Ming (pseudonym) is an employee in a corporate environment, feeling overshadowed and isolated at work. Recent experiences have led to diminished interactions with colleagues and feelings of invisibility. He is seeking to improve his social connections and communication skills within the workplace.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li Ming exhibits symptoms of low self-esteem and social anxiety. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder reported during consultations.

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
Li Ming feels frustrated and isolated at work, perceiving himself as neglected and invisible to his colleagues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li Ming is challenged by negative self-perceptions and communication barriers, but shows openness to exploring empathy-driven approaches to improve workplace dynamics.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Li Ming in challenging negative beliefs about his contributions and encourage proactive engagement in communication with colleagues. Focus on fostering empathetic interactions to rebuild social connections.
