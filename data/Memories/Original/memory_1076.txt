# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Ruth (pseudonym) is a 39-year-old woman who has recently graduated and feels overwhelmed due to significant life changes. She struggles to balance the transition from student to homemaker, feels pressure to establish a meaningful identity, and fears becoming invisible or unimportant as her children become more independent.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Ruth experiences anxiety and feelings of inadequacy, particularly around her role after graduation and her perceived value within the family. There has been no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ruth feels anxious and overwhelmed by life changes, struggling with the transition from student to homemaker while fearing a lack of meaningful identity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Ruth's challenge with self-worth and societal expectations but observes her willingness to explore reshaping her self-perception and identity.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Ruth shift her perspective on self-worth, acknowledge her contributions as a homemaker, and incorporate activities that foster personal fulfillment and identity.
