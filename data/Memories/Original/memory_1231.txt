# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student experiencing confusion and anxiety, particularly with thoughts of being harmed by classmates. They feel overwhelmed due to academic pressures and strict expectations from their father.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and possible paranoid thoughts but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels confused and anxious, struggling with thoughts of potential harm from classmates and the pressure from academic demands.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user is dealing with heightened stress and some suspicious perceptions, which may stem from the intense academic and familial pressures.

[Consultation Goals]  
To introduce cognitive behavioral techniques for distinguishing between stress-induced perceptions and reality, and to encourage healthier coping strategies for academic and social anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is highly stressed about upcoming exams and feels conflicted about their roommate's suggestion to cheat, adding to their anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user is experiencing significant anxiety related to meeting parental expectations and maintaining academic integrity among peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user identify and challenge all-or-nothing thinking patterns, and encourage strategies for managing exam stress and ethical dilemmas using CBT techniques.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated with their father's lack of understanding, despite making progress in their studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that although the user has improved academically, familial communication remains a source of frustration and perceived judgment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Equip the user with communication techniques to effectively express emotions and efforts to their father, using assertiveness and role-playing strategies rooted in CBT.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user continues to experience pressure from their father's expectations, though there's a willingness to try new forms of communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in expressing personal achievements beyond academic grades and implementing stress management strategies, including structured study routines and intentional breaks.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to define self-worth beyond academic performance and prepare conversations with their father to communicate feelings and expectations.
