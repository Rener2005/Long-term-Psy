# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Bernadette (pseudonym) is a business owner who often feels anxious about maintaining control within her flower shop partnership with Daisy. She struggles with managing her emotions, especially related to decision-making autonomy and interpersonal communication. Bernadette seeks to develop a more balanced leadership approach.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Bernadette exhibits symptoms of anxiety primarily related to control issues and interpersonal dynamics, but there's no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Bernadette feels frustrated and anxious as her partner, Daisy, made decisions at work without her input, causing her to worry about her perceived authority and control.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that Bernadette values control and organization, which may undermine her relationship with Daisy. However, Bernadette shows a willingness to communicate and explore collaborative solutions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Bernadette express her needs using "I" statements and encourage shared decision-making, thereby reducing anxiety and improving partnership dynamics.
