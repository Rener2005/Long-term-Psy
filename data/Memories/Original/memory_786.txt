# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is preparing for graduate school and feels anxious about an upcoming physical examination, particularly concerned about how their heart rate might be perceived. They hold a strong desire not to appear unfit or unable to handle stress in front of others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences anxiety primarily linked to performance situations, but there is no mention of any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is highly anxious about the upcoming examination, worried about the implications of their heart rate and the potential judgments from others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency toward extreme thinking and self-imposed pressure to perform flawlessly, demonstrating a need for challenging these thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ CBT strategies to identify and challenge cognitive distortions, like all-or-nothing thinking, while promoting techniques such as deep breathing and visualization to manage anxiety during the examination.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains anxious about the examination, expressing a fear of past heart issues recurring and impacting their performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user's underlying fear and uncertainty about the examination, though the user has begun acknowledging their ability to manage symptoms with assistance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Further promote the practice of deep breathing and the development of coping strategies, such as mantras and visualization, to alleviate the stress surrounding the health assessment.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels marginally better, aided by social support, but remains fixated on past health concerns causing recurring anxiety about the upcoming check-up.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's struggle with intrusive negative thoughts regarding their health but notes progress in questioning these unfounded fears.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to focus on factual evidence regarding their health and continue practicing anxiety-reduction techniques, reinforcing the importance of cognitive reframing and support networks.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more at ease after self-educating on their physical health, yet anticipates potential anxiety during the upcoming examination.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees improvements in the user's knowledge reducing fear, but a residual anxiety about the exam experience persists.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop strategies such as simulating the examination environment, continued positive affirmations, and deep breathing exercises to reinforce preparedness and control.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels encouraged after a successful mock examination experience, although there is lingering nervousness regarding the official test.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives an increase in user confidence, aided by effective self-calming techniques and a positive outlook towards the upcoming exam.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen the application of practiced breathing techniques and visualization strategies, emphasizing the replacement of automatic negative thoughts with positive affirmations.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is emotionally stable, looking forward to their graduate studies, having gained confidence from successfully navigating previous health assessments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's developed resilience and reduced anxiety, attributing this to effective cognitive and behavioral strategies learned during past sessions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage maintenance of these cognitive reframing techniques and self-compassion, preparing for potential future stressors in their graduate studies and beyond.
