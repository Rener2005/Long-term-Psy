# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who is struggling with transitioning to university life, experiencing significant anxiety and frustration related to academic adjustments and reliance on their phone as a coping mechanism.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and relies heavily on digital devices for comfort and avoidance but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious about the transition to university life, using their phone as a primary coping strategy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with facing new social and academic challenges, relying on their phone for temporary relief while experiencing guilt about avoidance behaviors.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user build social confidence and manage phone usage, encouraging engagement in face-to-face interactions to foster new connections.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and guilty about excessive smartphone usage, which interferes with managing their academic workload.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's recognition of the cycle of procrastination driven by phone usage as temporary escapism, and a willingness to seek balance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in developing structured study habits with planned breaks to manage stress and reduce phone dependency, using CBT strategies to replace avoidance behaviors with constructive activities.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to experience anxiety and guilt over their study habits, struggling with phone addiction as a form of escape.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user seeks escape through their phone when academic pressure mounts, but is open to implementing structured study routines to regain control.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage implementing the Pomodoro Technique to balance study and leisure time, reinforcing how structured breaks can enhance focus and reduce reliance on phones.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels hopeful and more engaged in social settings after joining clubs but occasionally reverts to phone use for comfort.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress with the user's engagement in new activities, recognizing the user's insight into how shyness affects their interactions and the challenge of self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage gradual improvement in social confidence using positive self-talk, helping the user see their value in group dynamics and set small social goals.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels better about interacting with peers and appreciates their reduced phone usage, experiencing newfound confidence in contributing to discussions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes increased confidence in social and academic settings as the user realizes their potential to contribute effectively, though some self-doubt remains.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote consistent use of affirmations to reinforce the user's confidence during social engagements, encouraging ongoing participation in group activities and effective communication.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is experiencing improved self-confidence and satisfaction with reduced phone dependence, feeling more in control and present in social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes strengthened self-management skills and an increase in confidence, emphasizing the user's growing ability to balance technology use with interpersonal connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user's efforts to initiate conversations and manage anxiety using journaling and reflection to build on successful interactions, reinforcing their communication skills.
