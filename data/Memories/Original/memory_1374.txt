# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is a high school student who is struggling with social isolation and self-perceived inadequacies, particularly regarding social interactions and familial expectations. He reflects a desire to connect better with others and feels pressure to meet social and academic standards.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang shows symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem. There has been no mention of a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels frustrated and out of place, especially in social situations where he perceives himself as failing to connect.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Wang's tendency to internalize perceived social failures and his strong belief in personal flaws that hinder social connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help Wang reframe negative beliefs about social interactions and begin practicing positive social engagement techniques.
