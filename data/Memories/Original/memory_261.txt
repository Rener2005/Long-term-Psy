# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
E (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing feelings of loneliness and challenges with dormmate relationships. E describes recent conflicts based on differing beliefs leading to tension and exhaustion after social interactions. E desires stronger connections but struggles with balancing social engagement and personal solitude.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
E exhibits symptoms of social anxiety and uncertainty in forming relationships. There have been no formal disclosures of a mental health disorder diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
E feels torn between a desire for connection and the anxiety that potential conflicts with dormmates provoke.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that E is caught in a cycle of avoidance due to fear of arguments but maintains a willingness to explore low-pressure interactions to alleviate tension.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive-behavioral techniques to help E challenge the fear of conflict and initiate low-pressure social interactions. Aim to foster realistic expectations and coping mechanisms for navigating relationships.
