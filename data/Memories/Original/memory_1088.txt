# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xinyi is a recent high school graduate who has been dealing with feelings of anxiety and sadness due to the loss of a close friend. Despite being accepted to university, Xinyi struggles with an overwhelming sense of grief and a fear of reminders related to the loss. Xinyi is also reflective about this conflict between expected happiness and current emotional state.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xinyi experiences symptoms of grief and anxiety, particularly surrounding emotional events and social expectations. There is no formal diagnosis provided.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xinyi feels anxious and sad, struggling with a pervasive sense of emptiness and guilt for not feeling happier about university acceptance due to the recent loss.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xinyi is trapped in a cycle of grief and self-criticism but exhibits capacity for introspection and willingness to reframe guilt into a more compassionate self-perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xinyi reframe feelings of guilt and accept the coexistence of grief and joy. Encourage the use of journaling and small, enjoyable activities like walking to enhance emotional awareness and reduce anxiety.
