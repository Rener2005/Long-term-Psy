# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is a middle-aged woman experiencing significant marital distress. She feels disconnected from her husband, who drinks frequently, leading her to feel lonely and unsupported. Li is introspective, seeking better understanding and management of her emotional responses.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of loneliness and low motivation but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed and trapped by feelings of loneliness within her marriage, leading to a lack of motivation and enjoyment in activities she once loved.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li struggles with feelings of inadequacy and isolation but demonstrates a cautious readiness to explore her emotional state and relational communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li identify and challenge negative perceptions about her self-worth and relationship. Begin developing strategies for improving communication with her husband.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li continues to battle feelings of neglect and self-blame, behaviorally manifesting in replaying past arguments and doubting her worth in the relationship.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li's sense of self-worth is heavily tied to her husband's actions, indicating a cycle of blame and self-doubt, yet she shows openness to learn new communication methods.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in recognizing the pattern of self-blame and encourage a shift to a more self-compassionate perspective. Foster effective communication strategies by expressing needs without assigning blame.
