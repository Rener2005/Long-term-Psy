# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young adult dealing with significant personal challenges, particularly concerning a distant relationship with a boyfriend. She often feels overwhelmed with emotions and seeks understanding and control over her anxieties. 

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences anxiety and low self-esteem, primarily around personal relationships and self-worth, without any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and low, contemplating deeply about her boyfriend's lack of communication, which spirals into thoughts of being uncared for.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that the user experiences a cycle of self-critical thoughts, showing distress but also openness to constructive communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts and prepare for constructive communication with her boyfriend. Encourage engagement in activities for distraction and emotional regulation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more anxious, struggling with intrusive thoughts about her boyfriend's absence, leading to a sense of being consumed by these worries.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's difficulty in coping with solitude and her inclination towards negative speculation, yet notes her openness to distraction techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to manage thoughts by engaging in reading and journaling to redirect focus. Explore acknowledging thoughts as separate from reality, integrating CBT techniques to govern anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels low and unmotivated, impacted by unsuccessful attempts to reach her boyfriend, leading to a decrease in work motivation and hopefulness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's difficulty concentrating and the negative emotional impact this has had on her routine, observing openness to regulating emotions more effectively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user to set small, realistic work goals and incorporate mood-boosting techniques such as short walks, to regain motivation using cognitive behavioral strategies.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels better and more independent, participating in social activities that have improved her outlook on relationships and personal happiness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an increase in the user's independence and social engagement, noting her ability to experience happiness without reliance on her boyfriend.

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue reinforcing positive social interactions and encourage reflection on joyful experiences through journaling. Challenge negative expectations to foster an open mindset toward future engagements.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Sustain open communication with family and expand on engaging in fulfilling activities like painting and walking. Incorporate family support in maintaining emotional security and well-being.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels empowered and focused on personal and professional growth, dealing with some stress related to balancing work and studies.

[Observational Impressions]
