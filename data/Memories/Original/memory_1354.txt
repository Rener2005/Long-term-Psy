# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
User is navigating the pressures of impending graduation and is currently feeling anxious and uncertain about their future. These feelings are compounded by reflections on past academic failures, which weigh heavily on their mindset.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences anxiety and depression, driven by a persistent sense of inadequacy and negative self-comparison, although no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious due to upcoming academic assessments and recalls of past exam failures.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user often makes unfavorable comparisons with peers, contributing to feelings of low self-worth, yet shows readiness to explore new perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge all-or-nothing thinking and focus on attainable goals to manage academic stress and promote a more balanced sense of self.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel inadequate and frustrated after meeting old classmates and engaging in self-comparisons.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user's feelings of insecurity are exacerbated by social comparisons, yet the user exhibits openness to redirecting focus onto personal passions like writing.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT to help the user acknowledge personal achievements in writing and foster self-worth independent of peer comparisons. Encourage setting personal writing goals and exploring social support networks.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels discouraged by repeated job rejections, leading to diminished self-esteem and motivation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency towards cyclical negative thinking following setbacks, but also a willingness to find validation through creative pursuits.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to concentrate on personal writing goals as a means to bolster confidence and view creative success as progressive, not contingent on external validation. Support is focused on using CBT to manage negative thoughts about job rejections.

# Fourth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an ongoing struggle with comparing the user's life trajectory to that of others, though there is an improved recognition of personal success in writing.

[Consultation Goals]

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels better after recent support from a friend but grapples with social anxiety and confidence in new interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user's hesitance in social interactions but also identifies an increased willingness to practice conversational skills.

[Consultation Goals]  
Foster social confidence by engaging user in practice with small, achievable conversational goals. Utilize CBT to reduce fears of negative judgment in social contexts.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences increased positivity due to engaging in volunteer work, yet still struggles with self-doubt when alone.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes meaningful progress in the user's engagement with purposeful activities, and notes self-doubt remains when isolated.
