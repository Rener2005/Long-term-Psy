# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
H (pseudonym) is a high school student who experiences feelings of inadequacy and anxiety in academic and social settings, especially concerning interactions with teachers and classmates. H reports feeling overwhelmed by criticism and struggles with emotional responses to perceived judgments from peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
H exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
H feels overwhelmed and isolated after experiencing criticism from a teacher, leading to heightened feelings of inadequacy and negative self-worth.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that H is sensitive to perceived criticism and tends to internalize negative feedback, but shows potential openness to reevaluating these perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help H identify and challenge negative thought patterns, fostering healthier self-perceptions. Begin developing strategies for managing social anxiety and emotional responses to criticism.
