# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with social interactions and anxiety, particularly in group settings. Recently, Zhang has been trying to balance gaming habits with academic responsibilities, as gaming became a significant focus during the pandemic.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang shows symptoms of social anxiety and avoidance behavior, with no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels lost and anxious, using gaming as a coping mechanism, which further exacerbates anxiety regarding academic performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang is caught in an avoidance cycle, yet shows an openness to rethink habitual responses and consider small, manageable changes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang break the cycle of avoidance, establish a sense of accomplishment through small study tasks, and explore alternative outlets for achievement and engagement beyond gaming.
