# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a university student majoring in environmental science, who struggles with anxiety, particularly around authority figures, and is concerned about meeting expectations. Zhang has faced persistent pressure from family, especially a critical father, which influences current relationships and situations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms of anxiety linked to self-doubt and a fear of disappointing authority figures. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious about starting an internship, expressing concerns about interacting with supervisors and fearing potential criticism similar to past familial experiences.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang carries a significant burden of worry about living up to expectations and has a tendency to assume negative outcomes without evidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang using CBT techniques to reframe views on authority figures, encouraging a perspective that values learning and mentorship over perfection. Develop strategies to manage anxiety, such as positive affirmations and proactive communication planning.
