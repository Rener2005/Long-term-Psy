# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is an individual who recently completed a detox program and struggles with feelings of uncertainty and anxiety about their ability to stay on track with sobriety. They express doubts about their self-control and worry about how to cope with stress without resorting to substance use.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user is experiencing anxiety related to maintaining sobriety post-detoxification and has a history of substance use.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and anxious about their future, doubting their ability to maintain sobriety and fearing relapse.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with self-doubt and a lack of confidence in their ability to change, but is seeking support to improve communication with family members.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user communicate their needs more effectively and build strategies to manage stress without resorting to substance use. Assist the user in preparing to have constructive conversations with their parents.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels defeated and anxious following an unsuccessful attempt to discuss past substance use with their parents.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user feels misunderstood and struggles with feelings of powerlessness when communicating with their parents about past issues.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in developing effective communication strategies using “I” statements to express feelings without blaming others. Encourage the user to practice these techniques and explore coping strategies for managing anxiety related to discussions about past substance use.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences anxiety about potential relapse, particularly when encountering old friends associated with past substance use.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a conflict between the user's enjoyment of past social activities involving substance use and their current pursuit of sobriety, leading to anxiety and self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user identify strategies to engage in social situations without risking relapse, including setting boundaries and finding supportive environments. Promote self-confidence and personal control over social choices.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels encouraged by recent participation in a support group, finding value in shared experiences and empathy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s desire to contribute to the group environment but notes lingering fears of judgment and vulnerability.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in gradually increasing participation in the support group, using reframed thoughts to manage fears of judgment. Encourage the user to set personal goals for sharing experiences, fostering a sense of contribution and connection.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a mix of hope and anxiety about the feasibility of their personal development plan but is motivated to find fulfillment in work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user’s doubts are rooted in past experiences of relapse, yet acknowledges the user’s awareness of supportive resources and skills.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in incorporating supportive friends into their development plan as accountability partners. Assist the user in breaking down long-term goals into manageable tasks, focusing on skills enhancement and job fulfillment.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels increasingly in control of their life, particularly noticing improvements in family communication.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s growing confidence in expressing feelings to their parents, resulting in a more supportive and understanding relationship.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce the user’s progress by setting goals for regular meaningful family interactions and developing a plan to maintain positive changes. Encourage documenting achievements as reinforcement to counter any potential challenges.
