# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang is a nursing student who struggles with anxiety, particularly related to cleanliness and the upcoming qualification exam. Wang reports compulsive hand-washing and fears of contamination due to heightened awareness of diseases, referencing a nurse who contracted hepatitis B as a trigger for these fears.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang experiences symptoms of anxiety and compulsive behaviors consistent with obsessive-compulsive tendencies, particularly linked to fears of contamination and performance anxiety, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels overwhelmed and anxious about both the nursing exam and cleanliness, experiencing compulsive behaviors that interfere with daily activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wang’s compulsive hand-washing is a coping mechanism for anxiety, yet Wang shows an openness to cognitive restructuring and behavioral interventions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to address Wang's anxiety and compulsive behaviors. Focus on developing techniques to challenge contamination fears and manage exam-related stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels angry and isolated due to judgment from colleagues about cleanliness habits, which exacerbates anxiety and compulsive behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Wang's defensive reactions to colleague feedback elevate anxiety, but Wang demonstrates insight into the disparity between self-perception and reality.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Wang in examining perceived judgments and using cognitive re-framing to challenge negative thoughts. Encourage social engagement strategies to mitigate feelings of isolation.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang is struggling with feelings of self-doubt following familial confrontations about cleanliness habits, impacting focus on academic responsibilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Wang’s increasing awareness of the negative impact of compulsive behaviors but notes ongoing difficulty in applying balanced thinking to oneself.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate cognitive restructuring to promote self-compassion and realistic assessments of cleanliness needs. Encourage small, systematic changes to reduce compulsive behaviors.
