# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a professional navigating challenges related to anxiety, particularly in maintaining eye contact and social interactions. These difficulties impact both her personal and professional life, heightening feelings of guilt and self-consciousness.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of social anxiety, primarily surrounding eye contact and perceived social judgments, although no formal mental health diagnosis has been indicated.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed by guilt and anxiety, especially in work environments, due to her concern over inadvertently making inappropriate eye contact.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's tendency to fixate on perceived negative evaluations from others but notes her insight into her thought patterns and willingness to adopt new strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li challenge distorted perceptions about eye contact, improve confidence through self-compassion, and increase comfort in social environments.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li experiences persistent anxiety, exacerbated by feelings of inadequacy and fear of judgment during interactions with her husband.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li’s entrenched negative self-image linked to social interactions, yet sees openness to reframing perceptions and reducing self-critical thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Li in identifying and challenging skewed beliefs about eye contact with her husband, fostering self-acceptance, and encouraging gradual practice of eye contact alongside supportive partner communication.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and self-critical regarding her ability to engage with colleagues, particularly during meetings, due to fear of negative judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist detects Li’s recognition of avoidant behaviors and the implicit cycle of anxiety but sees potential in her self-awareness to alter this pattern positively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in incrementally changing behavior patterns in professional settings, using brief eye contact and positive reinforcement of small successes, implementing CBT strategies to shift focus from fear to engagement.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li continues to feel anxious and hesitant in social settings despite increased self-awareness; her fear of embarrassment limits active participation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li’s increased mindfulness about her anxiety triggers and sees her growing motivation to confront social challenges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li’s use of self-compassion and evidence-based reality testing to challenge fears about social inadequacy, using CBT to strengthen confidence in social engagement.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels a mix of nervousness and excitement about applying new social strategies; doubt lingers regarding her capability to execute them effectively.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Li's readiness to embrace social skill improvements gained from group therapy, balanced by natural apprehension about practical application.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strategize for Li’s real-life application of social skills through manageable conversation goals, reinforcing positive self-talk and anxiety management techniques before social gatherings.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels significantly more confident, reporting improved communication with her husband and colleagues since practicing new strategies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li’s increasing self-assurance and proactive engagement in conversations, indicating effective application of CBT techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Li in maintaining her newly found confidence by setting specific social interaction goals, using past successes as reinforcement, and challenging remaining fears with positive self-dialogue.
