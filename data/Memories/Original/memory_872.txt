[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is nearing graduation and feels significant anxiety about upcoming social interactions post-graduation. Wang is particularly concerned about fitting in and feels performance pressure in social settings, especially when meeting new people.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang exhibits symptoms of social anxiety but has no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels anxious and overwhelmed by the prospect of future social interactions, fearing embarrassment and rejection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Wang's inclination towards negative self-comparison and a sense of being stuck in a cycle of doubt, but also sees motivation to change these thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help Wang challenge negative thoughts and apply self-compassion. Develop manageable steps towards facing social anxieties.
