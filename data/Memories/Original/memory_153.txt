# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
A (pseudonym) is a university student who often feels inadequate, especially in social situations. A feels anxiety and isolation related to social interactions and often doubts self-worth due to past experiences that include feelings of rejection and criticism, reflecting a lifelong struggle to fit in.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
A experiences symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem stemming from past interpersonal experiences but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
A feels anxious and low, particularly concerning a misunderstanding with roommates that exacerbates feelings of isolation and inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes A's tendency to internalize misunderstandings as personal failures, leading to heightened feelings of loneliness and fear of rejection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help A reframe negative thoughts about self-worth and develop skills to express emotions in social settings, aiming to improve communication and reduce isolation.
