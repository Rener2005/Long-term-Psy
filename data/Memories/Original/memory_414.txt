# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Abel is a student who experiences anxiety due to his mother's declining health and fears about being expelled from school. He seeks acceptance from classmates and Mr. Brown at school.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Abel exhibits symptoms of anxiety related to familial health issues and academic performance anxiety, without a formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Abel feels anxious about his mother's health and fears potential expulsion from school, seeking acceptance and understanding from peers and teachers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Abel's struggle with feelings of isolation and the fear of judgment, but notes a willingness to express emotions creatively and to reach out for support.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Abel express his feelings constructively, like through art, and build confidence in communication with peers and teachers.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Abel continues to experience anxiety, particularly related to his mother's health, and uses drawing as an emotional outlet.

[Observational Impressions]  
Abel is beginning to recognize the benefit of externalizing his emotions through art and is open to finding new ways to communicate his feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Abel in reinforcing positive affirmations and using drawing to communicate emotions. Encourage self-kindness when feeling judged.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Abel feels nervous participating in class, fearing judgment, causing him to hold back and question his intelligence.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Abel’s awareness of his abilities but notes that self-doubt deeply affects his participation and confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Abel to challenge negative self-perceptions with evidence of his abilities and introduce a reward system to enhance class participation.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Abel feels more confident socially, having made new friends, although still grapples with anxiety about fitting in and academics.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Abel’s improved self-confidence and social integration, highlighting his growing comfort in peer interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Abel’s social skills and friendships by encouraging deeper interactions and support through structured conversational practice.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Abel feels better after the school music festival but remains anxious about potential expulsion, correlating it with self-worth.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist finds that Abel is starting to recognize his achievements but struggles with attributing them to something other than mere luck.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Abel in adopting self-affirming beliefs and recognizing his achievements, reducing anxiety related to expulsion fears.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Abel feels somewhat safer at school but continues to experience anxiety regarding academic workload and familial concerns.

[Observational Impressions]  
Abel is using his support network effectively but remains critical of his academic abilities, impacting his mental state.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Abel manage his workload by implementing organization strategies and encourage open emotional expression through journaling to cope with anxiety.
