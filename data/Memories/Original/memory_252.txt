# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Little Pan (pseudonym) is an individual facing emotional distress following the death of a grandparent. They struggle with anxiety, particularly regarding thoughts of death. Little Pan is seeking ways to manage these feelings and regain a sense of connection to their interests and relationships.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Little Pan experiences symptoms of anxiety characterized by intrusive thoughts about mortality. There is no mention of a formal mental health disorder diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little Pan feels overwhelmed by thoughts of death, leading to disconnection from daily activities and relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Little Pan's anxiety is significantly impacting their ability to enjoy previously pleasurable activities, yet they show some openness to discussing and managing these heavy thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to help Little Pan develop coping mechanisms for intrusive thoughts about death. Encourage reconnection with enjoyable activities to foster emotional resilience.
