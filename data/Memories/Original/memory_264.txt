# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
H (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with communication issues and emotional regulation, particularly in interactions with their father. H is eager to improve these aspects and is open to learning new strategies after experiencing recent challenges in managing personal interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
H experiences feelings of anger and frustration, particularly when dealing with family conflicts. There is no disclosed formal diagnosis, but H identifies challenges in emotional control and communication.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
H feels angry and frustrated due to difficulties in communicating effectively with their father, which leads to spillover irritability in other relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that H seeks understanding and acknowledgment from their father but feels dismissed, contributing to feelings of isolation and escalation in anger.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help H identify and challenge unproductive thought patterns regarding communication and emotional responses. Practice using "I" statements for more effective dialogue with their father.
