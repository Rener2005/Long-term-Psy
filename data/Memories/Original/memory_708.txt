# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who has been experiencing heightened anxiety and self-consciousness following a failed exam. They describe feeling overwhelmed by parental expectations and believe that these pressures have led to self-doubt and social withdrawal.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, primarily related to academic performance and perceived social judgments. There is no formal diagnosis disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and burdened, unable to dismiss feelings of failure and perceived judgment from peers and family.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's tendency towards catastrophic thinking and negative self-perception, but also a readiness to explore these patterns and open up about personal struggles.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user identify and challenge cognitive distortions. Encourage self-compassion and integrate coping mechanisms to improve anxiety management and social engagement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel intense academic pressure and struggles with self-worth, expressing fears of disappointing others if they fail to meet high expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's fixation on academic success as a measure of personal worth but also notes an emerging awareness of their own strengths, such as creativity and personal talents.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the user in recognizing and shifting unhelpful beliefs about self-worth. Encourage open communication with family and integration of hobbies to create a more balanced self-identity.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user expresses distress about academic performance and feels isolated, with anxiety thwarting concentration and social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees a pattern of avoidance and procrastination due to fear of failure, yet discerns potential for improvement as the user begins to question negative beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user break the cycle of anxiety and avoidance by recognizing past achievements and developing consistent study routines with positive reinforcement. Encourage socially supportive connections.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is engaging more socially but still feels anxious and self-critical, fearing negative judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress in the user’s readiness to connect with peers and self-awareness but notes persistent self-doubt in social and self-appreciation contexts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance the user’s social comfort through improved communication skills, like active listening, and reinforce self-worth through positive self-reflection and supportive peer interactions.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports feeling more relaxed and open, benefiting from the expressive arts experience and beginning to embrace vulnerability in social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes increased confidence in self-expression and a growing sense of ease in social settings, hinting at a shift away from solely academic-centered identity.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to maintain art as a form of emotional expression and explore additional mindfulness practices to support ongoing self-discovery and stress management.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels less pressured and more supported by their parents, contributing to improved emotional well-being and academic performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes the user’s strengthened familial relationships and social engagement, alongside an appreciation for learning beyond academic outcomes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Consolidate techniques that balance learning and well-being, celebrating small achievements to sustain confidence. Address any residual anxiety with positive reinforcement and support network engagement.
