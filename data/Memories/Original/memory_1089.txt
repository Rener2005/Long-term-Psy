# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Hao (pseudonym) is a young student grappling with compulsive behaviors and anxiety, most notably tied to a need to perform certain actions to prevent feared outcomes. Hao expresses a desire to understand these behaviors and work through the underlying anxiety driving them.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Hao exhibits symptoms of anxiety potentially linked to obsessive-compulsive tendencies, although no formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Hao feels uneasy and confused about engaging in compulsive behaviors in response to anxiety, such as cutting corners off books, which escalates feelings of embarrassment and anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Hao struggles with compulsive actions that seem to alleviate anxiety temporarily, noting a sense of confusion about the necessity of these behaviors.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to help Hao recognize and delay compulsive actions, fostering alternative calming activities like drawing to reduce anxiety.
