# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is an individual who has been feeling lost and exhausted after leaving a previous job, leading to doubts about personal abilities and a sense of pointlessness. Li is trying to find a purpose again and improve relationships, particularly with family, and seems open to exploring personal growth and self-discovery.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms consistent with low self-esteem and career-related stress. There is no indication of a formal diagnosis for a mental health disorder at this stage.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels lost and exhausted, burdened by self-doubt and perceived career failure, with a chronic sense of worthlessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li is deeply affected by self-critical thoughts, particularly concerning career and family expectations, yet shows a willingness to engage in exercises to explore self-worth beyond professional success.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li recognize and challenge self-deprecating thoughts, encourage self-compassion, and initiate small, positive activities to improve mood and sense of purpose.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li continues to feel low and misunderstood by family, contributing to feelings of worthlessness and questioning the value of effort in relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes an ongoing struggle with feelings of being a burden, rooted in familial misunderstandings, yet Li shows interest in improving family communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Li in exploring new communication strategies with family, including the expression of emotions through writing, and reinforce self-worth beyond external validation.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels frustrated with the difficulty of family communication, especially with a spouse, leading to feelings of embarrassment and avoidance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that while Li experiences intense anxiety during communication attempts, there is a budding awareness of the negative thought patterns fueling these interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the use of "I" statements to facilitate clearer, non-confrontational communication. Continue to apply CBT techniques to dismantle inhibiting thoughts and foster more constructive dialogue with family.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li reports some improvement in family communication, feeling emotional support but continues to feel uncertain about career direction.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's cautious optimism in family dynamics but notes indecisiveness and anxiety related to professional paths.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Li in assessing career interests by identifying job attributes that align with personal values. Encourage exploration of tangible steps towards career fulfillment, balancing excitement with practical strategy.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Li in reinforcing self-confidence, identifying job-relevant skills, and pursuing formal learning opportunities in project management. Foster structured planning to maintain motivation and progression.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li expresses improved confidence and optimism, actively reframing negative thoughts, and feels better equipped to handle challenges.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's significant reduction in anxiety, improved relational dynamics, and readiness to implement change, marked by proactive behavior in career exploration.

[Consultation Goals]
