# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
W (pseudonym) is a student experiencing stress and pressure from school and family expectations. W feels a sense of frustration with studying and the resulting academic performance, perceiving it as a barrier to parental approval and self-satisfaction.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
W displays symptoms of anxiety and low confidence in academic abilities but has not been diagnosed with a formal mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
W feels stressed and frustrated by academic challenges and the perceived expectations of parents, expressing a cycle of distraction and a lack of motivation for studying.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant recognizes W's struggle with negative perceptions of academic incompetence, yet notes a willingness to explore new approaches to learning.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to help W reframe learning as a positive, achievable task. Initiate the process of breaking down tasks into small, manageable goals to foster a sense of accomplishment.
