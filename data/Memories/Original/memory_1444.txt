# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaofan is a young individual who experiences overwhelming feelings of self-blame and anxiety, primarily triggered by parental conflicts concerning academic performance. Xiaofan seeks to find healthier ways to cope and manage these emotional challenges.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaofan exhibits signs of anxiety and self-esteem issues, particularly relating to family dynamics and a perceived expectation of perfectionism, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaofan feels burdened by self-blame for parental discord and experiences anxiety with associated physical symptoms like chest tightness and sleep disturbances.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiaofan shows a strong tendency towards internalizing parental conflicts and negative self-assessment but is receptive to exploring cognitive restructuring.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to help Xiaofan challenge self-blaming thoughts, develop self-compassion, and implement anxiety-reducing techniques such as deep breathing.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaofan feels trapped by escalating family conflicts and struggles with cycles of anger and guilt, sometimes resorting to self-harm.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiaofan's need for alternative coping mechanisms and acknowledges a pivotal openness to exploring healthier emotional outlets.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce and practice replacement strategies for self-harm, such as drawing and journaling, and reinforce the use of CBT techniques like identifying cognitive distortions to alleviate guilt and anger.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaofan experiences increased stress and difficulty concentrating on school tasks due to exam pressures and fear of parental disappointment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes prevailing perfectionistic thoughts and emotional paralysis, highlighting a need for cognitive reframing to reduce educational stress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Xiaofan to set specific, achievable study goals and adopt relaxation strategies to alleviate examination anxiety, focusing on self-compassion practices.

# Fourth Consultation
