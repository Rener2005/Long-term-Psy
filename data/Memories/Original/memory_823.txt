# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
User A is a university student grappling with feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, primarily in connection to academic performance and social interactions. A describes the initial college experience as overwhelming and feels isolated due to the sudden change in environment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
User A experiences symptoms of anxiety and loneliness but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
User A feels down and overwhelmed by the transition to college, missing the familiarity of high school and considering dropping out due to isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes User A's pervasive negative self-comparisons and feelings of isolation, but observes a willingness to adopt a patient and proactive approach to making social connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help User A challenge negative self-thoughts and perceptions about friendship-making. Encourage engaging in social activities gradually to counter feelings of isolation.

# Second Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist User A in challenging beliefs of inadequacy by recognizing personal strengths and using CBT to encourage social interactions without self-imposed pressure.

# Third Consultation
