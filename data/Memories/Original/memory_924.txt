# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user (who hasn't disclosed their name) is a student dealing with anxiety related to academic pressure and social interactions, specifically related to physical appearance. The user is self-conscious about acne and has recently been avoiding social situations due to this discomfort.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user shows symptoms of social anxiety characterized by a significant focus on appearance and fear of judgment in social contexts. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and self-conscious about their appearance, particularly in social situations, and this affects their willingness to engage in those scenarios.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with negative self-comparisons and avoidance behaviors but is open to addressing these issues through exploration of thoughts and alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify and challenge negative thoughts related to social anxiety. Initiate a gradual exposure plan to manage and reduce the anxiety associated with social interactions.
