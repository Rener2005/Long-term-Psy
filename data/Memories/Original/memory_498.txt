# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li Ming is a professional who grapples with anxiety and feelings of inadequacy, particularly in response to perceived failures and social interactions. Li Ming is motivated to improve self-confidence and communication with colleagues and peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li Ming experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, often exacerbated by self-critical thoughts and social comparison, but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming feels anxious and overwhelmed by a recent project failure, struggling with feelings of worthlessness and a lack of belonging at work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li Ming is prone to all-or-nothing thinking and experiences intense self-doubt, but is open to challenging negative perceptions and exploring personal achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to aid Li Ming in identifying and challenging all-or-nothing thinking, encouraging recognition of successes to balance self-perceptions and reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
