# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang is a university student who struggles with feelings of anxiety, particularly about social acceptance and academic performance. A recent argument with a classmate has heightened concerns about being judged due to family financial circumstances. Yang is eager to improve social interactions and manage feelings of inadequacy.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang experiences symptoms of anxiety, primarily related to social situations and self-esteem, but has not received a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels anxious and insecure, predominantly due to worries about how peers perceive them after a recent conflict.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Yang’s tendency to focus on negative predictions about social acceptance, yet there’s openness to exploring positive past interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang challenge negative beliefs about social interactions and increase self-acceptance. Encourage Yang to engage in small, low-pressure social interactions to gradually build confidence in social settings.
