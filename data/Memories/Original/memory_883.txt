# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who recently took a break from school, feeling anxious and lonely, with difficulties in social interactions and communication with parents. The user is eager to reconnect with classmates and manage academic pressures while also addressing parental expectations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences persistent anxiety, loneliness, and fear of judgment, impacting social interactions and self-esteem. There is no specific mental health disorder formally diagnosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and lonely due to time away from school, expressing a desire to reconnect with classmates but fearing rejection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's struggle with perceived social rejection and a developing openness to self-kindness and strategic social re-engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative assumptions about social rejection. Encourage incremental social interactions and promote self-compassion in social contexts.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and rejected after unsuccessful attempts to reconnect with classmates, leading to self-doubt and feelings of unworthiness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user's negative thoughts are pervasive but acknowledges the user's potential to view such situations from a more compassionate and balanced perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in reframing negative thoughts with more balanced perspectives, increasing resilience against perceived social rejection. Reinforce small steps to encourage proactive social engagement.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]
