# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) experiences social anxiety, particularly in professional settings where interaction with clients is required. Zhang reports a fear of judgment and embarrassment, and frequently experiences physical symptoms of anxiety such as trembling, sweating, and dizziness during social interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang has indicated symptoms consistent with social anxiety disorder but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis or treatment history.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious about interacting with clients and fears appearing incompetent, leading to physical symptoms of anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's tendency to fixate on potential negative outcomes despite receiving some positive feedback, indicating a pattern of negative thinking.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang recognize and challenge negative thoughts about social interactions. Encourage gradual exposure to social situations with preparation and self-compassion as part of the behavioral strategy.
