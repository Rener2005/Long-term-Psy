# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
L (pseudonym) is a 22-year-old university student preparing to graduate. L has been feeling anxious about job interviews and struggles with self-doubt regarding their qualifications and abilities. Recent experiences include participating in job interviews and group interview practices.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
L experiences symptoms of performance anxiety and low self-confidence in professional settings but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
L feels anxious and overwhelmed by self-doubt, especially regarding job interviews and perceived inadequacies compared to others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that L is receptive to exploring thought patterns and exhibits a willingness to reframe their self-critical beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help L challenge and replace negative thoughts with more realistic and positive ones. Develop strategies to manage interview-related anxiety and improve self-confidence.
