# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang (pseudonym) is a military professional who struggles with feelings of inadequacy and career-related anxiety, particularly concerning promotions and meeting family expectations. Yang is contemplative about future aspirations and is actively seeking ways to improve self-perception and motivation.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang experiences symptoms of anxiety and self-doubt, particularly regarding career advancement, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels defeated and unsure about the future following another missed promotion, accompanied by a sense of failing to meet personal and familial expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Yang tends to focus on setbacks and failures, leading to a cycle of negative self-assessment, yet shows willingness to explore personal strengths and achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang challenge negative self-talk, acknowledge past successes, and set realistic, achievable goals for career advancement to alleviate feelings of inadequacy and uncertainty.
