# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Chen is in her early twenties and is currently navigating feelings of anxiety and inferiority, particularly concerning her educational achievements and relationship status. She has not shared any recent significant events, but the context suggests ongoing self-reflection about her personal and academic growth.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Chen exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly relating to self-worth in academic and relational contexts. There is no formal diagnosis disclosed at this time.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Chen feels anxious and inferior regarding her educational background and relationship, struggling with negative self-perceptions and self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Chen's persistent negative self-comparisons, yet notes her openness to recognizing her positive qualities and willingness to change her thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to aid Chen in challenging negative thoughts, fostering self-compassion, and devising strategies to manage anxiety, especially in relation to academic and relational self-worth.
