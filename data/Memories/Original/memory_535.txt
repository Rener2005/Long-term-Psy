# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liang (pseudonym) is an adult male who struggles with alcohol use as a coping mechanism for work-related stress. He expresses feelings of guilt, especially regarding the impact of his drinking on his relationship with his wife, who is supportive but concerned.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liang has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder, but he experiences symptoms of stress and guilt related to his drinking habits.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liang feels conflicted between using drinking as a form of relaxation and the ensuing guilt it causes, particularly concerning his relationship with his wife.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Liang is caught in a cycle of stress-induced drinking followed by guilt, but he demonstrates readiness to explore and implement healthier coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Liang in identifying stressors and developing alternative stress-relief strategies. Encourage communication with his wife to involve and gain support in his efforts to reduce drinking.

# Second Consultation
