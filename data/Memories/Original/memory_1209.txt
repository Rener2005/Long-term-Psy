# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
G is a young adult navigating feelings of loneliness and self-comparison, particularly when witnessing their peers' relationship happiness. G often puts on a brave front but internally battles with questioning self-worth in social contexts.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
G experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, mainly manifesting through self-comparison and doubts about personal likability. No formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G feels lonely and frustrated, comparing themselves negatively to peers and questioning their own value and worth.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that G struggles with harsh self-judgments and maintains a facade of composure, yet displays a nuanced understanding of interpersonal dynamics.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help G identify and challenge distorted thinking patterns, fostering more self-compassion and balanced self-awareness. Encourage proactive engagement in social activities to alleviate feelings of isolation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G experiences moments of self-doubt, particularly in new social settings, feeling inadequate and fearful of disapproval despite some progress in interpersonal relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes G's improvement in connecting with others, yet recurring anxieties about personal value persist. G is gradually embracing more optimistic thinking.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support G in continuing to challenge self-demeaning thoughts and in recognizing their unique contributions in social contexts. Encourage setting small, attainable goals to boost social confidence.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G experiences anxiety in expressing themselves during group activities, fearing judgment and failing to voice individual ideas due to self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees G gradually acknowledging the irrationality of some fears but also notices persistent hesitation in sharing opinions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help G build confidence in expressing ideas through preparation and practice, using CBT to reinforce positive interpersonal experiences and reduce performance anxiety.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G shows increased willingness to communicate but still experiences situational anxiety when interacting with unfamiliar peers, though with moments of growing self-assertiveness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes G's emerging self-confidence and adaptability. Despite uncertainties, G sustains efforts to participate actively and express openly.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage G to internalize affirmations and mindfulness exercises to sustain this momentum. Focus on fostering authentic self-expression and appreciation of personal insights.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G feels positive about new friendships formed through shared activities, but underlying anxiety about acceptance and social faux-pas persists.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes G's enjoyment of mutual interests with new friends and notes the occasional surfacing of self-conscious thoughts, inhibiting full engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce G's efforts in fostering friendships by practicing vulnerability and open communication, cultivating reassurance and resilience towards potential disagreements.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
G exhibits enhanced confidence in group social scenarios, experiencing satisfaction from active participation and feeling a deeper connection with peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices G's growing ease in social environments, attributed to increased self-assurance and proactive involvement, yet minor apprehensions linger.
