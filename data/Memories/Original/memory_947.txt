# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Zhang (pseudonym) is a university student who is facing challenges with job interviews and social anxiety. Zhang recently participated in several job interviews but has not yet received any offers. He feels anxious about his future career prospects and often compares himself to his peers, which contributes to feelings of inadequacy.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Zhang exhibits symptoms of anxiety related to job interviews and social interactions. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder, but Zhang struggles with low self-esteem and self-doubt.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Zhang feels anxious and inadequate due to unsuccessful job interviews and comparisons with peers, leading to a negative outlook on his career prospects.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang shows signs of low self-worth linked to external achievements, but there is a willingness to explore self-improvement strategies, particularly in job interview skills.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help Xiao Zhang challenge negative self-perceptions and improve self-confidence. Develop practical strategies for interview preparation and set manageable goals for skill enhancement.
