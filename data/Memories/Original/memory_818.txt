# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student experiencing feelings of loneliness and insecurity due to age differences with peers. They find approaching classmates intimidating and avoid social situations because of anxiety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem, though no formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and insecure, particularly in social situations, due to a perceived gap between themselves and their classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s difficulties with approaching peers, increased anxiety, and critical self-talk, but there is a willingness to challenge these beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to assist the user in recognizing and reframing negative thoughts. Develop small, actionable goals for engaging with peers to build confidence in social settings.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious after participating in class activities, still doubting their social adequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes cognitive distortions where the user gravitates towards worst-case scenarios, but there is an openness to changing perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the identification and challenge of cognitive distortions, encouraging a supportive inner dialogue. Use role-playing and grounding techniques to build social confidence.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences excitement and frustration, feeling both eager to engage in campus activities and overwhelmed by new social environments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes automatic negative thoughts impacting the user’s self-perception, but new evidence of social successes is recognized.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in recognizing and countering self-critical thoughts, focusing on small social achievements. Encourage gradual involvement in smaller groups to enhance comfort.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is pleased with some initial connections but uncertain about deepening these relationships and worries about social etiquette.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes progress in forming connections, yet notes the user’s concern over maintaining relationships.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to take small, intentional steps in interactions, focusing on shared experiences and support. Reinforce self-trust and the importance of gradual relationship building.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels accomplished and more confident after successful collaboration in social and academic settings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an increase in self-esteem and recognition of personal contributions in group dynamics.

[Consultation Goals]  
Cultivate proactive engagement by identifying opportunities for socializing, emphasizing the significance of personal contributions. Continue building communication skills and confidence.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports feeling positive about their social skills, having gained comfort in inviting classmates for gatherings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s improved mood and reduction of loneliness due to stronger social support networks.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support further involvement in social activities such as clubs or sports, reinforcing existing strategies for managing anxiety. Encourage continued exploration of new social opportunities.
