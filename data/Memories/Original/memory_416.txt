# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a working professional in the IT field, who faces significant stress due to balancing a demanding job and familial responsibilities, including spending time with a young child.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei experiences symptoms of work-related stress and anxiety, particularly in striving to achieve a work-life balance, but has not indicated any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels overwhelmed by work commitments and guilty for not spending enough time with family, expressing the pressure of being inadequate in both areas.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang Wei is caught in a cycle of self-doubt and guilt concerning work and family roles but shows willingness to explore strategies for better balance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei reframe negative thoughts, recognize achievements, and develop practical strategies for managing time between work and family commitments effectively.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Zhang Wei struggles with constant work preoccupations affecting family interactions yet is open to exploring communication strategies to improve these interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang Wei in establishing boundaries for work communications and devise new approaches for fostering meaningful family connections, using cognitive behavioral strategies to prioritize presence and engagement at home.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]
