# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is preparing for a crucial entrance exam and is dealing with intrusive thoughts about taboo subjects, which affect their ability to study and provoke anxiety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences significant anxiety primarily linked to intrusive thoughts and the perceived pressure to perform perfectly, yet no formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed by intrusive thoughts that disrupt their focus and create anxiety and guilt, especially regarding the upcoming entrance exam.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that the user has a tendency to equate academic performance with self-worth, manifesting in a cycle of increased stress and self-imposed perfectionism.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user recognize and challenge unrealistic pressures and negative thoughts. Encourage relaxation activities to create mental space and manage stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains anxious about the entrance exams, triggered by self-comparison and doubts about their adequacy in relation to peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes an ingrained habit of self-comparison fueling feelings of inadequacy, though the user demonstrates insight into the irrationality of these thoughts when encouraged.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to adopt a more balanced perspective on personal progress and self-worth, utilizing CBT techniques to reframe negative beliefs. Introduce regular breaks and relaxation activities to alleviate stress.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is suffering from anxiety and frustration concerning the possibility of failing the entrance exams, with notable impact on mood and sleep.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes critical internal dialogues, filled with fear of failure and perceived external pressures, yet the user shows openness to explore alternative narratives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in developing coping strategies for anxiety and self-doubt using cognitive restructuring and relaxation techniques. Reinforce the understanding that personal worth is not dictated by exam outcomes. 

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports a mild improvement in anxiety levels through the use of meditation but continues to struggle with self-doubt about capabilities and future success.

[Observational Impressions]  
There is a noted improvement in the user's openness to previously successful experiences, yet they continue to grapple with the pressure of self-comparison and success expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to actively recall past successes as a means to boost self-confidence, while promoting consistent mindfulness practices to enhance psychological well-being.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels positive, with mindfulness meditation reducing self-doubt and aiding focus during studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's improved focus and reduced anxiety, attributable to the regular practice of mindfulness, leading to increased presence in familial interactions and studies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in maintaining mindfulness practices during study sessions, using reminders to ensure break adherence and encourage ongoing self-compassion, particularly as exam dates approach.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user expresses a significant decrease in overall anxiety, managing emotional responses effectively while studying for the entrance exam.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a shift toward hopefulness and confidence, facilitated by effective use of coping strategies like breathing exercises and affirmations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce existing practices that manage anxiety, such as affirmations and supportive social connections, while emphasizing self-recognition of achievements as the exam approaches.
