# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Ke (pseudonym) is a school student struggling with feelings of frustration and anger, particularly when faced with accusations or misunderstandings from peers. Xiao Ke is inclined to react with anger to defend himself and often feels misunderstood by classmates and authority figures.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Ke exhibits symptoms of emotional dysregulation, yet there is no specific mental health disorder diagnosed. There is a notable difficulty in managing emotional reactions and interpersonal conflicts.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ke feels frustrated and misunderstood, leading to conflicts with classmates. There is a pervasive sense of needing to defend himself to be respected.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Ke experiences a strong impulse to react defensively, fueled by a belief that aggressive behavior is necessary to garner respect, but he is open to exploring alternative communication strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement CBT strategies to help Xiao Ke recognize and alter defensive thought patterns, aiming to improve emotional regulation and communication skills in peer interactions.
