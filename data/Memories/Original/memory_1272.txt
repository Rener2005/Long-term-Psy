# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Aiko (pseudonym) is a high school student experiencing anxiety and distress regarding her eating habits and body image. She is actively involved in athletics and has a pattern of comparing herself to her peers, leading to feelings of inadequacy. Aiko is struggling with binge eating and subsequent guilt, desiring to establish a healthier relationship with food and her body.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Aiko manifests symptoms of anxiety, low self-esteem, and disordered eating behaviors, although there is no formal diagnosis of an eating disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Aiko feels overwhelmed and anxious, particularly about her weight and eating habits, which is tied to a fear of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Aiko's strong tendency to engage in self-critical comparisons, yet she shows readiness to explore self-kindness despite current difficulties.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral strategies to help Aiko identify and challenge automatic negative thoughts about her body and eating habits. Promote self-compassion and develop actionable steps for healthier coping mechanisms.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Aiko feels self-conscious and anxious about her body image, leading to harmful comparisons and behaviors at school.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Aiko's deep-seated belief in her inadequacy, impacting her self-worth, but notes willingness to explore more compassionate, supportive thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Aiko in recognizing and countering negative beliefs through evidence-based challenges and alternative, positive self-talk. Encourage engagement in pleasurable activities that do not hinge on physical appearance.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Aiko in redefining her relationship with food through structured meal planning and mindful eating. Encourage Aiko’s exploration of non-appearance-based gratifications from her athletic involvement.
