# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Jacob is a young student who struggles with social interactions and is often overwhelmed by sensory inputs, such as loud noises. He enjoys solitary activities like art and feels nervous about sharing spaces with others. Jacob seeks to overcome his social challenges and build more comfortable peer relationships.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Jacob exhibits signs of social anxiety and sensory sensitivity but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Jacob feels nervous and uncomfortable in social settings, fearing negative judgment and preferring solitude.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Jacob experiences significant anxiety in social settings, driven by a fear of negative interactions, yet demonstrates a willingness to explore positive social experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Jacob challenge his fears around social interactions and gradually increase his comfort with peers. Focus on identifying positive social experiences and introducing small, achievable social goals.
