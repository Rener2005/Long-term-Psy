# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhenzhen (pseudonym) is a student who recently transferred to a new school and is experiencing challenges in connecting with classmates. Zhenzhen feels anxious about social interactions and desires to fit in, concerned about others' perceptions and judgments.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhenzhen experiences symptoms of social anxiety, especially in new environments, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhenzhen feels anxious and insecure about fitting in, worried about being judged or saying the wrong things in social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhenzhen has automatic negative thoughts during social interactions, but demonstrates some awareness of these patterns and is open to reframing them.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhenzhen identify and challenge negative thoughts, promoting more realistic and balanced perspectives. Initiate small social interactions to gradually reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhenzhen experiences continued anxiety during social interactions, with persistent fears of being judged or making mistakes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a pattern of catastrophizing and black-and-white thinking, yet Zhenzhen is receptive to learning and applying cognitive reframing techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Zhenzhen challenge catastrophizing thoughts by identifying evidence and fostering more compassionate self-talk. Encourage small, manageable social engagements to build confidence.
