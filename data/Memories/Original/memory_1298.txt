# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Little Ming (pseudonym) is a young child who experiences anxiety and uncertainty in social situations, particularly at kindergarten. Little Ming enjoys solitary activities, especially playing with toys, and is exploring ways to engage with peers. Recently, Ming has been adjusting to changes in routine and experiencing new social interactions within the family context.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Little Ming demonstrates signs of social anxiety and challenges with transitions and communication. The use of visual aids and structured, supportive interaction is starting to be explored as a part of the strategy to support Ming's growth.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little Ming is feeling anxious when faced with new social interactions but has shown some excitement about trying new activities and interacting more with peers and family.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Little Ming has a strong interest in familiar activities and toys, but is open to trying new experiences with guidance. Ming often feels uncertain about what to say in social interactions, yet shows eagerness to learn and practice.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive-behavioral strategies to help Little Ming develop social skills and confidence in communication. Use role-playing and visualization techniques to prepare for social interactions, promoting gradual participation in new activities.
