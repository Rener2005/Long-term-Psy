# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Chen (pseudonym) is a college student experiencing heightened anxiety, particularly in social situations and interactions with peers. Despite the transition to college, Chen finds it challenging to engage socially and often recalls past high school experiences that negatively impact confidence.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Chen experiences symptoms of social anxiety, characterized by fear of judgment and avoidance of social interactions, but there is no mention of a formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Chen feels anxious about social interactions and experiences a sense of isolation, fearing judgment and embarrassment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that Chen's anxiety is linked to past negative experiences, but also sees a willingness in Chen to challenge these perceptions with support.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Chen identify and challenge negative thoughts related to social situations. Encourage gradual exposure to social interactions alongside developing strategies for anxiety management.
