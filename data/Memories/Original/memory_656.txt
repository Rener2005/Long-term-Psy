# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who struggles with social anxiety related to peer interactions and academic performance. Recent challenges include adapting to new life dynamics at university, particularly concerning relationships with roommates.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of social anxiety, with no formal diagnosis disclosed. This anxiety is exacerbated by self-imposed pressures and fear of judgment from others.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and inadequate, particularly in social interactions, leading to physical symptoms like sweating and a racing heart.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's struggle with self-imposed perfectionism and a strong fear of judgment, which affects daily functioning. The user is open to exploring more positive perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts about judgment and perceptions of inadequacy. Encourage small, manageable steps towards engaging with roommates to reduce avoidance behaviors.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to experience high levels of anxiety in social situations, feeling awkward and fearing negative judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives a deep-seated habit of negative self-talk and a reluctance to apply rational perspectives to oneself, despite understanding such perspectives for others.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive restructuring to challenge assumptions of negative judgment by focusing on evidence-based evaluations of social interactions. Encourage keeping a log to objectively assess interactions and reactions of peers.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences intensified anxiety affecting both social interactions and academic focus, with concurrent fear of disappointing important figures like family.

[Observational Impressions]  
Persistent self-comparative thoughts hinder the user's social engagement, despite recognizing a lack of evidence for perceived judgment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user to apply cognitive reframing techniques to mitigate self-critical beliefs, emphasizing self-compassion and realistic appraisal of social interactions. Develop strategies to initiate and maintain conversations in a dorm setting.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about group activities, perceiving difficulty in fitting in and expressing thoughts openly.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices progress in the user's willingness to engage in group settings, albeit with persistent negative self-judgment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage proactive participation in group settings by preparing talking points and employing breathing techniques to manage anxiety. Reinforce self-kindness and recognition of mutual insecurities among peers.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more confident after initiating a new friendship but remains worried about maintaining social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an increase in self-awareness and initiative yet notes ongoing concerns about conversational skills and maintaining interest.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen conversational skills by preparing engaging topics and practicing self-reassurance during silences. Reinforce friendship maintenance through regular activities and open communication.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels increasingly proactive and confident but fears regression into anxiety-driven behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges the user's significant progress in social engagement and self-confidence, accompanied by aspirational goals for continued growth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Maintain engagement by balancing academic responsibilities with social activities. Encourage mindfulness of imperfection as a growth opportunity, supported by journaling to track progress and emotional responses.
