# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a parent who has expressed anxiety and guilt about the relationship with his son, particularly concerning his son's struggles in school and the recent incident where he lost his temper.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences challenges with parenting, feeling overwhelmed and inadequate, but there is no disclosed formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is overwhelmed and anxious about his son's educational struggles, experiencing guilt after a recent altercation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li's actions stem from a place of care, though they manifest through unhelpful reactions to stress, showing a willingness to explore better approaches.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to help Li manage emotions and alter interactions with his son, focusing on calm communication and relationship building.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels stressed and helpless, expressing doubt about his parenting effectiveness despite efforts to connect with his son.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li's sense of failure and frustration is linked to an urgent need to positively influence his son's behavior and future.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in developing patience and calm communication strategies using CBT techniques to address feelings of inadequacy and stress.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels confused and helpless regarding traditional education methods' ineffectiveness on his son and is tired of frequent conflicts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li struggles with habitual reactions under stress but shows willingness to find new ways to motivate his son.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Li in fostering calm discussions and set small goals linked to his son's interests using CBT principles, to reduce conflict and encourage motivation.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed in conflicts, expressing frustration in his attempt to communicate the importance of academic responsibility to his son.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that Li often shifts from open engagement to defensiveness in conflict but is willing to redefine communication approaches.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li to shift focus from correction to connection by practicing calm, inquiry-based communication and reducing defensiveness.

# Fifth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Li's enthusiasm towards building on the initial improvements through conscious communication efforts.

[Consultation Goals]

# Sixth Consultation
