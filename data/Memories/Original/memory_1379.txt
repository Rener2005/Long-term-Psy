# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing vivid hallucinations, stress, and paranoia related to recent personal losses, including the anniversary of their father’s death. Yang is increasingly scared in social settings, feeling watched with hostility.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang reports vivid hallucinations and persistent paranoia but is uncertain about the specific diagnosis, questioning whether it might be schizophrenia.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels overwhelmed by fear and anxiety, exacerbated by the anniversary of their father’s death and the accompanying hallucinations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Yang is trapped in a cycle of fear, doubting reality due to vivid hallucinations, but shows willingness to engage and adopt grounding techniques.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang distinguish between reality and hallucinations, beginning with grounding exercises to manage acute anxiety.
