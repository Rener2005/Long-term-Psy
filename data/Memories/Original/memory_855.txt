# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with feelings of isolation and anxiety, particularly in social situations and when attempting to connect with classmates. Yang is eager to find ways to improve social skills and overcome self-doubt to feel a greater sense of belonging.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang experiences symptoms of social anxiety and self-doubt but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels isolated and anxious about not fitting in and connecting with classmates, leading to persistent self-doubt and loneliness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Yang struggles with negative self-perceptions and avoidance of social interactions but shows readiness to challenge these thoughts and engage more socially.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang challenge negative thoughts about self-worth and develop strategies to engage more confidently in social settings.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels overwhelmed and anxious about social interactions, particularly after a negative experience, leading to a fear of being judged and further self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Yang in identifying and challenging cognitive distortions related to self-worth and judgment. Encourage practicing social interactions with manageable goals.
