# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu Ming is an employee who experiences anxiety and obsessive-compulsive tendencies, particularly with fears of contamination. He describes feeling pressure from work and battles with compulsive behaviors like frequent hand-washing impacting his daily functioning.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu Ming shows symptoms indicative of anxiety disorders, particularly obsessive-compulsive tendencies related to contamination fears. There is no formal diagnosis mentioned but self-reported anxiety and compulsive behaviors are noted.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu Ming feels anxious and trapped in cycles of compulsive hand-washing, driven by fears of contamination.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Liu Ming's struggle with distressing thoughts and compulsive behaviors. He shows awareness of the irrationality of these thoughts but is challenged by his strong compulsions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Liu Ming challenge irrational beliefs about contamination, and introduce gradual exposure exercises to mitigate compulsive hand-washing.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu Ming continues to experience elevated anxiety, exacerbated by work stress, and finds himself engaging in compulsive hand-washing.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that although Liu Ming is aware of the lack of evidence for his contamination fears, the compulsive urge remains strong. He remains open to developing strategies to cope.

[Consultation Goals]  
Work on graded exposure to reduce the compulsion to wash hands immediately, helping Liu Ming build tolerance for uncertainty and delaying compulsive behaviors.
