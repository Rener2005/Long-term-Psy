# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu is a student who experiences anxiety and self-doubt, particularly in social situations and academic environments. He desires to improve his self-confidence and reduce feelings of inadequacy.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu shows signs of anxiety, notably in social interactions, but has not indicated any formal diagnosis of an anxiety disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu feels anxious and overwhelmed, struggling with concentration in school and harboring feelings of not belonging.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Liu's strong tendency towards self-criticism and avoidance in social settings, but also a willingness to engage in self-reflection and small actions toward change.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Liu in challenging negative self-thoughts, encouraging small steps towards social interactions to alleviate feelings of isolation and build confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu continues to struggle with anxiety, particularly after a social encounter, leading to increased avoidance and embarrassment in similar situations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Liu's heightened self-awareness about avoidance patterns, indicating readiness to approach social interactions differently through gradual exposure.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Liu using CBT strategies to initiate simple social interactions, monitor self-reflection post-encounters, and apply anxiety-coping techniques to progressively reduce avoidance behavior.
