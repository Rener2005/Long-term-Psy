# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Chen is a young adult who struggles with social anxiety, particularly in situations involving new people or large gatherings. Recent events have heightened this anxiety, notably an overwhelming experience at a birthday party that led to feelings of embarrassment. Chen seeks to manage these feelings and improve social interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Chen experiences symptoms of social anxiety, including nervousness and fear of judgment in social situations. These symptoms have not been formally diagnosed as a disorder, but they significantly impact social engagements.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Chen feels nervous and embarrassed about a recent social event, where social interactions were particularly challenging and anxiety-inducing.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Chen is deeply concerned about external judgments and faces a strong internal pressure to perform well socially, which affects enjoyment and presence in the moment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to challenge the fear of judgment and help Chen develop self-compassionate perspectives. Identify small, manageable social interactions to build confidence gradually.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Chen continues to feel anxious about social situations, expressing avoidance behaviors that have led to negative feelings and missed opportunities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Chen anticipates negative social outcomes, which strengthens the avoidance cycle, yet shows willingness to reframe these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Chen to face social situations with reduced self-imposed pressure using CBT techniques and gradual exposure to smaller social interactions. Reinforce positive thinking through affirmations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Chen feels anxious about speaking in front of people, struggling with fears of judgment and appearing awkward.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Chen's tendency for all-or-nothing thinking, creating stress around imperfection in social interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Chen challenge extreme thoughts and embrace imperfect, authentic expressions in social contexts. Introduce and practice relaxation and breathing techniques.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce relaxation techniques as a personal coping mechanism and encourage practical application of insights from the course in upcoming social gatherings.

# Fifth Consultation
