# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a parent concerned about their young son, referred to as little B, who was recently taken out of kindergarten. Little B exhibits behaviors that suggest he may have specific needs, such as playing alone and demonstrating repetitive actions. The user is eager to understand and support little B's development, particularly concerning social interactions and emotional well-being.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Presently, little B shows signs of difficulty with social interaction and repetitive behaviors, potentially suggestive of autism spectrum characteristics. No formal diagnosis has been disclosed by the user.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is stressed and concerned about little B's withdrawal from kindergarten and how it affects his development, particularly in social skills.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that the user is highly attuned to little B's needs and displays a strong desire to help him engage with others, though feels uncertain about the best methods to achieve this.

[Consultation Goals]  
Explore strategies within the framework of cognitive behavioral therapy to encourage little B's social engagement and help the user foster an environment conducive to social interaction. Focus on using little B's interests to facilitate connections with peers.
