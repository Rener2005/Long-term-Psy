# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Bingbing (pseudonym) is a lively and energetic student who occasionally acts out in class by making noises and throwing things. These behaviors are done in the spur of the moment for excitement but often lead to trouble with teachers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Bingbing exhibits impulsive behaviors in class, often for excitement, but recognizes the negative consequences of these actions.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Bingbing feels excited by disruptive behaviors but also experiences nervousness and apprehension about getting into trouble with teachers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Bingbing's energetic nature and enthusiasm, along with an emerging understanding of the impact these behaviors have on others and a willingness to explore self-regulation strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral strategies to help Bingbing recognize the triggers for disruptive behaviors and develop self-control techniques, such as using a make-believe 'magic wand' to promote positive behaviors and reward systems for reinforcement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Bingbing reports feeling more in control and proud when able to restrain disruptive impulses in class.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Bingbing is engaging well with imaginative strategies, showing increased self-awareness and a sense of accomplishment in practicing self-regulation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Continue using cognitive strategies to reinforce self-control and focus on identifying specific scenarios where Bingbing can practice these techniques. Encourage the development of personal goals and self-reward systems.
