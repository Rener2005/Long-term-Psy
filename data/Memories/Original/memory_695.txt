# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student who experiences feelings of depression and anxiety, particularly related to his interactions with roommates and social connections. He often doubts his self-worth and worries about being rejected by peers, seeking to improve his relationships and emotional management.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li shows symptoms of anxiety and depression, notably in social settings, although no official mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels depressed and anxious about his interactions with roommates, interpreting their actions as signs of rejection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's tendency to engage in negative thinking patterns, such as jumping to conclusions, which amplifies his feelings of rejection, though he expresses a willingness to explore alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li recognize and challenge distorted thoughts, thereby fostering a more balanced perception of social interactions. Set feasible goals to alleviate anxiety through positive engagement with his roommates.
