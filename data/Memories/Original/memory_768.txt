# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student experiencing difficulties balancing gaming habits with academic responsibilities. They report feeling guilty when gaming interferes with study time and express concerns about upcoming exams.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits signs of academic-related stress and gaming as a coping mechanism but has not reported a formal diagnosis of any mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels down and guilty, struggling with anxiety and inability to focus on studies due to excessive gaming.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user uses gaming as an escape from academic pressure, leading to further guilt and anxiety but is open to exploring alternative strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify triggers for gaming and encourage the development of study routines and rewarding breaks to manage academic stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is overwhelmed and feels isolated, with increasing reliance on gaming to cope with academic stress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's struggle with balancing pressures from academic performance and self-perceived inadequacy but shows willingness to explore alternative study methods.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in developing effective study techniques and incorporate non-gaming breaks to reduce stress, using CBT to manage feelings of inadequacy.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels isolated with heightened tension, leading to difficulties in forming social connections and excessive gaming for community-like feeling.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a strong desire for social integration and growth in the group setting, although marred by anxiety and self-doubt about social abilities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower the user with skills to manage social anxiety using CBT, promoting engagement in structured social activities like study groups to enhance social support.

# Fourth Consultation

# Fifth Consultation

# Sixth Consultation
