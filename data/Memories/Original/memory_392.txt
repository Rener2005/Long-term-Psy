# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Ms. Zhang is a professional working in a high-pressure environment where she experiences feelings of isolation and frustration, particularly pertaining to work-related interactions. She is actively seeking ways to manage her emotions better.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Ms. Zhang exhibits symptoms of stress and anger, with no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder reported. She is focused on understanding and improving emotional responses in a constructive manner.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ms. Zhang is feeling angry and frustrated due to perceived misunderstandings and dismissiveness from colleagues, contributing to feelings of isolation at work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Ms. Zhang's anger is linked to a sense of not being understood, yet she exhibits a willingness to explore alternative perspectives on her colleagues' intentions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use CBT techniques to help Ms. Zhang recognize and challenge negative thoughts, develop empathy, and implement strategies like deep breathing to manage anger effectively.
