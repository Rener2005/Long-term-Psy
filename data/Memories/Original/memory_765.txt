# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student dealing with anxiety related to social interactions and feelings of being judged due to financial struggles. They are sensitive to how classmates perceive them, often feeling misunderstood and worried about being wrongly accused of misconduct.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li shows symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and misunderstood, particularly worried about classmates perceiving them negatively after a recent incident involving missing money.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li is experiencing cognitive distortions, particularly linking personal financial status to others' perceptions, but is open to exploring these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li identify and challenge negative thoughts, focusing on reframing perceptions based on evidence rather than assumption. Encourage Li to engage in social activities to rebuild confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Li’s tendency to assume the worst about social situations, highlighting their habitual negative thought patterns, yet notes Li’s openness to considering alternative explanations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in developing strategies to reframe social perceptions using evidence-based thinking. Encourage Li to engage in affirmations and recognize personal strengths to build resilience against self-doubt.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li to practice expressing ideas confidently in group settings, using role-play and positive self-talk. Strengthen Li’s ability to recall positive feedback and successes to challenge self-doubt.
