# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaohao is a student who is experiencing significant anxiety, primarily focused on health-related worries despite receiving reassurance from medical professionals. He is in search of strategies to manage these intrusive thoughts and regain focus on his daily life.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaohao experiences symptoms of health anxiety and intrusive thoughts but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohao is feeling anxious and helpless, frequently consumed by thoughts of having a serious health condition, impacting his studies and social life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiaohao's worries are fixated on health concerns despite medical reassurance, and this anxiety markedly disrupts daily functioning.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive-behavioral techniques to help Xiaohao challenge health-related worries by using rational counter-thoughts and evidence-based reassurances. Encourage engagement in activities that provide relief from anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohao remains anxious about health issues despite medical validation, struggling with concentration and intrusive thoughts during his studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiaohao's persistent anxiety arises from a lack of evident health issues versus an internalized fear, indicating a need for reassurance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Xiaohao’s ability to trust authoritative medical advice, and introduce stress-reduction strategies such as structured daily routines and concise monitoring of health-related thoughts.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohao experiences stress and avoidance behaviors towards studying due to fear of illness, feeling overwhelmed by anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
Xiaohao's anxiety about potential health problems undermines his ability to engage with academic tasks, yet there's acknowledgment of lacking substantial evidence for such fears.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage short, productive study sessions with interim breaks, using CBT to identify and challenge anxious thoughts, reinforcing the ability to maintain academic focus amid health anxieties.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohao feels a temporary reprieve from anxiety during pleasurable activities, yet apprehension about health concerns returns afterward.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress as Xiaohao engages in enjoyable social activities, though residual fear persists post-activity, indicating a need for sustained cognitive restructuring.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize positive activity experiences to counterbalance health worries post-engagement. Introduce a gratitude-focused journaling practice to solidify positive memories.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohao reports improved mood and reduced health anxiety through active involvement in social and extracurricular activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges Xiaohao’s growing self-efficacy in using engagement with activities as a distraction from health worries, leveraging this improvement for further cognitive resilience.

[Consultation Goals]  
Integrate enjoyable activities into regular routines to sustain emotional wellbeing and reduce preoccupation with health anxieties. Explore new interests like photography to enhance positive experiences.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaohao feels more confident and reports enhanced academic and social engagement, though anticipates potential challenges during exam periods.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiaohao's increased confidence and organizational skills contributing to reduced stress, supported by parental guidance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop proactive strategies to manage exam-related anxiety, reinstating breathing exercises, and regular study breaks to reinforce calm and preparedness.
