# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a professional in a company, facing anxiety and self-doubt related to his job performance and decision-making. Zhang expresses a desire to improve his work efficiency and confidence when participating in team activities.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang exhibits symptoms of work-related anxiety and occasional self-doubt but has not been diagnosed with a specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang is anxious about his performance and the impression he makes on colleagues, feeling uncertain and overwhelmed by new tasks and responsibilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's tendency to second-guess his actions and observe a cycle of stress and avoidance, though Zhang shows openness to exploring alternative thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang identify and challenge automatic negative thoughts, aiming to increase his confidence and ability to manage workplace anxiety effectively.
