# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang is a university student dealing with loneliness and insecurity, especially after a recent exam failure. Yang feels trapped in a routine and struggles with fears of judgment and inadequacy.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang experiences symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels lonely and insecure, coupled with fears of being judged by others, particularly after the public servant exam.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Yang’s struggle with negative self-talk and avoidance of social interactions, but notes a willingness to explore self-compassion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang challenge negative self-perceptions and develop self-compassion, gradually encouraging Yang to engage in social interactions.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels confused and overwhelmed about post-graduation career uncertainties, with persistent worries about lacking skills and direction.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Yang’s tendency to compare negatively to peers, resulting in anxiety and self-doubt, yet Yang shows openness to redefining personal strengths.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Yang in identifying strengths and set small, manageable goals for career exploration and network building, utilizing CBT strategies to reduce self-comparison.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Yang’s automatic thoughts of judgment in social settings, though there is an awareness of their lack of concrete evidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Yang in developing conversation initiation and maintenance strategies through CBT techniques, reinforcing self-acceptance and self-kindness.
