# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Pengpeng (pseudonym) is a kindergarten student adjusting to a new environment. Pengpeng wants to be a hero, like Ultraman, and often expresses frustration through physical interactions but is eager to learn better ways to communicate their needs.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Pengpeng exhibits signs of difficulty in managing emotions, particularly anger and frustration, in social settings. There’s no formal diagnosis of a behavioral disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Pengpeng feels frustrated and struggles with handling emotions when playing with classmates, often resorting to physical actions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices that Pengpeng is imaginative and desires to embody a heroic character, yet struggles with expressing emotions verbally.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to teach Pengpeng alternative ways to express emotions and needs verbally, fostering skills to manage frustration and improve peer interactions.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Pengpeng continues to experience frustration and anger, particularly in situations involving sharing toys with classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Pengpeng’s reactive nature when encountering perceived unfairness but notes the willingness to explore peaceful resolutions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Pengpeng to replace physical responses with verbal requests and to practice patience and empathy in social interactions, reinforcing these ideas through role-play.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Pengpeng feels conflicted about following rules and struggles with impulses when engaging in school activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist identifies Pengpeng's resistance to conforming, mainly due to a lack of understanding of the reasons behind rules.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive strategies to help Pengpeng understand the purpose of rules, promoting self-regulation and communication with authority figures.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Pengpeng’s nervousness around authority figures persists, yet comfort with peers has increased.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Pengpeng’s progress with peers but identifies a continuing ambivalence about perceived authority figures' opinions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower Pengpeng by building confidence to interact positively with authority figures, using role-play to reduce anxiety and explore perspective-taking.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Pengpeng is excited about sharing experiences and has begun forming positive connections with classmates through storytelling.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes improved confidence and social skills, as Pengpeng actively engages peers in sharing sessions and enjoys these interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Pengpeng in fostering communication skills and using structured planning to articulate thoughts, enhancing participation during playtime and group activities.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Pengpeng feels happy and confident, enjoying engaging with both friends and authority figures at school.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees significant progress in social adjustment, with supportive environments at home and school facilitating Pengpeng's emotional growth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce Pengpeng’s self-esteem and social skills through continued practice of communication strategies and deep breathing techniques, promoting long-term resilience and emotional stability.
