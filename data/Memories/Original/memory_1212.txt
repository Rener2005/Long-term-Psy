# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a university student, frequently experiences feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, particularly linked to academic performance and self-expectations. Recently, the user expressed disappointment about receiving a third-class scholarship after previously attaining a second-class one. The user aims to maintain high standards as a form of self-validation.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels disappointed and anxious due to unmet academic expectations and self-imposed pressure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's cyclical thoughts of self-doubt but notes the user's openness to reevaluating these expectations through compassionate self-reflection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to challenge the user’s negative self-thoughts and foster a healthier self-image by emphasizing past achievements and setting realistic goals to manage academic expectations.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences anxiety and frustration, primarily induced by social comparisons and a perceived failure to meet personal and external expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the deep-seated belief that self-worth is tied to outperforming peers, yet the user is amenable to reconceptualizing personal success and progress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the user's understanding of intrinsic value apart from academic success by using CBT methods to focus on personal achievements and mitigate the stress of self-comparisons.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and frustrated due to a gap between academic efforts and results, contributing to a sense of helplessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s difficulty in sustaining effective study habits and the emotional toll of academic pressure, whilst noticing the user's drive to improve study techniques and emotional responses.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce effective CBT strategies to reorganize study habits and promote emotional resilience in pursuit of academic aspirations, emphasizing manageable actions and emotional regulation techniques.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains open to new study methods but is challenged by feelings of inadequacy during group interactions, accompanied by comparisons with peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s emerging confidence but also recognizes ongoing feelings of overwhelm and doubt, observing the user's adaptable approach to study challenges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen the user's confidence in group settings through CBT interventions, focusing on active engagement and self-validation techniques to counteract performance anxiety.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports reduced feelings of isolation due to positive group study interactions but continues to grapple with self-imposed high expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an improved sense of community in the user's academic environment, highlighting the user’s continuous struggle with self-expectations and confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to embrace self-compassion and realistically assess academic success through continuous self-reflection and affirming self-accomplishments via CBT strategies.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user expresses increased confidence and motivation, having found effective study methods, and is keen on pursuing further academic goals.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees significant progress in the user’s self-efficacy and collaborative learning, reflecting a resilient attitude towards academic challenges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Maintain and build on the user's confidence by setting long-term academic and career goals, promoting proactive engagement with potential mentors, and fostering a supportive academic network.
