# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a concerned parent, probably in their 30s or 40s, who struggles with anxiety related to their parenting style. They often worry about whether their overprotectiveness is impacting their child's development and independence, and struggle with feelings of guilt and self-doubt in their parenting decisions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety tied to parenting, but there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder. The anxiety is specifically related to fears about their child's independence and safety.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is anxious about their parenting, particularly regarding how their actions might impact their child’s independence and emotional well-being.

[Observational Impressions]  
The user appears genuinely committed to improving their parenting style and is reflective about their anxieties and overprotectiveness. They demonstrate willingness to explore more balanced approaches.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge and reframe anxious parenting thoughts. Develop strategies for promoting the child's independence while managing parental anxiety using CBT principles.
