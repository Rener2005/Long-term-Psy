# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Lin Feng is a student dealing with feelings of inadequacy and anxiety related to academic performance and social interactions. He is beginning to explore his emotions and is eager to find healthier ways to manage stress and expectations from both himself and others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Lin Feng faces symptoms of anxiety and struggles with self-esteem but has not shared any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Lin Feng feels overwhelmed and hopeless due to disappointing exam scores, fearing he might not meet others' expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Lin Feng struggles with negative self-comparisons and parental pressure, but he shows openness to reframing thoughts and noticing achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Lin Feng recognize his effort and achievements, while creating a balanced approach to studying and managing academic stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Lin Feng feels uneasy with classmates and doubts his abilities, fearing negative judgment and struggling with social anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Lin Feng's pervasive negative assumptions about peer interactions, highlighting his need to explore evidence and alternative views.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Lin Feng to challenge negative perceptions using evidence-based questioning and gradually engage in social settings to boost confidence.

# Third Consultation
