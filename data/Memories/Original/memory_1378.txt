# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a student dealing with recent distress due to a discovered incident that resulted in a serious warning from their school. Zhang is reflective about the incident's consequences and is eager to learn how to manage stress and impulses.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang feels anxious and ashamed about the incident and perceives a strong compulsion towards certain behaviors, indicating a need for coping strategies to manage stress and behavioral impulses.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang is overwhelmed with anxiety and shame due to recent behavioral discovery and school warning, feeling worried about judgment from peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang’s tendency to replay the incident, struggling with self-judgment but shows openness to identifying and managing urges more constructively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to help Zhang identify stressors and develop alternative coping mechanisms, encourage positive outlets like drawing and music to counteract urges.
