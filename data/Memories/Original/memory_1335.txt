# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a former smoker in their 50s who has been experiencing heightened anxiety about their health following the death of a colleague from lung cancer. Despite having quit smoking two years ago and receiving normal health check-up results, the user remains anxious and agitated, particularly concerned about past damage from smoking.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of health-related anxiety, with no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and worried about their health, particularly their lungs, due to a history of smoking despite normal health check-ups.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user exhibits signs of persistent health-related anxiety but is open to exploring rational perspectives and practicing anxiety-reducing strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user recognize and challenge irrational health-related fears. Encourage engagement in enjoyable activities and mindfulness practices to reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels tired and anxious about their health, affecting concentration and self-confidence at work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's anxiety is compounded by self-imposed high standards at work and a lack of evidence for feared judgments from colleagues.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in reframing negative self-judgments and anxiety through CBT, encouraging self-compassion and evidence-based assessments of perceived inadequacies.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
