# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a university student, is grappling with anxiety and nervousness, especially when it comes to future planning and maintaining relationships with friends and roommates. They have a strong interest in art and design but worry about job stability and success compared to peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences anxiety and self-doubt, notably when comparing themselves to others, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and overwhelmed by academic and peer-related pressures, and isolates themselves due to feelings of inadequacy and jealousy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's anxiety is fueled by negative self-comparisons and isolation but notes the user's openness to improving social connections and self-concept.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help the user challenge negative comparisons and focus on personal strengths. Encourage the user to engage in activities that develop social connections and showcase personal skills.
