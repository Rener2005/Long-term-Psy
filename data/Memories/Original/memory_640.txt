# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, referred to as "A" (pseudonym), is a young individual experiencing feelings of sadness and loneliness, particularly relating to familial changes and perceived emotional neglect. A's parents are divorced, and the user yearns for more connection with their busy father. A engages with siblings sporadically to alleviate feelings of neglect.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
A exhibits signs of social anxiety and distress related to familial relationships. The user experiences low self-worth and a sense of inadequacy in family dynamics.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
A feels ignored and unimportant due to limited communication with the father, which contributes to feelings of sadness and loneliness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes A's tendency to internalize feelings and a resistance to express vulnerability, yet there is some openness to alternative methods of emotional expression, like drawing.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to encourage A to express emotions through creative means and identify personal activities that promote small, manageable positive interactions at home.
