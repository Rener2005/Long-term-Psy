# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a family man struggling with feelings of helplessness and anxiety due to a cramped housing situation and its impact on family relationships. He feels responsible for providing a better living environment for his family.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei experiences significant stress and feelings of inadequacy related to family and housing issues but hasn’t mentioned any formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious and guilty about the cramped living situation, doubting his ability to provide for his family.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang Wei struggles with feelings of responsibility and self-doubt, yet shows openness to exploring constructive ways to manage family tension.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei reframe negative thoughts, improve communication with family, and brainstorm practical strategies for managing housing stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels frustrated and helpless about the lack of progress in resolving their housing situation, fearing he is failing his family.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Zhang Wei's persistent feelings of responsibility are driving his anxiety, but also acknowledges his willingness to involve his wife and potentially lighten the emotional load.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang Wei to identify and challenge negative thoughts through open discussions with his spouse. Promote small actionable steps to regain a sense of control and alleviate helplessness.
