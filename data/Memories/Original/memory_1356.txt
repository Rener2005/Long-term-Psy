# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a student dealing with anxiety related to repetitive hand washing, especially triggered by social events and concerns over contamination. Zhang is cautious about public perception and seeks control over compulsive behaviors.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms consistent with anxiety, particularly Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) tendencies related to hand washing, though no formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious and isolated due to compulsive thoughts about contamination and germs, leading to excessive hand washing.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang's heightened sensitivity to cleanliness and fear of germs, manifesting in behavior that creates social discomfort and embarrassment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang challenge irrational thoughts about contamination and develop practical strategies for managing compulsive hand washing.
