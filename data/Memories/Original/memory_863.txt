# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang is a university student navigating the challenges of a new study environment. She experiences pressure and anxiety related to academic performance, coupled with concerns about social acceptance among classmates. She is eager to learn methods to manage stress effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang exhibits symptoms of anxiety and stress under academic pressure, yet there is no formal diagnosis disclosed during the consultations.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang is experiencing significant stress and anxiety, feeling overwhelmed by academic pressure and struggling with concentration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Wang's anxiety stems largely from self-doubt and comparison with her peers, yet she shows a willingness to explore ways to alleviate this pressure.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy techniques to help Wang reframe negative thoughts and manage academic stress, starting with small, achievable study goals and relaxation techniques.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]

# Third Consultation

[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Wang to adopt structured study sessions like the Pomodoro Technique, incorporating relaxation methods to reduce anxiety and acknowledging her preparation efforts.
