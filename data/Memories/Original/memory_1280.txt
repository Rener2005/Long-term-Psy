# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is dealing with inner conflict regarding their relationship with their boyfriend, leading to anxiety and feeling overwhelmed in both personal and work life. They have a history rooted in early school years that affects their confidence and reliance on others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety linked to relationship conflict and self-worth issues, without formal mental health disorder diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious due to indecision about their relationship status, caught between affection for their boyfriend and unmet personal needs.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s struggle with anxiety and indecision stemming from past reliance on relationships for support, compounded by fear of loneliness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user identify and challenge negative thought patterns related to self-worth and relationship fears using CBT. Encourage journaling and mindfulness to reinforce positive affirmations and support from friends and family.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels stuck in a cycle of doubt and uncertainty, grappling with feelings of inadequacy in their relationship.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the impact of past negative experiences on the user's current thoughts and emotions, emphasizing fear of being alone and self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in differentiating past experiences from the present and develop balanced cognitive assessments. Introduce journaling to document positive relationship experiences and encourage open communication with their boyfriend through scheduled quality time.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is conflicted and confused, torn between past feelings for an individual named A and their current relationship.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user’s tendency to engage in black-and-white thinking and dwell on past relationships, leading to guilt and anxiety in their current relationship.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user to appreciate lessons from past relationships while focusing on building a positive present with their boyfriend. Use enjoyable activities to reinforce the current relationship and reduce anxiety through grounded mindfulness practices.
