# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who struggles with anger management and conflicts at school and home. They have a strong sense of loyalty and pride in defending friends but encounter repercussions for their actions. The user appears to find it challenging to manage emotions constructively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user displays symptoms of emotional dysregulation, primarily anger and frustration, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated and misunderstood, particularly when punished for defending friends, and experiences a strong sense of injustice.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's actions are well-intended but lead to negative consequences, and there is a struggle with impulse control.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to explore alternative ways of showing loyalty and managing conflicts without aggression. Introduce anger management strategies such as deep breathing and pausing before reacting.
