# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a recent graduate navigating the job market while experiencing doubts and anxiety about future career prospects. Li is thoughtful and eager to enhance job-seeking skills and overcome interview-related setbacks.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences anxiety and self-doubt related to career decisions and job interviews but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is feeling down and anxious due to a recent interview rejection, leading to self-doubt and apprehension about future job prospects.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li struggles with negative self-perception following setbacks but shows openness to challenging these thoughts by acknowledging past academic accomplishments.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li challenge negative beliefs and foster a more compassionate self-view while developing strategies for managing interview anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li continues to feel anxious and pressured as graduation approaches, struggling with comparisons to peers and feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li’s tendency to compare achievements unfavorably against peers but also recognizes Li’s willingness to reflect on personal strengths and accomplishments.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in identifying and challenging negative thought patterns with evidence from past achievements. Encourage a shift in perspective toward self-compassion and realistic self-assessment.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed with the multitude of career options and is anxious about making decisions, compounded by feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li is burdened by a fear of making wrong choices but is increasingly aware of the potential for positive reframing and self-encouragement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Li in recognizing strengths and viewing career decision-making as a positive exploration. Utilize CBT strategies to manage anxiety and build confidence in decision-making.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is anxious about an upcoming interview, plagued by self-doubt and fear of poor performance despite prior preparation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Li’s persistence of negative self-talk but acknowledges a developing ability to focus on strengths and utilize feedback.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce Li’s use of relaxation techniques and positive self-affirmation. Prepare Li to present strengths confidently during interviews, enhancing calmness and reducing negative self-comparisons.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels a mix of relief and lingering anxiety after completing an interview, with an increased awareness of the importance of preparation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's growing confidence as a result of thorough preparation and successful interview performance, alongside ongoing uncertainty about career direction.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage consistent preparation and reflection on positive experiences to bolster confidence. Guide Li in exploring supportive networks and potential career paths with curiosity and openness.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li experiences improved confidence from sharing experiences with classmates but continues to feel anxious about personal job prospects.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's positive impact from community engagement and increasing self-worth recognition, alongside persistent concerns about personal capabilities and job availability.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote continuous self-reflection and community involvement as sources of support. Encourage Li to maintain a strengths-based approach and use journaling to consolidate confidence during the job search.
