# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Wang (pseudonym) is a student dealing with anxiety related to cleanliness, especially triggered by a recent traumatic event involving a classmate. Wang describes a constant fear of germs leading to compulsive handwashing. Overall, Wang is seeking to manage anxiety and regain focus on studies and social life.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Wang shows symptoms consistent with anxiety disorder, primarily exhibited through obsessive thoughts about contamination and compulsive behaviors like excessive handwashing. No formal diagnosis has been provided.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Wang feels anxious and frightened about contamination, leading to compulsive handwashing and impacting daily life functionality.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Wang's heightened fear stemming from recent traumatic events and a struggle to balance this fear with a desire for normalcy, exhibiting both self-awareness and frustration.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral therapy strategies to help Xiao Wang challenge intrusive thoughts about contamination, enhance coping mechanisms, and establish a gradual reduction in compulsive behaviors related to handwashing.
