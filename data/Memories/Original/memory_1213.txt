# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student majoring in accounting, experiencing feelings of dissatisfaction and uncertainty regarding their current academic and career path. Recently, they attempted to transfer to the English department, which did not succeed, intensifying their feelings of doubt about their major and future.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of uncertainty and a lack of fulfillment in their current studies, although there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and frustrated, grappling with a strong disconnect between their major in accounting and their passions, leading to self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes the user's inclination towards creative endeavors, such as writing and analysis, which seem to be at odds with accounting's structured nature.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user explore how interests in writing and analysis could connect to career possibilities, fostering a more balanced perspective on career planning.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling anxious and doubtful about their major, affected by comparisons with peers and apprehensive about their skills in accounting.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that the user's self-doubt is exacerbated by self-comparison, but there is recognition of their determination and efforts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to focus on personal growth and setting small, achievable goals to mitigate anxiety and build self-confidence while exploring interest in problem-solving aspects of accounting.
