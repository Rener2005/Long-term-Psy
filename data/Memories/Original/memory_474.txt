# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Jim (pseudonym) is a student who experiences feelings of insecurity and self-doubt, particularly regarding social interactions and personal competency. Jim wishes to improve social skills and boost self-confidence, often feeling anxious in group settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Jim shows symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem but has not reported any formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Jim feels awkward and excluded during social interactions, with a persistent worry of not being accepted by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Jim tends to internalize negative peer interactions, leading to feelings of inadequacy and self-doubt, but shows a willingness to explore and challenge these perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Jim identify and challenge negative self-beliefs, encourage self-compassion, and develop strategies for engaging in social activities with more confidence.
