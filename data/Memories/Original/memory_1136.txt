# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Bai (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with forming connections, particularly with others outside of her comfort zone. She has expressed difficulty in trusting new acquaintances and often feels isolated. Bai is introspective and eager to improve her social interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Bai experiences symptoms of social anxiety and feelings of inadequacy. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Bai feels anxious and isolated, particularly struggling with forming connections with women, which contributes to her loneliness and frustration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Bai holds negative perceptions towards women, influenced by past experiences, which hinders her ability to form social connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Bai challenge negative thought patterns and foster more positive interpretations of social interactions. Begin developing strategies for initiating conversations and reducing feelings of isolation.
