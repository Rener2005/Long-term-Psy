# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
L (pseudonym) is a young student experiencing anxiety about attending school, especially around classroom participation and peer judgment. L describes feeling nervous and inadequate in social and academic settings. L's supportive family encourages open communication and personal growth.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
L shows signs of social anxiety, particularly fear of negative evaluation, but there is no formal mental health diagnosis recorded.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
L feels anxious and fearful about engaging in classroom activities and is particularly worried about being judged by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes L's struggle with self-doubt and avoidance in potentially anxiety-inducing situations, yet notes L's curiosity about trying new approaches to ease anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive-behavioral techniques to help L identify and reframe negative thoughts about classroom participation. Develop strategies to gradually increase comfort with speaking in social settings, beginning with preparatory exercises at home.
