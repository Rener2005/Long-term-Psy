# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a professional facing anxiety and self-doubt related to work performance, particularly in handling complex client situations. He is eager to improve his strategies in managing challenges and feels a strong drive to prove his competence.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei experiences symptoms of anxiety related to work performance, but no formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious and defeated due to doubts about his skills and effectiveness in managing work challenges.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei's pattern of self-doubt and high expectations for perfection, but also sees readiness to adopt more compassionate self-evaluations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei reframe negative thoughts and set practical goals to manage anxiety, fostering a mindset that acknowledges mistakes as learning opportunities.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei continues to feel overwhelmed due to uncertainty and self-doubt about his ability to handle complex cases at work.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's struggle with focusing on negative outcomes but also acknowledges his successes as evidence against these fears, signaling a pattern of cognitive distortion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang Wei to shift focus to strengths rather than perceived failures, developing mindfulness practices and reinforcing his confidence by celebrating successful cases and seeking peer support.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels anxious and pressured to perform well on new complex cases, experiencing avoidance due to fear of failure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of black-and-white thinking in Zhang Wei's self-assessment, which contributes to performance anxiety, but notes his openness to recalibrating these thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Zhang Wei in recognizing progress as sufficient and breaking tasks into manageable steps to alleviate perfectionistic tendencies, utilizing reminders of personal worth to strengthen confidence.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels optimistic about implementing new skills but remains anxious about incorporating them effectively into his work practice.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei's willingness to enhance client interactions with cognitive behavioral techniques, though there is an initial discomfort in applying new methods during sessions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Zhang Wei in gradually integrating new techniques by preparing guiding questions and practicing role-play, fostering comfort through structured planning and reflection on client interactions.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels positive and encouraged by recent successes at work, but wants to sustain the momentum and confidence gained.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes growing self-efficacy in Zhang Wei, alongside a conscious desire to continuously improve skills without regressing to past habits.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Zhang Wei set achievable goals for technical and leadership skill development, emphasize the importance of reflection, and encourage tracking progress through journaling and self-reward systems.
