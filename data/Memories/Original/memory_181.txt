# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Tang is a young adult, dealing with a recent breakup which has left her feeling lost and concerned about her future. She is particularly anxious about her upcoming civil service exam, worrying about her ability to focus and succeed.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Tang experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, exacerbated by recent personal events, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Tang feels hopeless and unfocused after a breakup, compounded by worries about an impending civil service exam.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Tang’s cycle of self-doubt, intensified by negative thoughts surrounding personal and academic challenges, yet notes Tang’s openness to exploring supportive self-talk.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Tang replace negative self-talk with supportive affirmations and develop a structured study plan to manage anxiety and improve focus.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Tang feels confused and anxious following contact with her ex-boyfriend, which disrupts her study routine and amplifies her self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Tang’s mixed emotions of happiness and guilt, identifying a pattern of anxiety-induced cognitive cycles tied to past interactions, while recognizing her willingness to implement mental strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Tang in establishing emotional boundaries and implementing structured “worry time” to regain study focus, supported with relaxation techniques and mindfulness practices.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Tang feels overwhelmed with dread about her civil service exam results, focusing on negative outcomes and potential disappointment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Tang’s pressure from familial expectations, but also her openness to considering alternative life paths and recognizing personal success stories.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote cognitive restructuring to help Tang recognize alternative outcomes and manage exam-related anxiety, reinforcing her resilience and exploring new opportunities.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Tang feels slightly more positive due to family support, yet continues to worry about career options post-breakup and the pressures of the civil service exam.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Tang’s initial exploration of career alternatives, noting her blend of anxiety and relief as she considers new opportunities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Tang to balance her preparation for the exam with exploring new career paths, using structured goal-setting and open communication with her support network.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Tang feels optimistic after passing the civil service exam but remains concerned about adapting to her new work environment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Tang’s excitement tempered with self-doubt about fitting in and performing well in her new job role, while recognizing her potential to apply learned coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Tang’s use of affirmation and reframing techniques to build confidence in her professional abilities, preparing her for interpersonal interactions in the workplace.
