# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Little L (pseudonym) is a student who experiences significant anxiety related to academic performance and parental expectations. She has been feeling overwhelmed by exams and struggles with the fear of failing and disappointing her parents.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Little L exhibits symptoms of anxiety, particularly performance anxiety, influenced by perceived high parental expectations. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little L feels anxious and overwhelmed by the pressure of upcoming exams, particularly fearing potential failure and parental disappointment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Little L is deeply affected by her parents’ expectations, resulting in avoidance behaviors that exacerbate her anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Little L reframe negative thinking and build evidence against cognitive distortions. Establish relaxation techniques to manage anxiety effectively during exams.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little L continues to experience significant anxiety triggered by exam-related stimuli, struggling with a cycle of negative thoughts about her performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Little L's openness to CBT techniques and her ability to rationalize her fears, though emotionally, she remains in the grip of anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on breaking negative thought loops through evidence-based challenges and positive affirmations. Strengthen routine relaxation techniques and propose exposure through practice simulations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Little L's growing ability to recognize and counter negative thoughts, yet anxiety persists, especially under pressure.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce strategies for identifying and reframing unhelpful thoughts, and conduct exposure therapy through mock exams to build confidence in stressful environments.
