# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student who is experiencing distress in her romantic relationship, leading to uncertainties about its future. She is also facing stress related to academic performance, affecting her ability to concentrate on studies.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li exhibits feelings of anxiety and self-doubt associated with relationship conflicts and academic pressures but has not indicated a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels lost and anxious due to frequent arguments with her boyfriend and is unsure whether the relationship should continue.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li struggles with negative thought patterns, particularly assumptions that her relationship issues may be unresolvable, but she is willing to explore healthier communication methods.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li identify and challenge negative thought patterns. Encourage open communication with her boyfriend to facilitate understanding and reduce stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels powerless due to continuing relationship arguments and increasing academic pressure, making her unable to focus on her studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's all-or-nothing thinking, revealing a tendency toward cognitive distortions in both relationship and academic contexts, but she is open to reframing her thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in recognizing cognitive distortions and encourage reframing thoughts using evidence and positivity. Develop a study plan to manage academic workload and maintain emotional equilibrium.
