# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a college student who is struggling with anxiety and feelings of guilt, particularly concerning eating habits and social comparisons. The user finds adapting to college life challenging and seeks to improve emotional well-being and self-worth.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety, low self-esteem, and potentially disordered eating behaviors, but there is no disclosure of a formal diagnosis of a specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and out of control, struggling to manage eating habits and experiencing guilt afterwards.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user uses food as a coping mechanism for stress and a perceived lack of self-worth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to identify triggers of anxiety and guilt, and develop healthier coping mechanisms for managing stress and emotions related to eating behaviors.
