# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
A (pseudonym) is a student who struggles with feelings of emptiness and confusion about life’s purpose, despite outward academic success and involvement in school activities. A is introspective and seeks meaningful connections and validation from peers and family, often contemplating the significance of achievements and future aspirations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
A experiences symptoms of existential anxiety and low self-worth, often linked to comparisons with peers and the pursuit of perfection. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
A feels lost, disheartened, and questions the meaning of life despite achieving good grades and receiving positive feedback from others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that A struggles with automatic negative thoughts and experiences feelings of hopelessness, yet shows openness to exploring and challenging these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help A identify and counter negative beliefs about self-worth and progress. Initiate exercises focusing on recognizing personal strengths and achievements to foster a balanced self-view.
