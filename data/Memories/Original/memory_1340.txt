# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who has been experiencing stress and feelings of embarrassment due to a persistent nail-biting habit. This habit is exacerbated by anxiety related to schoolwork and perceived judgment from peers. The user is seeking assistance to gain control over this behavior.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder but experiences anxiety and symptoms of stress linked to the nail-biting habit.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and embarrassed due to the nail-biting habit, which seems to be triggered by stress and fear of judgment from peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes that the user appears motivated to understand the triggers of their nail-biting habit and is willing to explore alternative perspectives and behaviors.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify and challenge the thought patterns leading to nail-biting. Develop alternative behaviors and strategies to manage stress and perceived judgment from peers.
