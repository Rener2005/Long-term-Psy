# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, known as A (pseudonym), is a student who recently took on a leadership role as class monitor. A faces anxiety and self-doubt, particularly from new responsibilities and balancing schoolwork. The user is keen on self-improvement and emotional regulation.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
A experiences symptoms consistent with anxiety and stress, particularly linked to responsibilities and self-imposed expectations, but has no formal diagnosis disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
A feels overwhelmed by the expectations and pressure associated with being a class monitor, leading to increased anxiety and self-doubt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that A is experiencing anxiety due to negative self-comparison and is hesitant about meeting expectations but demonstrates openness to explore and change perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help A challenge negative thoughts and develop more confidence in leadership. Introduce strategies for anxiety management and encourage participation in activities that promote stress relief.
