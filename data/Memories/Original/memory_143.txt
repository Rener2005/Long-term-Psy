# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who often feels anxious and isolated, particularly about studies and familial expectations. The user has faced a recent betrayal from a trusted individual, which adds to the sense of hopelessness.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, especially related to academic performance and familial relationships, though no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and isolated, perceiving academic endeavors as stuck despite efforts to improve.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a cycle of self-doubt and a tendency to see failure despite achievements, yet notes the user's openness to expressing emotions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to assist the user in articulating emotions and developing more supportive self-talk. Begin fostering strategies for emotional management and break down negative perceptions about achievement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels discouraged and less motivated to study due to harsh parental criticism and below-expected mid-term results.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user's motivation and confidence are significantly impacted by familial criticism, with procrastination becoming a common response.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user challenge negative self-perceptions and establish achievable study goals. Employ CBT techniques to reshape the association between criticism and self-worth, encouraging the acknowledgment of past successes.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences continued anxiety, particularly regarding academic performance and parental expectations, feeling overwhelmed by pressure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist detects a recurring theme of fear regarding disappointment, yet sees potential for self-directed goal-setting as a mechanism for relief.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in setting personal academic goals independent of external expectations, using CBT strategies to break goals into manageable tasks. Foster improvement-focused study habits with integrated reward systems.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated due to perceived lack of understanding from family despite attempts at communication about stress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist regards the user's willingness to address familial expectations but notes difficulty in being heard, suggesting the need for strengthened self-advocacy.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in practicing effective communication techniques and self-care strategies to manage stress. Use CBT approaches to assess and improve interaction dynamics with family.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences a mix of accomplishment from recent academic performance and anxiety about impending college entrance exams.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes an internal conflict between current achievements and future anticipations, with familial pressures exacerbating underlying anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement mindfulness exercises and affirmations to aid the user in staying present and reducing exam-related anxiety. Encourage reliance on social support networks to mitigate isolation during stress.
