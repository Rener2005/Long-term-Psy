# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Mr. Li is a professional facing significant anxiety related to work situations, particularly in collaborative settings. He struggles with expressing his ideas in meetings and often worries about being judged by colleagues. He is motivated to improve his communication skills and work through his anxieties.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Mr. Li experiences anxiety centered around professional performance, especially during collaborative interactions. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li feels anxious and overwhelmed with the increasing need for collaboration, fearing judgment and underappreciation of his ideas during meetings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Mr. Li has a deep-seated fear of negative evaluation from colleagues but is open to exploring strategies for improved self-confidence and communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Mr. Li recognize and challenge negative thought patterns, focusing on improving self-confidence and effective communication in meetings. Encourage practices such as positive self-talk and preparation strategies.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li reports slight improvement but continues to battle anxiety and self-doubt, especially during meetings, and finds it difficult to consistently apply learned techniques.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that while Mr. Li’s anxiety persists, his willingness to use deep breathing and self-reflection strategies indicates his commitment to change.

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance Mr. Li's use of cognitive restructuring techniques by identifying and challenging cognitive distortions. Strengthen confidence through positive affirmations and deliberate rehearsal of meeting contributions.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li continues to feel anxious ahead of meetings, struggling with pervasive doubts about his contributions' value despite preparation efforts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes persistent anxiety but acknowledges Mr. Li's increased awareness of thought mistakes and his efforts in self-preparation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on reinforcing Mr. Li's positive self-perception through deliberate practice and affirmations. Encourage journaling of experiences to support cognitive restructuring.

# Fourth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
While Mr. Li remains challenged by strong feelings of inadequacy, the therapist observes a consistent effort in working toward anxiety management.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Mr. Li in expanding his coping strategies, incorporating visual reminders of self-worth and role-playing exercises to increase confidence.

# Sixth Consultation
