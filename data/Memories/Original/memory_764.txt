# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who has been experiencing intense pressure from academic research, leading to increased self-doubt and difficulty focusing. They report low energy levels and sleep disturbances.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms consistent with anxiety, including trouble sleeping, overthinking, and fear of judgment, but no formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious due to the intense pressure of academic expectations, resulting in low energy and concentration issues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's tendency to engage in negative self-talk and catastrophizing but sees a willingness to adopt more positive self-reflection and challenge these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user recognize and challenge negative thought patterns, introduce journaling for tracking emotions, and encourage small steps toward seeking peer support.
