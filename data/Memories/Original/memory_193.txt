# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, referred to as Y, is a university student who recently started attending college. They struggle with social anxiety and feelings of inadequacy, especially in new social environments like university. They have a family background that includes a strong connection with grandparents.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Y experiences symptoms of social anxiety, predominantly characterized by fears of social judgment and self-doubt. No formal mental health disorder has been diagnosed or disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels lost and anxious about integrating socially in the university environment, which includes difficulties in bonding with roommates and anxiety in academic settings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Y shows patterns of negative self-perception during social interactions, such as assuming others are judging them, yet they demonstrate a willingness to explore and challenge these self-perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Y identify and challenge negative thoughts, develop social skills, and build confidence in interactions. Encourage engaging in social activities to foster better connections.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y continues to feel anxious and helpless in social settings, particularly feeling awkward and out of place during group activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Y’s negative beliefs about themselves in social settings are persistent but acknowledges the user’s openness to explore alternative thoughts and engage with peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Y in challenging distorted thinking patterns by gathering evidence and using alternative perspectives. Encourage gentle exposure to social activities for gradual confidence building.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
