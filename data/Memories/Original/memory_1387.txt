# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is an adult who has been experiencing anxiety related to safety concerns after a classmate's accident. These anxieties are exacerbating compulsive checking behaviors at home, causing distress and impacting their daily life.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of anxiety, heightened by recent traumatic events, leading to compulsive checking behaviors. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed and anxious about safety, leading to repeated checking behaviors at home, particularly after a traumatic event involving a classmate.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li is caught in a cycle of fear and checking behaviors but shows an understanding of the irrational nature of some thoughts and a willingness to explore alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Li in challenging and reframing anxiety-provoking thoughts and in finding alternative strategies to manage safety-related anxiety.
