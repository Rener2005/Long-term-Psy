# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang is employed in a professional setting and struggles with sensitivity to others’ opinions, especially after a disagreement with a colleague. Zhang is keen on improving interactions and self-confidence in social and workplace settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms of anxiety related to social interactions and negative feedback, though has not received a formal mental health disorder diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious and sensitive to peers' perceptions, leading to self-doubt and low self-esteem.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang's struggles with self-critical thought patterns and notes the impact of recent negative comments on Zhang's self-perception.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Zhang identify and challenge negative beliefs through CBT by using positive affirmations and exploring evidence of successes to bolster self-esteem. Teach grounding techniques to manage anxiety in social and professional environments.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels sensitive to perceived judgments and struggles with feelings of inadequacy after being indirectly labeled by a colleague.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang’s persistence in combating negative self-perception and highlights an underlying fear stemming from social labels.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Zhang in reframing thoughts when faced with negative labels, applying self-compassionate strategies, and using evidence-based thinking to counterbalance perceived judgments.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious and self-conscious during social gatherings, leading to feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes that Zhang is aware of the impact of self-doubt yet is making efforts to engage positively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce positive self-talk and cognitive restructuring strategies for Zhang to use in social interactions, focusing on self-acceptance and reducing anxiety about others’ opinions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang exhibits increased confidence in expressing opinions, though with some lingering concern about negative feedback.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress in Zhang's willingness to advocate for personal views and acknowledges the residual caution surrounding criticism.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Zhang’s use of affirmation and positive reinforcement techniques to continue building self-confidence, while also preparing for handling dissent without distress.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels more open to sharing feelings and experiences, though remains cautious of potential judgments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's gradual embrace of openness and recognizes ongoing concern about judgment, perceiving a shift towards more adaptive thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang to engage in activities that foster connection, utilizing self-reflection and journaling to track emotional progress and affirm positive social experiences.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels empowered and validated after meaningful conversations, finding new confidence in handling feedback.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Zhang's growth in managing external perceptions and using objective evaluation of feedback as a tool for self-betterment.

[Consultation Goals]
