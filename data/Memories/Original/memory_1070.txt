# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a young adult who experiences feelings of inadequacy and frustration, particularly influenced by recent events such as not gaining college admission while witnessing her twin sister's achievements. Li also struggles with a persistent skin condition that seems to be exacerbated by stress and emotional states.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of stress, anxiety, and low self-esteem. These issues seem to be intertwined with physical symptoms such as skin irritation, but there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels ignored and frustrated, with heightened irritation linked to stress and emotional turmoil due to recent setbacks and sibling comparisons.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that Li is caught in a cycle where emotional distress aggravates physical symptoms and vice versa, yet there is an openness to consider reframing negative thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li identify and challenge negative thought patterns, fostering self-compassion and realistic self-appraisal. Address the connection between stress and physical symptoms with practical coping strategies.
