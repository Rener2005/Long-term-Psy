# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing feelings of loneliness and difficulty connecting with peers in the dormitory. Wang struggles with finding common ground with others and often feels out of place in social settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang exhibits symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels lonely and isolated, especially in the dormitory environment, and expresses frustration about not fitting in with peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Wang's tendency to engage in negative self-comparison and assumptions about being unlikable, though there is a willingness to engage in social activities with planned strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang identify and challenge negative thoughts about social acceptance and encourage small, manageable steps towards building connections with peers.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang continues to struggle with feeling out of place during social activities, experiencing self-doubt about being accepted by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Wang’s improvement in self-awareness and openness to consider strategies for reducing anxiety in group settings, indicating a desire to overcome social barriers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Wang in setting small, achievable social goals and using positive self-talk to manage anxiety in social situations. Promote self-compassion to alleviate feelings of inadequacy.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Foster Wang's ability to identify and appreciate communication strengths, employing CBT techniques to counteract negativistic self-assessments and affirm the value of emerging social bonds.

# Fifth Consultation
