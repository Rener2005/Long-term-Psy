# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Graham is a seasoned actor who grapples with anxiety, particularly related to stage performances and fear of forgetting lines. Graham expresses a strong desire to tackle stage fright and improve confidence in both acting and improvisation.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Graham experiences significant performance-related anxiety and self-imposed pressure for perfection, although no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Graham feels anxious about upcoming performances, haunted by the fears of forgetting lines and being judged for imperfections.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Graham's anxiety is fueled by perfectionism and fear of external judgment, yet Graham is open to exploring more compassionate perspectives on mistakes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Graham challenge perfectionistic thoughts and develop a more balanced view of mistakes as opportunities for learning. Introduce relaxation techniques to manage performance anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Graham continues to struggle with anxiety, particularly after experiencing a challenging audition that impacted confidence levels.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Graham's self-doubt is exacerbated by high personal expectations, but Graham shows understanding of past successes and their relevance to current self-perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Graham to recall past positive performances to counteract negative thoughts, and teach relaxation and visualization techniques to manage pre-performance anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Graham remains anxious about an impending performance, with thoughts focusing on potential mistakes and negative audience reactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that while Graham harbors significant anxiety, there is an emerging ability to recognize that past worst-case scenarios have been unfounded.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote self-compassion and practice visualization techniques to enhance confidence, while using affirmations to replace negative self-talk.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Graham is conflicted between recognizing the overall success of a performance and focusing on minor errors that provoke anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Graham's internal struggle with self-criticism despite positive feedback, highlighting the challenge of managing perfectionistic tendencies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Graham to balance self-criticism with positive feedback, embrace imperfections, and utilize affirmations to reinforce self-compassion.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Graham feels generally more supported by the team, which has enhanced confidence, though pre-performance nerves remain.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that teamwork contributes to a reduction in solo performance pressure, yet Graham still experiences significant anxiety prior to taking the stage.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen pre-performance affirmations that utilize team support to calm nerves and focus on the shared experience, while reinforcing confidence in individual capabilities.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Graham is experiencing a newfound confidence in handling unexpected situations on stage, leading to a reduction in stage fright.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Graham's successful handling of a live performance challenge is indicative of significant personal growth and the effective integration of therapeutic techniques.
