# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li Ming is a young professional who experiences anxiety and feelings of disconnection, particularly related to work pressure and maintaining social interactions. He is proactive in seeking methods to better manage his stress and improve his mental well-being.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li Ming exhibits symptoms of anxiety, especially in relation to work and social situations, but does not have a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming feels overwhelmed and anxious due to work deadlines and a decrease in social interactions, leading to feelings of guilt about neglecting friends.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li Ming's struggle with balancing work demands and social life, along with a pattern of negative self-assessment and avoidance behaviors.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li Ming identify and challenge negative thinking patterns that contribute to anxiety. Establish strategies to re-engage socially and manage work-related stress.
