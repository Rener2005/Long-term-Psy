# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who struggles with anxiety related to speaking issues and social interactions. They feel overwhelmed in the new university environment and express a fear of judgment due to their stutter.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly in speaking and social situations, without disclosing any formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and alone, worrying about being judged in social situations due to their speaking difficulties.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's cycle of negative thoughts and assumptions but notes a willingness to explore more positive and compassionate perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts and develop more balanced perceptions. Encourage forming small, manageable social interactions to build confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel anxious and isolated, experiencing difficulty communicating with roommates and avoiding social activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes deeply rooted negative thoughts contributing to the user's anxiety, yet there is an openness to make small positive changes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in challenging skewed thought patterns and encourage engaging in simple social interactions. Foster a sense of self-compassion.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels trapped by anxiety, leading to a lack of participation in desired social activities, such as club joining.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes habitual negative thinking patterns but sees growth in the user's awareness of these thought distortions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to practice rehearsing speeches and positive affirmations to alleviate speaking anxiety. Reinforce the idea of growth through winning small social victories.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels increased anxiety regarding classroom presentations and continuously avoids speaking opportunities, heightening stress levels.

[Observational Impressions]  
The user's self-perception struggles against fear-based cognitive distortions, but there’s a willingness to address these distortions during conversations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower the user to adopt relaxation techniques and challenge negative thoughts surrounding presentations. Support gradual exposure to public speaking contexts.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a mix of nervousness and accomplishment after successfully completing a classroom presentation, marking it as a significant personal achievement.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a small increase in self-confidence and a shift towards a more positive mindset regarding public speaking.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to continue setting achievable speaking goals and challenge fear-based thinking with supportive alternatives. Focus on building resilience through repeated exposure.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels proud after participating in a speech competition, experiencing positive audience connection despite initial anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a shift in the user’s perception of public speaking from fear towards willingness, showing elements of self-reflection and courage.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to maintain a journal to track speaking progress and continue applying cognitive strategies to manage anxiety. Promote setting incremental speaking challenges for continual growth.
