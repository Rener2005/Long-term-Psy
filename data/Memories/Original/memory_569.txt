# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student experiencing feelings of shame and self-doubt, particularly related to past actions during a hospital stay. The user is working on rebuilding confidence and connecting with friends.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user shows symptoms of anxiety and self-esteem issues related to past experiences but has not reported any formal mental health diagnoses.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and burdened by shame regarding past actions, which impacts self-view and ability to connect with others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user is stuck in a cycle of negative self-perception tied to past events but is open to developing self-compassion and acknowledging progress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help the user challenge negative beliefs about being "broken." Develop self-compassion through affirmations and encourage reconnecting with peers as a step toward healing.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to struggle with self-acceptance due to persistent guilt over past actions, seeking forgiveness and freedom from this self-imposed burden.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's ongoing challenge with guilt, which hampers self-acceptance, but recognizes the user's willingness to explore narratives of self-forgiveness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in identifying skewed perceptions of guilt by presenting evidence to counteract negative thoughts. Reinforce self-worth through more frequent self-affirmations and integrate positive social interactions.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences fluctuating emotions, characterized by feelings of shame and doubt about accomplishments when reflecting on past mistakes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that while the user struggles with negative self-evaluation during social interactions, there's a potential for growth in self-awareness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen the user’s self-esteem by identifying and celebrating personal strengths. Address automatic negative thoughts with supportive evidence, fostering more realistic self-perceptions through CBT methods.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user strives to maintain a positive outlook but feels overwhelmed by self-comparisons, impacting perceptions of capability and progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees the user's inclination to withdraw when anxious but notes a readiness to adopt a more forgiving self-pace and seek social support.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in setting manageable goals to enhance academic focus, using CBT to counteract detrimental comparisons. Encourage participation in supportive networks to reinforce progress and self-acceptance.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a mix of optimism and fear about engaging in new social activities, resulting from previous positive group experiences.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices the user's wariness about regressing into isolation but also recognizes emerging excitement about social engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the user’s exploration of new social opportunities by challenging fears and reinforcing positive outlooks. Develop strategies for consistent engagement in community activities to sustain confidence growth.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more in control of emotions but has occasional doubts about future academic success due to peer comparisons.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives a growing ability in the user to manage emotional responses but acknowledges the impact of academic pressures and self-doubt.
