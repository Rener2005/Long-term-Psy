# First Consultation

## User Background

[Personal Profile]  
Yuma is a young child who enjoys playing with train toys and other interactive playthings. He finds comfort in familiar objects and often seeks quiet environments to engage with them. Recently, he has been exploring ways to express himself more around others, especially during playtime.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yuma has not been formally diagnosed with any specific mental health disorder, but he demonstrates traits verging on social anxiety and mild communication challenges.

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
Yuma is focused on his train toys and finds joy in watching them move, though he appears indifferent towards exploring other activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Yuma's preference for routine and familiarity. His interactions are limited to discussing topics he feels comfortable with, like his train toys, indicating a potential challenge in social expansion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yuma explore and express his feelings in a supportive environment. Gradually introduce alternative activities to broaden his scope of interest and social engagement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
Yuma appears curious and open to engaging with new toys, responding positively to their features, such as spinning and making noise.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Yuma's tentative yet growing willingness to try new toys, which indicates flexibility and a potential readiness to explore beyond his primary interests.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Yuma to continue experimenting with diverse toys to foster adaptive play habits using CBT principles. Support him in expressing feelings of excitement and preference through these interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
Yuma is interested in expressing himself through play, such as block building, and finds comfort in using visual cues like pictures to convey feelings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress in Yuma's comfort within the session setting as he uses creative play to convey thoughts, suggesting growing confidence in self-expression.

[Consultation Goals]  
Develop Yuma's ability to express emotions and thoughts through creative means like art and play in line with CBT approaches. Encourage storytelling and structured play to build a narrative framework for emotional expression.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Mental State]  
Yuma finds himself wanting to engage in group activities but struggles with initiating conversation, leading to feelings of distance despite a desire for participation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes Yuma’s openness toward play and social engagement, albeit hindered by a lack of communicative initiation, highlighting a path to foster assertiveness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Equip Yuma with simple communication strategies to build confidence in social settings. Use CBT methods to plan and practice small phrases for engaging others during play.
