# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu is a university student facing the pressure of graduating and making significant career decisions, particularly concerning pursuing a master's degree or entering the job market. Liu feels uncertain and seeks guidance to navigate these choices.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu experiences symptoms of anxiety and fears related to decision-making and self-doubt, but there’s no mention of a formal diagnosis for a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memories

### First Consultation
[Mental State]  
Liu feels stressed and anxious, particularly overwhelmed by the decisions regarding higher education or starting a career.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Liu's apprehension linked to past decision-making regrets, yet Liu demonstrates a willingness to engage with alternative strategies and perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Liu in examining and challenging negative beliefs, fostering greater self-assurance in decision-making. Encourage a step-by-step approach to gather information and lessen anxiety.

### Second Consultation
[Mental State]  
Liu feels overwhelmed by anxiety related to future academic and career choices and is concerned about meeting personal and family expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Liu's struggle with self-imposed pressure and high expectations, but Liu is receptive to reframing negative beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Liu in reframing anxious thoughts and reinforcing strengths. Encourage practical steps to prepare for applications and alleviate anxiety toward future decisions.

### Third Consultation
[Mental State]  
Liu is experiencing a mixture of clarity and pressure, feeling overwhelmed despite gaining insights from recent job lectures.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges Liu’s discovery of useful information yet recognizes the added burden of fear in decision-making.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Liu to view career choices as explorative steps rather than definitive end points. Promote listing options and pros/cons to alleviate anxiety and propose healthier coping strategies.

### Fourth Consultation
[Mental State]  
Liu feels more relaxed and introduces new perspectives but remains pressured about career choices due to familial financial concerns.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist appreciates Liu's openness to exploring expanded career options, mindful of the family’s expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Liu to leverage new insights in decision-making and initiate supportive dialogues with peers to share experiences and alleviate pressure.

### Fifth Consultation
[Mental State]  
Liu feels more optimistic after participating in group activities and realizing shared decision-making challenges among peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Liu's boosted confidence and connection from group interactions, alongside emerging self-assurance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Liu’s participation in supportive dialogues to further confidence in decision-making. Promote compiling and discussing pros and cons with the peers for additional perspectives.

### Sixth Consultation
[Mental State]  
Liu feels confident and positive due to progress in handling internship preparations and job applications, with reduced uncertainty about future choices.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Liu's growth in confidence through successful internship experiences and positive feedback, facilitating proactive engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Liu's continued growth by setting small, achievable goals and maintaining a balance between studies and internship responsibilities. Encourage journaling and sharing experiences for further development.
