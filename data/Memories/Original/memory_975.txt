# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who expresses feelings of anxiety and inadequacy, particularly around academic performance and interactions with teachers. Recently, the user is concerned about returning to school and facing exams, fearing criticism and failure.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety related to academic pressure and negative self-perception, but there's no indication of a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and paralyzed at the thought of returning to school and facing potential failure and criticism from teachers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency to focus on potential failure and verbalize self-doubt, yet notes the user's ability to articulate compassionate advice that they struggle to apply to themselves.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify and challenge negative thoughts, promoting self-kindness and realistic self-appraisal to increase motivation and reduce anxiety. 

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to struggle with feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, particularly when studying, and experiences a cycle of avoidance due to overwhelming thoughts of failure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's awareness of the impact of negative thoughts on behavior and the willingness to explore and challenge these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in systematically challenging negative thoughts with evidence-based perspectives, encourage the use of a journal to reinforce positive moments, and establish small, achievable study goals to foster a sense of accomplishment.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels trapped in a cycle of anxiety and self-doubt, particularly after attempting practice exams, fearing disappointment and parental expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's internalized parental expectations and the paralyzing effect of these on the user's emotional state and performance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce the user to calming techniques such as deep breathing, encourage engagement in social situations to reduce isolation, and reinforce the application of compassionate self-talk in high-pressure situations.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains anxious about social interactions, feeling out of place with classmates and fearing embarrassment, yet shows a desire to connect.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's automatic negative thoughts concerning social acceptance, but sees openness to practicing and improving social interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to gradually engage in social activities, using positive self-talk and deep breathing to manage anxiety. Support the user in recognizing and challenging unfounded negative beliefs about social acceptance.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences a blend of joy from school activities and anxiety about future challenges, particularly fear of criticism and perceived inadequacies during assessments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s growing awareness of the positive moments and suggests a residual fear of criticism affecting self-perception.

[Consultation Goals]  
Work on reinforcing positive experiences and self-compassion with strategies that accept imperfection. Role-play scenarios to prepare for evaluative situations, enhancing coping skills and reducing anticipatory anxiety.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about upcoming exams, wanting to replace feelings of inevitable failure with confidence in their abilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives internalized harsh judgments and an avoidance pattern linked to academic pressure, yet notes the user's willingness to replace negative beliefs with evidence-based self-assurance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Further cultivate the user's ability to challenge negative thoughts and set realistic, structured study schedules. Encourage relaxation activities post-study, and enhance user’s supportive network through social connections.
