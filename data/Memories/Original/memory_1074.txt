# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is a concerned parent of a young daughter who is demonstrating withdrawn behavior and a preference for repetitive activities over social interaction. Li is worried about her daughter's social development and is seeking strategies to support her in becoming more socially engaged.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li's daughter exhibits behaviors that suggest potential difficulty in social interaction, though no formal diagnosis has been mentioned. Li's concerns highlight a desire to understand and support her daughter's developmental needs.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels confused and worried about her daughter's tendency to isolate herself and engage in repetitive behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's keen observation of her daughter's behavior and her openness to understanding and addressing these concerns with supportive strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to explore how Li can facilitate her daughter's social engagement by respecting her pace and gradually encouraging interaction with peers through manageable and familiar activities.
