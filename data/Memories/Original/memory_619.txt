# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young individual grappling with family and peer conflicts, resulting in anger and feelings of being misunderstood. The user has expressed desires for autonomy, especially regarding personal space and phone usage, and is reflecting on the impact of their actions towards others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences challenges in managing anger and communication, particularly in familial and social interactions. They show a pattern of expressing anger aggressively, which affects relationships.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels angry and restricted by parental controls, particularly concerning phone usage, and desires more freedom.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency to react impulsively when feeling controlled, but also notes a willingness to improve communication methods with their mother.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user develop better anger management techniques and communication skills using CBT, focusing on expressing needs calmly and recognizing triggers for anger escalation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels regret over aggressive actions at school and reports feeling disrespected by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's recognition of inappropriate reactions and their desire to find non-violent ways to assert themselves.

[Consultation Goals]  
Practice alternative responses to conflict and role-playing scenarios to improve assertiveness and reduce reliance on physical aggression. Reinforce the importance of self-awareness in stressful situations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and pressured by parental expectations regarding academic performance, leading to feelings of inadequacy and avoidance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the impact of the user's negative self-talk on their motivation and ability to study, alongside a growing awareness of this cycle.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce time management strategies to break down academic tasks into manageable parts and utilize relaxation techniques, like deep breathing, to manage anxiety.

# Fourth Consultation

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
