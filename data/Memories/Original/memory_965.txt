# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a university student who struggles with obsessive thoughts surrounding cleanliness and germs, leading to anxiety and compulsive behaviors. Zhang is also dealing with pressures from academic responsibilities and social interactions, and is exploring strategies to manage these challenges.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang has symptoms indicative of Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) characterized by excessive cleanliness and contamination fears, though there is no formal diagnosis mentioned.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels anxious about cleanliness and germs, resulting in compulsive checking behaviors that interfere with daily life and academic focus.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang exhibits heightened anxiety in shared spaces and struggles with extreme thoughts regarding cleanliness, but shows openness to exploring cognitive strategies to manage these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang identify and challenge distorted thoughts related to cleanliness. Introduce relaxation exercises to mitigate anxiety and develop a more balanced perspective on cleanliness and contamination.
