# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang is a student experiencing overwhelming pressure from school, particularly with focusing on studies and managing expectations related to academic performance. Recent exam results have heightened feelings of inadequacy and anxiety about future academic prospects.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang experiences symptoms of anxiety and self-doubt but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels overwhelmed and anxious, struggling with chaotic thought patterns and a pervasive sense of inadequacy after recent academic evaluations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wang displays significant negative self-talk and hesitancy in taking breaks, indicating potential unawareness of the benefits of self-care balanced with study.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang create structured study routines, including breaks to enhance focus and productivity. Introduce relaxation exercises, like breathing techniques, to manage anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang continues to experience anxiety and self-doubt, exacerbated by the pressure to perform academically and falling behind peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Wang's tendency to interpret academic struggles as personal inadequacy, yet Wang shows willingness to explore and attempt to implement new strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Empower Wang to break tasks into manageable portions and recognize personal achievements, while reinforcing the use of positive affirmations to combat negative self-perceptions.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels anxious about academic performance, wrestling with fears of failure and their implications for the future.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Wang's entrenched belief linking self-worth with academic success, yet observes a readiness to explore alternative perspectives about self-value.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Wang to challenge the perceived relationship between self-worth and academic achievement, establishing smaller, realistic goals to reduce anxiety and rebuild confidence.
