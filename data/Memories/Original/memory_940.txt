# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Ding is a student who recently discovered his father's affair, which has disrupted his motivation and emotional stability. He struggles with feelings of abandonment and distrust concerning his family. Ding describes himself as previously engaged in academics but now finds it challenging to focus due to these personal issues. 

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Ding is experiencing symptoms of anxiety and depression linked to his family's situation. He has not disclosed any formal diagnosis but exhibits emotional distress and diminished motivation.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ding feels lost and apathetic, struggling to care about his studies due to the emotional impact of his family issues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Ding's pervasive sense of abandonment and distrust, compounded by his current academic disengagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy techniques to help Ding disentangle his identity and self-worth from his family's crisis, encouraging him to establish small, achievable academic goals to regain motivation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ding experiences disappointment and frustration after attempting to communicate with his parents, feeling ignored and misunderstood.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Ding's struggle with feeling dismissed and the resultant erosion of trust in family relationships.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Ding in expressing his feelings constructively to his parents and reinforce his recognition of personal value beyond family validation.

# Third Consultation
