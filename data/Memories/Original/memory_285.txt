# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student who has been feeling isolated and lonely since starting college. Li initially got along with classmates but now struggles to fit in, feeling left out and losing hope about social connections.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences feelings of loneliness and isolation but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels isolated and invisible in social settings, carrying negative thoughts about not being interesting enough.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li is self-critical and feels excluded but is open to reframing thoughts and considering kinder perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to challenge Li's negative beliefs and build a healthier self-view. Encourage small steps in joining classmate activities to foster social engagement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels anxious and confused about social interactions, doubting the ability to connect with classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's uncertainty in social settings but a willingness to explore strategies for building confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Li recognize and challenge negative self-thoughts using evidence and positive reframes. Encourage participation in smaller group settings to enhance connection.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li experiences both satisfaction from friendships and anxiety in new social situations, worried about embarrassment and acceptance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's progress in socializing but notes persistent fears of judgment and rejection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Li's confidence by focusing on past positive social experiences, using CBT to redirect anxious thoughts during interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels positive about new friendships but worries about sustaining them long-term, fearing a lack of interesting contributions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees increased social involvement but notes ongoing concerns about maintaining connections and self-interest.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li to share personal interests and initiate conversations based on mutual topics, reinforcing confidence in social roles.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is gaining a sense of belonging and support from new friendships but continues to feel pressure from social comparisons.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's growing comfort and self-expression in social contexts, balanced by occasional self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Li embrace unique qualities and reinforce self-worth, using activities that highlight personal interests for deeper connection.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels empowered by recent social initiatives and personal growth, experiencing some residual avoidance due to fear of awkwardness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's significant progress in initiating social interactions while recognizing lingering apprehension.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage incremental exposure to social scenarios to further alleviate avoidance, using prepared strategies for engaging conversations.
