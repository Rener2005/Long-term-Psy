# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Mr. Li is an adult male who has recently stopped consuming alcohol and is primarily concerned with insomnia and anxiety over sleep. He is motivated to improve his sleep without the use of medication and seeks techniques to help reduce anxiety related to sleep disturbances.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Mr. Li is experiencing anxiety connected to sleep issues and dependence on alcohol for sleep but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li feels anxious and terrified about his inability to sleep after ceasing alcohol consumption. He is worried about losing control and the impact of lack of sleep on daily functioning.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Mr. Li's dependence on alcohol as a crutch for sleep and notes his anxiety appears to intensify his perception of lack of control. Despite this, Mr. Li shows openness to exploring alternative relaxation strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Mr. Li reframe negative beliefs about sleep and explore non-alcoholic relaxation methods. Assist him in developing a new bedtime routine to support sleep without alcohol.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li remains anxious and skeptical about ceasing alcohol use and fears he cannot sleep without it. He struggles with insomnia and feels stressed due to a lack of rest.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Mr. Li’s heightened anxiety and panic about sobriety and sleep. There's an acknowledgment of his historical reliance on alcohol, yet an emerging willingness to consider new methodologies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Mr. Li challenge his fear of insomnia through cognitive restructuring and support exploration of relaxation skills by integrating breathing exercises and other calming activities into his routine.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li experiences skepticism about the efficacy of relaxation strategies and worries about insomnia returning, clouding his progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Mr. Li is concerned about setbacks with insomnia but recognizes his instances of success, indicating self-awareness and a gradual adaptation to new strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Mr. Li’s resilience against setbacks by teaching him how to reframe waking episodes as normal occurrences and support the practice of affirmations for boosting confidence and calmness.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li feels relaxed after yoga sessions but struggles with meditation due to intrusive thoughts about responsibilities and productivity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Mr. Li’s efforts to engage in yoga and meditation, appreciating his attempts to relax amidst challenges, while also recognizing the struggles with an overly active mind during meditation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Mr. Li to continue with short, manageable meditation sessions combined with evening yoga to enhance relaxation before sleep. Recommend establishing a routine to optimize these practices.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li feels a mixture of improvement and anxiety regarding his insomnia. He notices progress in falling asleep but is challenged by waking during the night with anxious thoughts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges Mr. Li’s progress and notes his ongoing concerns over middle-of-the-night awakenings. There is continued effort in adapting to relaxation techniques despite difficulties focusing.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Mr. Li in developing a structured evening plan incorporating body scan techniques with breathing exercises when waking mid-night. Emphasize the importance of a consistent approach to reduce anxiety.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Li feels more confident and proactive about his sleep, reporting an improved ability to sleep without alcohol and recognizing personal progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Mr. Li’s boosted self-assurance and acknowledges his evolution from dependence on alcohol to implementing consistent sleep-enhancing practices.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce Mr. Li’s commitment to positive sleep routines by minimizing screen use before bed and regular reflection on achievements, maintaining momentum in his progress for long-term benefits.
