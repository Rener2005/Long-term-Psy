# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Z (pseudonym) is a competitive athlete preparing for the World Military Games. Z is experiencing anxiety related to performance expectations and struggles with insomnia due to the pressure of the upcoming event.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Z exhibits symptoms of performance anxiety and occasional insomnia but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Z feels anxious and struggles with sleep due to the pressure of the upcoming competition, compounded by fears of underperforming and potential disappointment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Z is highly self-critical, demonstrating a pattern of negative thinking, particularly in response to perceived performance expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Z manage performance anxiety and improve sleep by reframing negative thoughts and establishing a relaxation routine.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Z feels overwhelmed and anxious, with feelings of isolation due to an intense training schedule and persistent worry about performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Z's tendency to deprioritize personal relationships in favor of training, contributing to feelings of isolation. There is openness to minor adjustments in habits for better emotional balance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Z to reach out for social support and implement small changes to enhance emotional well-being. Promote the use of brief relaxation techniques to manage anxiety and improve sleep.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Z’s anxiety is exacerbated by the injury, leading to negative forecasting and diminished motivation, yet Z has awareness of their past resilience and support systems.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Z in recognizing and challenging negative projections using evidence from past recoveries. Encourage the practice of relaxation techniques and goal-setting to maintain motivation.

[Consultation Goals]
