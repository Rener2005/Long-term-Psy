# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, who remains unnamed, is an individual dealing with the aftermath of a mistake made at work, which has led to significant anxiety and self-doubt. They have expressed concerns about their ability to meet job expectations, and this has exacerbated sleep difficulties and concentration issues.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem related to professional performance but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and burdened by a recent mistake at work, resulting in persistent self-doubt and preoccupation with potential further errors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user is caught in a cycle of self-criticism and fear, but there is a readiness to explore coping strategies and recognize personal achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user identify and challenge negative thought patterns, encouraging a balanced view of work experiences. Introduce journaling to document successes and use task-oriented time management to reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains anxious about work performance and expresses fear of repeating previous mistakes, despite a history of positive feedback.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s difficulty in accepting past achievements due to a persistent focus on recent failures, yet there’s an openness to self-compassionate reframing of these experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in reinforcing positive self-perceptions by recalling past successes to counterbalance current anxieties. Encourage small steps towards self-compassion and obtaining constructive feedback regularly.
