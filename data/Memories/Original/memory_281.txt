# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Li is a dedicated student facing pressure primarily from academic responsibilities. He strives to manage study stress while exploring involvement in extracurricular activities to maintain balance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Li experiences symptoms of academic-related anxiety but has not been diagnosed with any specific mental health disorders. He expresses a strong desire to improve his time management and study habits.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li feels overwhelmed by the volume of material to cover for exams, coupled with a fear of not meeting expectations, leading to persistent anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Xiao Li holds high expectations from himself and perceives familial pressure, which exacerbates his anxiety. However, Xiao Li shows eagerness to adopt new perspectives on self-kindness and realistic goal setting.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Li establish realistic study goals and challenge perfectionist thoughts. Encourage small, manageable steps in study routines and the use of relaxation techniques to alleviate anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li reports moderate improvement but struggles with time management and balancing study with relaxation, feeling overwhelmed at times.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Li tends to compare himself with peers, affecting his self-esteem and increasing stress levels. However, he is open to exploring more balanced perspectives and self-compassion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Xiao Li in identifying and implementing effective time management strategies and cognitive restructuring techniques. Promote self-compassion and encourage the establishment of realistic study and relaxation schedules.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li feels stressed and his confidence wanes after experiencing perceived academic setbacks, leading to doubts about his abilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Xiao Li is affected by negative automatic thoughts and experiences cycles of self-doubt. The awareness of cognitive distortions is growing, alongside a willingness to use past successes as learning opportunities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Li in applying cognitive behavioral strategies to challenge cognitive distortions and set structured study routines. Encourage acknowledgment of personal achievements to strengthen self-worth and reduce anxiety.

# Fourth Consultation

[Consultation Goals]

# Fifth Consultation
