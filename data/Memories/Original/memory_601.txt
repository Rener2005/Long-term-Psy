# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Little H (pseudonym) is a school-aged child who frequently feels ignored and isolated by classmates. He often acts out to gain their attention but struggles with the repercussions. He desires friendship and acceptance from peers.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Little H showcases signs of social anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly tied to peer interactions. No formal mental health diagnosis has been documented.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Little H feels angry and upset due to perceived neglect from classmates, leading to actions driven by frustration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Little H's attempt to gain attention through negative behaviors as a sign of underlying loneliness and need for acceptance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Little H explore healthier ways of expressing feelings and initiating peer interactions. Practice strategies to improve self-esteem and communication skills.
