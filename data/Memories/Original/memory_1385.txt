# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is a designer struggling with work-related stress and anxiety. He experiences feelings of inadequacy and often second-guesses his abilities, stemming partly from past negative experiences in therapy. Li is seeking ways to manage these emotions better and improve his self-confidence.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li suffers from symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels depressed and anxious due to work pressures, experiences self-doubt, and fears judgment and failure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li struggles with performance pressure and negative self-perception, yet shows openness to exploring new perspectives and coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li reframe negative thoughts, acknowledge strengths, and incorporate stress-relief activities like painting into his routine.
