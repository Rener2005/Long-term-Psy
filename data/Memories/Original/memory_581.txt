# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Ms. Li (pseudonym) is a mother who is concerned about her daughter’s social anxiety, particularly in kindergarten settings. She has experienced postpartum depression in the past and carries a sense of guilt over how it might have influenced her daughter's behavior.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Ms. Li does not disclose any formal diagnosis for her daughter but describes symptoms of social anxiety. She herself is dealing with feelings of guilt and worry.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ms. Li feels worried and helpless about her daughter's social anxiety, fearing her own past emotional struggles may have contributed to it.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Ms. Li's strong sense of guilt and concern for her daughter's social development, alongside her willingness to explore supportive strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Ms. Li to use cognitive behavioral techniques to manage guilt and support her daughter during moments of anxiety. Foster strategies to discuss emotions with her daughter and gradually introduce her to social interactions.
