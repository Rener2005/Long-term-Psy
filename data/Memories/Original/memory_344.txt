# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li Hua is a teacher who feels anxious about meeting expectations in her teaching role at the beginning of a new semester. She is committed to her work but struggles with feelings of inadequacy and fears of not performing up to standards.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li Hua experiences symptoms of anxiety related to her professional responsibilities and has no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Hua feels anxious and overwhelmed about her teaching workload, fearing she might not meet her students' expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li Hua is caught in a cycle of self-doubt stemming from self-comparison and a fear of failure, but she is receptive to strategies that could help manage these feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li Hua identify and counter negative thoughts and improve time management skills to alleviate feelings of inadequacy. Encourage focus on teaching strengths and effectiveness.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Hua continues to feel anxious and dissatisfied with her teaching performance due to time management pressures.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Li Hua's procrastination exacerbates her anxiety, yet she is motivated to improve her scheduling and task management.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li Hua in creating a more structured schedule to manage workload effectively and reduce anxiety. Introduce relaxation techniques to cope with stress and reinforce planning skills.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Hua feels a bit better about processing negative feedback but still struggles with self-doubt and fears of inadequacy as a teacher.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li Hua is open to reevaluating student feedback and shows a willingness to consider alternative interpretations of her teaching capabilities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Li Hua to seek out and reflect on positive feedback alongside criticism, fostering a more balanced view of her teaching abilities. Reinforce the use of positive self-talk techniques to build confidence.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes progress in Li Hua's self-awareness and acceptance of personal strengths, though anxiety persists about performance and peer comparison.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Li Hua in setting small, achievable goals for new teaching techniques. Encourage reflective journaling and self-affirmation strategies to promote self-efficacy and reduce performance anxiety.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
