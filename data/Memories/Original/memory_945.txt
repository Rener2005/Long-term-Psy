# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Chen is a university student experiencing overwhelming anxiety and feelings of inadequacy, primarily connected to academic performance and social interactions. Recently, Chen has encountered challenges during a group presentation, which exacerbated self-doubt and a sense of exclusion.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Chen exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, reflecting a pattern of negative self-assessment. No formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Chen feels overwhelmed and anxious, continually struggling with thoughts of not being good enough compared to peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a tendency in Chen to internalize negative comparisons, leading to cycles of anxiety and self-doubt, though there's a readiness to consider more positive self-viewing methods.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help Chen identify and challenge negative automatic thoughts, fostering a compassionate self-perception. Introduce techniques such as deep breathing to manage anxiety effectively.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Aid Chen in distinguishing between unfounded fears and facts within social contexts. Enhance feelings of self-worth through gradual social exposure and self-soothing strategies, including deep breathing.
