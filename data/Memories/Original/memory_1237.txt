# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a university student, feels inadequate, particularly in academic settings and in integrating into university life. They are reflective about past successes and seek to manage emotional responses to academic and social pressures better.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, although no formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious about academic performance, particularly after failing an exam, which exacerbates feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's tendency toward negative self-comparison and feelings of doubt but notes a willingness to revisit past successes for perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to challenge negative self-assessments and develop a more balanced view of personal abilities. Encourage the use of positive affirmations to foster self-esteem.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences intense anxiety concerning group presentations, leading to avoidance of participation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes excessive worry about judgments and intense pressure to perform well, but recognizes the user's openness to gradual exposure and new coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply CBT methods to gradually reduce avoidance behavior and build confidence through low-pressure social interactions and affirmations. Use anxiety-reducing techniques like visualization and breathing exercises.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and doubtful during social activities, hampered by self-comparison and negative self-talk.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that habitual negative self-assessments undermine attempts to engage socially; however, the user shows awareness of this pattern.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to document positive interactions and successes in a journal. Use CBT to challenge self-limiting comparisons and foster self-kindness and confidence in social settings.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels slightly better due to improved study skills but still struggles with self-confidence in comparison to peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees improvement in self-awareness yet notes that comparisons continue to negatively impact motivation and self-esteem.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in recognizing personal growth and accomplishments. Use smaller, supportive group settings to practice voicing opinions and gradually build speaking confidence.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels increased confidence in communication but still grapples with anxiety during conflicts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist identifies progress in effective communication and recognizes ongoing anxiety during disagreements but notes user's openness to practicing scenarios and mindfulness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Refine assertive communication skills using CBT techniques and practice mindfulness exercises to manage anxiety during conflicts. Encourage consistent application in real-life situations.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a surge in self-confidence due to academic achievements and is more willing to contribute in group projects.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes significant strides in self-recognition and pride in accomplishments, reflecting positively on social engagement and stress management.

[Consultation Goals]  
Emphasize leadership development and further reinforce self-confidence through incremental responsibilities and public speaking practice. Strengthen support networks for continued growth.
