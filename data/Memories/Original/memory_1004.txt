# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, referred to as L, is a student experiencing feelings of overwhelm and procrastination due to academic pressures and deadlines. L describes conflicts with their mother, which contribute to their stress.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
L exhibits symptoms of anxiety and high levels of self-imposed perfectionism but has not reported any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
L feels overwhelmed by academic tasks and procrastinates, which leads to feelings of guilt and anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that L experiences a cycle of procrastination fueled by a perfectionistic mindset, yet shows openness to practical strategies for improving task management.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help L break down tasks into smaller parts and challenge perfectionistic thoughts, while fostering a mindset that values progress over perfection.
