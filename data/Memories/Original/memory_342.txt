# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Daniel is a student who experiences anxiety primarily in social and academic settings. Recent improvements include better behavior at school and an improved relationship with his mother. He is focused on developing confidence and reducing anxiety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Daniel exhibits symptoms consistent with social anxiety but has no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Daniel feels anxious, particularly in classroom settings, worrying about making mistakes and others’ perceptions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Daniel’s tendency towards self-doubt and performance anxiety but also sees a willingness to engage in strategies to build confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Daniel challenge negative assumptions and develop skills for classroom confidence. Introduce methods for reinforcing learning and managing real-time anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Daniel feels generally good but remains anxious about interacting with new people and making mistakes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that while Daniel has seen positive reinforcement in social activities, his fear of negative judgment persists, suggesting an opportunity for deeper cognitive restructuring.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Daniel to reframe worst-case scenarios through evidence-based thinking. Support self-compassion, focusing on gradual improvement in social confidence.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Daniel’s use of deep breathing techniques to manage anxiety symptoms. Support Daniel in identifying and celebrating incremental successes in social and public situations.
