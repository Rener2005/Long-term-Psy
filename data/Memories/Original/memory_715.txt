# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiaoli (pseudonym) is a young student experiencing difficulties with social interactions in school, feeling isolated, sad, and frustrated because classmates do not engage in play with them. Xiaoli seeks to manage these emotional reactions effectively and navigate new social settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiaoli shows signs of social anxiety and difficulty in emotional regulation in social contexts but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiaoli feels sad and angry because they find it challenging to make friends in a new school environment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiaoli's struggle with expressing feelings and initiating social interactions but notes a willingness to adopt new strategies for self-expression and social engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiaoli articulate feelings constructively and practice self-calming strategies like deep breathing. Foster approaches for initiating peer interaction.

# Second Consultation

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Xiaoli in exploring alternative interpretations of classmates' and teachers' actions, focus on verbalizing needs, and use breathing exercises to manage emotional overwhelm.
