# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student who recently transferred to a new school and is struggling with feelings of loneliness and isolation. They are facing challenges in adapting to the new environment, particularly in forming relationships with roommates, which affects their mood and sleep. 

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and disconnected, with significant anxiety about social integration at the new school.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with self-doubt and assumptions of negative social perceptions, yet shows willingness to reframe thoughts and attempt new social engagements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge assumptions about social rejection and encourage practical steps towards social engagement and improved sleep hygiene.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel anxious and experiences insomnia, expressing doubts about their choice of study, leading to self-questioning.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's negative thoughts are linked to academic pressures, with a tendency to overgeneralize potential failure, but there’s readiness to explore coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in disaggregating overwhelming study tasks into manageable goals, and apply CBT to challenge catastrophic thinking. Introduce healthier bedtime routines to address insomnia.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about participating in social activities due to language barriers, though there is a slight improvement in their overall mood.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s ongoing struggle with negative judgment anxiety in social contexts but recognizes growing awareness of the lack of evidence for these judgments.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage self-compassion and reframing of negative thoughts, using CBT techniques. Facilitate active participation in social activities through preparation and role-playing.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is more aware of their emotions and uses deep breathing techniques for relaxation, but struggles with anxious thoughts about schoolwork and self-assessment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees progress in emotion regulation through relaxation techniques, but identifies persistent self-doubt affecting concentration and focus.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help the user integrate relaxation practices with positive affirmations and reframed self-assessment to cultivate a balanced approach to emotional regulation and academic focus.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more confident and supported after forming friendships at school, experiencing improved emotional well-being and stress management.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s increasing openness and confidence, benefiting from social support, but recognizes a need to further develop communication skills for deeper connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Build on existing social skills by practicing open communication and emotional expression, reinforcing the value of social support in stress management.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels organized and positive about a new study plan, yet experiences occasional doubts about its effectiveness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user’s progress in organizational skills and stress management, but identifies a need for refined focus and goal-setting strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance study strategies using goal-oriented planning and self-assessment, supported by relaxation techniques, to build confidence in academic pursuits and maintain emotional balance.
