# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a university student experiencing feelings of anxiety and pressure, particularly related to academic performance and social interactions. They have expressed a desire to improve their communication skills and manage social anxiety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of social anxiety, primarily linked to fear of judgment and the pressure to perform well in social and academic contexts. There is no disclosed formal diagnosis of any mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed with anxiety and frustration, primarily due to challenges in communication and social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's cycle of self-doubt and anxiety, with a tendency to avoid speaking. However, there is a willingness to explore new approaches to manage social anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts and improve communication skills. Set small goals for participating in social and academic settings to gradually reduce anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel anxious about speaking up in class, experiencing self-consciousness and fear of judgment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's pervasive negative self-assessment and fear of public speaking, but acknowledges the user's insight into these patterns and willingness to address them.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in reframing negative thought patterns by focusing on evidence and self-compassion. Encourage small, manageable steps toward participation in safer group settings.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and embarrassed about seeking help from classmates, leading to avoidance of social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's tendency towards avoidance due to fear of judgment, but recognizes the user's openness to explore self-perception and communication challenges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to confront avoidance behaviors by initiating small conversations, using positive affirmations and rehearsing communication in low-pressure environments.

# Fourth Consultation

[Consultation Goals]

# Fifth Consultation

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage continued practice of communication skills in low-risk settings. Support the user in developing confidence-building strategies and self-kindness.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]
