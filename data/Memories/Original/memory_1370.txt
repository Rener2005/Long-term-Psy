# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zheng is an individual experiencing feelings of anxiety and unease, which have been exacerbated by recent changes in their work routine and commuting through busy areas. Zheng is sensitive to external events, which seem to trigger deeper reflections about luck and control in their life.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zheng experiences symptoms of anxiety and feelings of being trapped in cycles of negative thoughts, particularly around concepts of luck and their future. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zheng is feeling down and anxious, perceiving a series of ordinary challenges at work as a sign of bad luck, leading to a sense of being trapped in a negative cycle.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zheng's tendency to link external events with personal misfortune, along with a readiness to challenge these beliefs and seek alternative interpretations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral strategies to help Zheng reframe negative thoughts and break the perceived cycle of bad luck. Encourage Zheng to focus on positive past experiences and develop coping mechanisms to manage anxiety.
