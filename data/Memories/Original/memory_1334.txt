# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who struggles with feelings of self-blame and anxiety, particularly around relationships and family dynamics. They have a history of challenging past experiences impacting their emotional wellbeing, seeking to navigate complex emotions and find self-acceptance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user shows symptoms of anxiety and guilt related to past actions, intertwined with family relationships, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and trapped in self-critical thoughts, particularly revolving around feelings of shame regarding familial relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user is dealing with deep-rooted guilt, yet exhibits a willingness to explore kinder perceptions towards themselves.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user reframe self-critical thoughts and promote self-compassion. Encourage the user to engage in positive self-reflection and activities that reinforce their self-worth.
