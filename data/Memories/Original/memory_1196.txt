# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student, anxious about public speaking and concerned about being judged by others. They have experienced some improvement but still feel overwhelmed in situations where they're required to speak publicly.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms consistent with social anxiety focused on public speaking. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels nervous and overwhelmed by the fear of judgment and embarrassment during public speaking but exhibits determination to overcome this anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's significant self-awareness regarding their anxiety triggers and their potential to develop greater self-compassion and resilience.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to assist the user in recognizing and challenging negative thoughts related to public speaking. Develop gradual exposure strategies to mitigate anxiety and boost confidence.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels a mix of nervousness and pride about their initial attempts at speaking in class discussions, indicating cautious progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's inclination towards negative assumptions about peer judgment, though there is a clear openness to reject these assumptions and focus on self-acceptance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance the user's self-compassion and confidence by reframing skewed thought patterns. Encourage expressive participation and recognize personal growth.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels both surprised at the progress in developing confidence and yet remains anxious about new opportunities like joining a campus association.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's growing recognition of previous successes, which stands in conflict with lingering self-doubt.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce the user's ability to manage self-doubt through evidence-based self-reflection and small, targeted social exposure tasks using CBT techniques.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences mixed feelings of confidence after positive feedback from hosting a meeting, yet anxiety persists when anticipating public speaking.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's understanding of their cyclical thought patterns and their active pursuit of evidence to counter negative perceptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in adopting relaxation techniques and cognitive reframing to handle anxiety-provoking situations. Emphasize progressive desensitization to improve public speaking comfort.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels satisfied and beginning to enjoy public speaking due to recent success in a debate, but remains anxious about sustaining this progress.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's clear vision for future goals, yet recognizes persistent anxiety stemming from all-or-nothing thinking patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate cognitive restructuring to appreciate successes beyond all-or-nothing results. Encourage reflection on intrinsic motivations and continued participation in public speaking activities.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels significantly more comfortable in social situations, showing improvement in communication and reduced anxiety in recent interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's proactive engagement in listening and preparing, which has contributed to increased comfort in social exchanges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Maintain the momentum gained by reinforcing preparatory and mindfulness techniques. Introduce structured reflection through journaling to enhance self-awareness and track progress.
