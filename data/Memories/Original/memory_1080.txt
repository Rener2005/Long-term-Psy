# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yong Ma is a young child who struggles with shyness and finds it challenging to engage with peers in social settings. Yong Ma’s mother is supportive and seeks ways to help him integrate more comfortably into social situations, especially after moving to Nagoya.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yong Ma demonstrates signs of social anxiety and a tendency to prefer solitary play but has not been formally diagnosed with a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yong Ma feels hesitant and overwhelmed by new social settings, preferring solitary play despite a desire to engage with peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Yong Ma experiences paralyzing shyness and anxiety in social contexts but shows potential for positive social interaction with the right support.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yong Ma gradually build confidence in social settings by practicing simple social interactions. Encourage Yong Ma to use small phrases to initiate interactions and foster a sense of achievement through playful practice.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yong Ma seems uncomfortable during social activities, feeling left out and preferring solitary play with familiar toys.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Yong Ma’s social anxiety stems from fears of negative reactions and misunderstanding but recognizes opportunities to connect through shared interests.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Yong Ma’s ability to challenge negative thoughts about social interactions by using positive affirmations and encouraging attempts to say "hi" or ask questions, supported by evidence of positive past experiences.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yong Ma remains attached to familiar toys, showing reluctance to engage with new activities or toys, which frustrates his mother.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees a pattern of resistance to change in Yong Ma’s play but acknowledges his mother's efforts to introduce new stimuli and supports her in creating connections between familiar and new experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Yong Ma and his mother in incorporating new activities by bridging them to familiar interests, emphasizing the importance of patience and positive reinforcement.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yong Ma is slow to warm up to new activities, yet he shows signs of engagement when music is involved.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that music might be a connecting element for Yong Ma, facilitating moments of engagement and potential connection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Incorporate familiar songs into play activities to enhance Yong Ma’s engagement and gradually build his comfort in social settings, using music as a bridge.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yong Ma is beginning to show interest in participating but remains primarily passive, observing his peers rather than interacting directly.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Yong Ma's potential for engagement with others through games and stories but acknowledges his current struggle with initiating interactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Yong Ma develop strategies to initiate conversations with peers, such as preparing simple greetings or a story to share, aiming for gradual participation.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yong Ma shows curiosity and slight improvements in engaging with peers through classroom activities, though he remains shy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes that Yong Ma’s participation in singing and group activities is promising, signifying growth in social and communicative skills.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Yong Ma’s ongoing participation by focusing on interests, using visual and playful interactions to support language development, while celebrating small achievements to bolster confidence.
