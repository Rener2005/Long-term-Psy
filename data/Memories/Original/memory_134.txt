# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Sissie (pseudonym) is a school-aged child who enjoys engaging with friends and sharing ideas but has been noted by teachers for talking excessively in class. Sissie values friendships and expresses a desire to fit in and make people laugh, with a recent focus on improving classroom behavior.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Sissie exhibits signs of restlessness and eagerness for social interaction but has not been diagnosed with any formal behavioral disorders.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Sissie feels sad and confused about being perceived as disruptive in class but is eager to have fun with friends.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Sissie's desire to help and be a good friend, alongside a tendency to talk enthusiastically, which can be misinterpreted as disruptive.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Sissie regulate when to speak and listen, supporting balanced social interactions and positive classroom experiences through creative expression.
