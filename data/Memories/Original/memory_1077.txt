# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Mr. Chen (pseudonym) is a working professional in a corporate setting who struggles with self-imposed high standards and anxiety about underperformance. He often feels pressure to meet expectations at work and experiences difficulties balancing personal and professional life.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Mr. Chen experiences symptoms of anxiety, characterized by perfectionistic tendencies and fear of failure, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. Chen feels stressed and overwhelmed due to a significant work project, leading to fears of failure and job insecurity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Mr. Chen's tendency towards negative self-comparison and anxiety over perceived judgment, but recognizes his openness to adopt more compassionate self-perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Mr. Chen identify and challenge negative thought patterns. Encourage the development of strategies for managing work-related stress and anxiety.
