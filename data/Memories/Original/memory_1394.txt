# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Ai (pseudonym) is a young child who recently started attending kindergarten. She experiences challenges with concentration during activities and feels frustrated when receiving negative attention for not listening. Xiao Ai enjoys engaging in creative activities like building blocks.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Ai shows signs of difficulty in maintaining attention and expresses frustration with structured activities. There is no formal diagnosis of a learning or attention disorder at this time.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ai feels challenged by the structured environment of kindergarten, experiencing frustration and sadness due to difficulties in maintaining attention.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Ai's preference for self-directed activities and her struggle with the structure imposed by the school environment, indicating a need for engaging and interactive tasks.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Ai enhance focus and engagement through interactive and enjoyable activities. Implement strategies to improve task attention and minimize boredom.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Ai feels uncomfortable and trapped by rules and instructions during group activities, leading to feelings of annoyance and anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Ai's tendency to resist structured instructions, favoring collaborative and interactive play that allows for personal input and flexibility.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce opportunities for Xiao Ai to participate actively in rule-making within group activities and use CBT methods to foster a sense of control and cooperation.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
