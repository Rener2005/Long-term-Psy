# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student dealing with heightened anxiety related to academic performance and pressure from parental expectations. This impacts their self-esteem and ability to concentrate on studies effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, particularly in relation to academic achievements and family interactions, without any disclosed formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious about slipping grades and struggles with feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user tends to focus on self-critical thoughts and it's difficult for them to appreciate their past achievements, but they express willingness to cultivate self-compassion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge the notion of self-worth being solely tied to academic outcomes. Encourage small, manageable study increments to combat procrastination and anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious due to conflicts with their mother, which exacerbates stress about studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's defensive communication style may be escalating conflicts, yet notes their motivation to improve these interactions and seek understanding.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral techniques to transform the user's communication from defensive to assertive, practicing expressing emotions non-judgmentally to foster a supportive family environment.

# Third Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]
