# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
A (pseudonym) recently started a new job with increased responsibilities, leading to feelings of inadequacy and self-doubt. He is concerned about meeting high expectations at his workplace and managing interpersonal relationships, while also maintaining a good work-life balance and supporting his family.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
A experiences symptoms of anxiety, self-doubt, and stress, particularly related to work pressures. There is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
A feels overwhelmed and anxious due to the pressures of his new job, frequently questioning his abilities and feeling inadequate as he compares himself to colleagues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that A engages in negative self-talk and experiences a cycle of anxiety and doubt, yet shows willingness to explore alternative, positive thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help A challenge negative thoughts, focus on small successes, and manage work-related anxiety. Encourage relaxation strategies to improve mental well-being and focus.
