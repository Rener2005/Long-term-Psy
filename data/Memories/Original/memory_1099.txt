# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is a student who struggles with anxiety, particularly around social interactions and expression. Wang experiences feelings of regret and self-blame due to perceived social inadequacies. Recently, Wang has been attempting to improve social skills and build confidence in group settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang shows symptoms of social anxiety and possibly low self-esteem. There is no official diagnosis from a mental health professional documented in the session notes.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels tense and anxious about past social interactions, particularly focusing on regret and self-blame.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wang tends to replay past social interactions negatively, highlighting feelings of inadequacy but shows an openness to reframe these situations with a more supportive perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang challenge the cycle of negative thoughts and assumptions surrounding social skills. Develop strategies for engaging in social activities with increased confidence and reduced anxiety.
