# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Mr. L is an adult male who experiences feelings of guilt and anxiety related to compulsive urges towards children's toys and women's handbags. These urges recall comforting feelings from childhood but have become disruptive to his life and relationships. Mr. L is seeking to understand these behaviors and find healthier emotional outlets.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Mr. L is not formally diagnosed with a specific mental health disorder but reports symptoms of compulsive behavior and anxiety.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Mr. L feels overwhelmed by his urges towards certain objects, leading to a cycle of excitement followed by guilt.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Mr. L is insightful about his urges being tied to comfort from past experiences, yet he struggles with controlling these compulsions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Mr. L identify and challenge impulse-related thoughts. Encourage him to find and engage in alternative fulfilling activities to mitigate the urges.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Mr. L in redefining thrill-seeking thoughts and finding alternative activities like hiking to satisfy his need for excitement while managing anxiety effectively.
