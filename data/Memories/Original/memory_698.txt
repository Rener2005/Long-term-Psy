# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a young adult experiencing anxiety linked to social interactions and a strong need for self-improvement, recently confronting a situation with classmates that heightened feelings of inadequacy and doubt.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user manifests symptoms of social anxiety and low self-esteem but has not been formally diagnosed with a specific mental health condition.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and anxious due to perceived social judgment and criticism following an incident with classmates.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user experiences intensified anxiety from critical self-evaluations and appears caught in a cycle of negative self-assessment, yet acknowledges strengths under guidance.

[Consultation Goals]  
Apply cognitive behavioral strategies to challenge negative self-talk and redirect focus toward personal achievements. Encourage mindfulness to manage anxiety and promote positive affirmations for self-esteem reinforcement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s intense apprehension about social acceptance and tendency toward negative assumptions without validating evidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive-behavioral techniques to assess and reframe skewed perceptions, emphasize self-compassion, and cultivate strategies for confronting anxiety-provoking social situations.

# Third Consultation

[Consultation Goals]

# Sixth Consultation
