# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Duan (pseudonym) is encountering social anxiety and feelings of isolation as a university student, particularly in interactions with roommates and peers. Duan is seeking ways to improve social connections and manage emotional responses.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Duan experiences social anxiety, sometimes leading to withdrawal in social settings, but there is no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Duan feels anxious and frustrated following an argument with a roommate, which has led to discomfort in his living situation and difficulty focusing on studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Duan is caught in a cycle of negative self-beliefs, feeling responsible for conflicts, but is willing to reflect on these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help Duan recognize and reframe negative thoughts about personal responsibility and social self-worth. Start with strategies to improve communication with the roommate and manage situational anxiety. 

# Second Consultation
