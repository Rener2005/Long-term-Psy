# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who has been feeling down and overwhelmed, particularly regarding their studies, leading to issues with concentration and social connections. They perceive a growing distance from friends and a lack of joy in activities previously enjoyed, often stemming from academic pressure and self-comparison.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits signs of anxiety and possibly depression. They frequently experience negative self-comparisons and doubts about personal adequacy concerning academic and social contexts but have not mentioned any formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels heavy and disconnected, struggling to find meaning or motivation in academic and social aspects of life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's notable level of self-doubt and difficulty in managing academic stress, albeit showing openness to changing these patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Leverage cognitive behavioral therapy to help the user identify and challenge negative self-talk and perceptions, promoting gradual engagement with academic tasks and rebuilding social connections.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels stressed and anxious about exams, with a pervasive sense of low energy and hopelessness impacting their motivation and interest in studies.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's continued struggle with self-comparisons and sees potential in their self-awareness and willingness to take advice.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to apply structured study techniques and reframing of negative thoughts to improve focus and reduce academic anxiety, using self-controlled strategies and incremental goals.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is overwhelmed and frustrated by their perceived inability to concentrate on studies, expressing concerns about their grades and social isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees the user's readiness to explore manageable, methodical approaches to studying and social engagement, indicating progress in addressing self-imposed barriers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the user in developing a feasible, adaptable study schedule paired with challenging negative cognitions, and encourage initial steps toward re-establishing social involvement.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user experiences mixed emotions, having engaged in a social activity that felt rewarding yet continues to harbor anxiety about social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives the user's growth in participation and self-efficacy even as they grapple with fears of judgment in social contexts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in utilizing recent positive social experiences to counter negative social anxieties, while encouraging gradual, realistic exposure to social settings with mindfulness techniques.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels positively impacted by interactions with new friends, though alone time still brings about a sense of overwhelm and disinterest in earlier enjoyed activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the beneficial effects of social support on the user's mood and engagement, alongside a sustained vulnerability to feelings of isolation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote further integration of enjoyable social activities into the user's routine and explore new interests to bolster emotional health and maintain positive social momentum.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels improved and energized, having actively participated in school activities that renewed their motivation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes significant advancements in the user's self-awareness and involvement in constructive activities, along with an emerging confidence in expressing ideas and willingness to lead.

[Consultation Goals]
