# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student experiencing intense anxiety related to social situations and academic performance. His self-perception is heavily influenced by fears of being judged and perceived inadequacies. Li is motivated to improve his social skills and confidence.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li exhibits symptoms typical of social anxiety disorder, though no formal diagnosis has been confirmed. His anxiety is characterized by avoidance of social situations and excessive worry about how he is perceived by others.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is anxious and distressed about social interactions, particularly fears of judgment and making mistakes in class.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Li's tendency towards anticipatory anxiety and catastrophizing but also observes his willingness to explore self-compassion and challenge negative thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral strategies to help Li reframe anxious thoughts and practice behavioral experiments by engaging in low-risk social interactions progressively.
