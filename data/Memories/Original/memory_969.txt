# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu (pseudonym) is a male deeply impacted by secondary trauma from his involvement in earthquake relief efforts. He is also anticipating the birth of his first child, which brings both excitement and anxiety. Liu worries about his ability to support and balance work and family responsibilities.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu experiences symptoms of anxiety and self-doubt, particularly in relation to past traumatic events and future family responsibilities, but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu feels anxious and overwhelmed by the prospect of parenthood and is burdened by unresolved guilt related to past earthquake relief efforts.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Liu struggles with self-perception issues and is burdened by guilt, but demonstrates openness to exploring self-compassion and alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Liu reframe negative self-perceptions and acknowledge past achievements. Encourage gradual sharing of feelings with trusted individuals to alleviate emotional burden.
