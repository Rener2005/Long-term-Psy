# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
B (pseudonym) is an unemployed young adult experiencing feelings of guilt and anxiety. They recently engaged in an online financial transaction they regret, which has heightened their concern about future prospects and familial expectations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
B exhibits symptoms of anxiety and guilt related to past decisions but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
B feels overwhelmingly guilty and anxious about a recent decision, replaying the event in their mind, which disrupts their daily focus.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that B is burdened by shame and fear of familial disappointment, yet shows openness to considering more balanced viewpoints.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help B reframe negative self-judgments and develop a more constructive perspective on mistakes. Encourage setting small, achievable goals to rebuild confidence, such as initiating a job search.
