# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a recent employee adjusting to a new job, experiencing significant anxiety related to their performance and impression at work. They have been at the job for two months and are struggling to feel comfortable with both the work and social environment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user demonstrates symptoms of anxiety, particularly related to self-evaluation and fear of judgment from others. No formal mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious about their performance at work, fearing negative judgment from colleagues and supervisors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user's fear of inadequacy leads to a reluctance in engaging with colleagues and seeking support, showing a tendency to overthink situations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user reframe their perception of asking for help as a positive step toward learning. Encourage gradual social engagement and introduce anxiety management strategies like deep breathing.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely at work and experiences increased anxiety as they continue adjusting to the new environment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with perceived negativity from colleagues, showing difficulty in interpreting social cues optimistically.

[Consultation Goals]  
Aid the user in developing a balanced view of social interactions at work and set small, achievable goals for daily social engagement. Encourage self-compassion in managing self-expectations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed by a challenging project, leading to anxiety and self-doubt about their abilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of perfectionism, where the user equates difficulty and mistakes with personal inadequacy.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral strategies to help the user reframe mistakes as learning opportunities and develop a supportive mindset. Encourage seeking help as a practical step in overcoming work-related challenges.
