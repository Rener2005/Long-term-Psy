## User Background

Zhu (pseudonym) is a university student who experiences anxiety about academic performance, particularly in English class, due to past incidents where harsh criticism led to feelings of embarrassment and fear. Zhu is eager to improve self-confidence and find strategies to manage this anxiety.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhu experiences symptoms of anxiety primarily in academic settings but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhu feels anxious and afraid of attending English class due to past critical feedback leading to feelings of failure and fear of embarrassment.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhu tends to focus on negative self-assessment and fear of criticism, but also shows openness to using supportive strategies and engaging in self-compassion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhu challenge unrealistic fears and build self-esteem, focusing on setting small, achievable goals to foster classroom engagement and manage academic anxiety.
