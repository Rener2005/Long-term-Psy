# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Y is a student experiencing feelings of isolation and frustration due to peer exclusion and a perceived lack of understanding from those around them. Y has recently felt abandoned by close friends, resulting in emotional distress and difficulties concentrating on studies.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Y exhibits symptoms of anxiety tied to interpersonal relationships and feelings of inadequacy, though there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder reported.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels angry and alone, experiencing a persistent cycle of negative thoughts centered around self-worth and perceived judgment by others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Y's tendency to internalize blame for peer rejection, revealing an underlying struggle with self-esteem, but also a willingness to explore more self-compassionate views.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help Y challenge negative self-perceptions and recognize personal achievements. Encourage the development of coping strategies to manage academic and social stress.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels overwhelmed and frustrated, noticing how negative emotions disrupt academic focus and social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Y's increasing self-awareness of the impact of negative emotions, yet Y continues to struggle with self-doubt linked to external validation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Y in identifying and challenging irrational thought patterns about self-worth, using journaling to reinforce positive perspectives and self-compassion.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels anxious and worthless, particularly in response to parental criticism regarding academic performance, but is gaining awareness of emotional triggers through journaling.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Y's persistent feelings of inadequacy exacerbated by familial expectations, but also an emerging tendency to explore alternative, kinder perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Y practice self-validation and reinforce the value of effort over achievement. Encourage engaging in positive social interactions to bolster confidence and reduce isolation.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels isolated and frustrated with seemingly one-dimensional communication with parents focused on academic performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Y's courage in attempting to express feelings through writing, alongside a longing for deeper familial connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate strategies for Y to initiate and sustain meaningful conversations with parents, focusing on shared interests and emotional validation to enhance familial support.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels relieved by participation in a writing class but remains anxious about potential judgment and parental disapproval due to past bullying experiences.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist recognizes Y's sustained anxiety around acceptance and judgment from peers and family, but notes growing enthusiasm for self-expression.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Y in challenging fears of negative evaluation using evidence-based reassurances. Encourage gradual sharing of self-expression with trusted friends to build confidence in creative pursuits.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Y feels more supported and confident following positive changes in family communication, leading to increased motivation and social engagement.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes an improvement in Y's emotional well-being and communication skills, leading to enhanced self-expression and participation in social settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Y to maintain positive momentum by setting small, achievable goals in sharing creative work. Reinforce coping strategies for anxiety, focusing on progress and positive feedback.
