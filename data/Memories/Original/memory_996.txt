# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
L (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing anxiety primarily related to exam preparation and the pressure of meeting future academic goals. L frequently engages in self-comparison with peers, which contributes to self-doubt and stress. L seeks help to better manage emotions and improve academic performance.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
L presents with significant anxiety and low self-confidence. No formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
L feels anxious and overwhelmed by recent poor performance in a mock exam, worrying about failing to gain admission to a desired school.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that L holds high expectations for themselves, resulting in a cycle of self-criticism and stress despite recognizing past successes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help L challenge negative thoughts, particularly around academic performance. Introduce strategies to improve sleep and manage study-related anxiety.
