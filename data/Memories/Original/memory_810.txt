# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li (pseudonym) is a female in her late 40s who recently lost her husband, leaving her struggling with overwhelming grief and sadness. Li has been finding it difficult to cope with her loss and expresses feelings of loneliness and emotional pain while trying to process her husband's belongings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li is experiencing profound grief, manifesting as intense sadness and feelings of loneliness. She has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels deeply sad and overwhelmed by memories of her late husband, finding it challenging to manage her emotions and daily activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li struggling with intense grief, but she shows a willingness to explore coping mechanisms and engage in activities that might offer some relief, though she seems unsure where to begin.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li acknowledge her grief while gradually incorporating small, manageable activities that bring her a sense of peace and normalcy, such as short walks and bedtime routines.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li continues to feel lost and consumed by sadness, particularly when faced with personal reminders of her husband, spiraling into self-critical thoughts about past actions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's tendency toward self-blame and guilt, which are common in grief, but sees potential for Li to reframe these thoughts through compassionate self-reflection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in challenging self-critical thoughts and introducing self-compassion. Encourage engaging in nurturing activities like gardening to help manage grief and ground her emotions.
