# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang is an adult female who experiences feelings of anxiety and inadequacy, particularly in response to familial interactions and work pressure. Recently, she encountered conflict with her mother-in-law, which heightened her anxiety and emotional distress.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang exhibits symptoms of anxiety and self-critical thought patterns, without a disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels anxious and hurt due to a recent conflict with her mother-in-law, which has disrupted her sleep and emotional well-being.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wang struggles with recurring self-doubt and emotional responses to criticism, but she shows readiness to explore coping strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang manage anxiety and challenge negative beliefs about self-worth. Introduce grounding techniques and cognitive reframing to handle familial criticism effectively.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Wang’s sensitivity to error and negative self-talk, yet she is starting to question the logic behind her beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Wang to engage in self-compassion and recognize thought patterns that affect her work. Support the development of mindfulness techniques to enhance emotional resilience.
