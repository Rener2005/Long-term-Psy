# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
User "P" is pursuing a master's degree and is dealing with heightened anxiety, particularly related to academic pressures and social interactions. P has expressed a tendency to avoid social situations and relies heavily on takeout food due to anxiety about going out.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
P experiences significant anxiety, especially in social contexts, characterized by avoidance and a pervasive feeling of inadequacy, but has not been formally diagnosed with a specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
P feels overwhelmed by anxiety about the new semester and socializing, resulting in avoidance behaviors like relying on takeout.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices P’s tendency toward self-doubt and negative self-comparisons, but also a willingness to confront these challenges.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help P identify and challenge negative thoughts about social interactions and encourage gradual exposure to social settings, starting with attending a student welcome event.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
P feels frustrated and disheartened after a difficult social outing, leading to feelings of inadequacy and withdrawal.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of catastrophizing social situations and persistent fear of judgment, although P is open to explore alternative, more positive perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist P in recognizing and challenging cognitive distortions during social interactions. Encourage setting small, manageable social goals and utilizing self-compassion.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
P feels anxious and invisible during a group discussion, doubting their ability to contribute meaningfully.

[Observational Impressions]  
There is a notable cycle of negative self-assessment and reluctance to engage, yet P shows a readiness to adopt compassionate self-talk.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support P in developing more balanced self-perceptions and promoting active participation using affirmations. Incremental exposure to group settings to reinforce positive interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
P feels somewhat relieved after a supportive group exercise but continues to struggle with self-doubt and the fear of not fitting in.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes an openness to new experiences and acknowledges the apprehension around judgment, despite evidence of supportive peer reactions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce positive experiences by recording instances of support and success. Implement relaxation techniques to manage nervousness and steadily increase contributory participation.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
P reports positive experiences and an increased sense of inclusion and appreciation in group projects, indicating a reduction in social anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
P shows marked improvement in social engagement and self-assurance, attributable to proactive engagement and peer validation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage continuation of proactive social engagement and set goals for participation in additional group activities. Track progress to build confidence.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
P feels more confident and acknowledges reduced anxiety, especially with positive feedback from peers and tutor.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes significant growth in P’s self-confidence and participation, with a constructive approach to handling anxiety in new social contexts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Consolidate communication skills and exposure strategies, targeting upcoming opportunities like networking events. Encourage continued practice in varied social settings.
