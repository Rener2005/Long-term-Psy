# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Cheng (pseudonym) is a university student dealing with epilepsy, which contributes to his anxiety and fear of social judgment. He feels overwhelmed by academic and social pressures and has a distant relationship with his father. Cheng seeks to improve his social interactions and family communication.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Cheng exhibits symptoms of anxiety and social withdrawal but has no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder beyond epilepsy.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Cheng feels overwhelmed and anxious, particularly in group settings at school, which affects his focus and appetite.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Cheng’s strong fear of judgment and avoidance in social situations, yet sees an openness to gradually engage and try new strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Cheng recognize and challenge negative thoughts related to judgment, develop coping strategies for social anxiety, and foster communication skills to improve interactions at school and at home.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Cheng feels anxious and helpless about bridging the communicative gap with his father and experiences discomfort in family relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Cheng's longing for connection, highlighting his vulnerability and desire for understanding within familial contexts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Cheng in initiating honest conversations with his father to express feelings and seek support. Encourage rehearsed positive interactions and CBT strategies to alleviate anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Cheng feels anxious and isolated after experiencing a seizure in class, heightening his fear of judgment by peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Cheng’s conflicting desire for socialization and fear of negative perceptions, alongside a willingness to confront social anxieties.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Cheng to gradually face social fears, starting with small interactions. Reinforce CBT techniques to challenge shame and improve self-worth beyond his condition.

# Fifth Consultation

# Sixth Consultation
