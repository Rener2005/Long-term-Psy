# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a high school student who identifies as feeling lonely and isolated among peers. They have a history of seeking attention through less constructive means, due to feelings of neglect at home, and are interested in finding ways to form genuine connections.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences feelings of loneliness and inadequacy but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lonely and regrets past actions that were attempts to gain attention from classmates, expressing a desire for inclusion.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's willingness to acknowledge past mistakes and a readiness to explore healthier ways to connect with peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge negative thoughts about being unliked and foster simple, honest interactions with classmates to build genuine connections.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels discouraged about their attempts to connect with classmates, interpreting reactions as personal rejection which intensifies feelings of isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s tendency to take classmates' reactions personally, but sees potential in the user’s ability to reframe negative experiences constructively.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in identifying and challenging skewed thought patterns regarding peer interactions. Encourage more balanced approaches in initiating conversations with peers.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels better after positive interactions in class but remains wary and uncertain about maintaining these new connections.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a growing openness in the user to social engagement, though fear of inadequacy still lingers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to continue taking small steps in social interactions, using CBT techniques to challenge negative self-perceptions and build social confidence.

# Fourth Consultation

[Consultation Goals]
