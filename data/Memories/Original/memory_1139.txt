# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Liu (pseudonym) is navigating university life while dealing with anxiety, primarily in social settings and concerning interpersonal dynamics, especially with a roommate. Liu is conscientious and open to exploring strategies for improving emotional well-being and social interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Liu displays symptoms of social anxiety without a formal mental health disorder diagnosis, characterized by anxious thoughts and avoidance behaviors in social contexts.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Liu feels anxious and overwhelmed due to ongoing conflicts with a roommate, questioning personal adequacy in social settings.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Liu’s anxiety as stemming from negative automatic thoughts and acknowledges Liu’s capacity for recognizing these cognitive patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to help Liu identify and challenge negative self-perceptions and assumptions about social situations. Encourage Liu to explore alternative explanations for social cues and interactions.
