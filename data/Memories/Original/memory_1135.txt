# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang is a university student struggling with feelings of anxiety and loneliness, particularly in relation to adapting to the differences between his own background and those of his roommates. He experiences apprehension about expressing himself and is working towards feeling more integrated within his social environment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang experiences symptoms of anxiety tied to social interactions but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels overwhelmed by the pressure and loneliness due to cultural and economic differences with his roommates, causing anxiety and frustration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Yang's difficulty in expressing his feelings and dealing with self-doubt, yet sees a willingness to explore more positive self-concepts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Yang recognize and counteract negative thoughts, fostering confidence in expressing his own experiences to improve social integration.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang continues to feel anxious and isolated, struggling with tension in interactions due to differing perspectives with his roommate.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Yang's low self-esteem linked to social comparisons but notes readiness to take small steps towards engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Yang in addressing skewed thought patterns and exploring gradual, low-pressure social opportunities to enhance connection and reduce anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels anxious about participating in social activities, fearing judgment and inadequacy in expressing his ideas.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Yang's hesitation in initiating participation due to fear of embarrassment but recognizes his awareness of personal interests as a potential bridge.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Yang to take incremental steps towards participation using CBT to reduce fear of judgment, gradually increasing his comfort in social settings.

# Fourth Consultation

[Consultation Goals]
