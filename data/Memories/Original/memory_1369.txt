# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Qian (pseudonym) is a newly appointed principal, experiencing feelings of inadequacy and anxiety due to taking on new responsibilities. Qian is transitioning into this role with apprehension about handling the expectations and stress related to managing a school effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Qian experiences symptoms of anxiety and self-doubt. There has been no disclosure of a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Qian feels overwhelmed by the responsibilities of being a principal, particularly fearing inadequacy and failure in public speaking and decision-making.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Qian's anxiety related to high expectations and avoidance of stressors, yet notes Qian's willingness to reframe negative thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Qian reframe self-doubt and anxiety into a focus on preparations and past successes. Encourage strategies for effective stress management and fostering self-compassion.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Qian continues to feel overwhelmed by workload and responsibilities, with persistent doubt about leadership abilities and fear of not meeting expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the presence of cognitive distortions such as "all-or-nothing" thinking but acknowledges Qian's openness to explore alternative perspectives and focus on achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Qian in identifying and challenging thought distortions, encourage logging successes for confidence-building, and incorporate self-care strategies to manage stress.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Qian feels anxious about decision-making responsibilities and doubts personal qualifications, fearing negative outcomes from leadership choices.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Qian’s struggle with self-doubt but notes progress in recognizing past successes and seeking collaboration with the school team.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce the practice of recognizing past successes and foster collaboration with the team to reduce feelings of isolation. Encourage the development of effective time management skills.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Qian is feeling slightly better but continues to experience anxiety about leadership and effective time management, with concerns about meeting expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Qian’s anxiety surrounding feedback situations but notes potential for adopting a more relaxed approach to leadership roles.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the creation of structured yet flexible communication strategies to ease anxiety in meetings. Foster self-compassion and realistic goal setting.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote the practice of setting achievable goals and reflecting on daily achievements to reinforce confidence. Continue integrating self-care into routine.

# Sixth Consultation
