# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
C is a university student who feels ashamed of a recent incident involving a classmate, Y, and struggles with feelings of loneliness and helplessness. C is reflective about personal behavior and is eager to improve interpersonal interactions through better communication and emotional control.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
C experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
C feels overwhelmed by shame and anxiety, worrying about the impact of a recent incident on social relationships and self-respect.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that C struggles with negative self-perception related to impulsive behavior and is open to learning ways to improve self-regulation and communication.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help C identify and manage impulsive thoughts and behaviors. Foster strategies for improving social comfort and rebuilding trust with peers.
