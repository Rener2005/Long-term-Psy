# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student facing significant pressure regarding the upcoming graduation, feeling inferior to peers mostly due to financial difficulties. Li is reflective and seeks to understand the relationship between self-worth and external circumstances, particularly financial status.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of low self-esteem and anxiety driven by financial stress and social comparisons, but there’s no formal diagnosis mentioned. Li displays a willingness to gain insight and manage emotions constructively.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels overwhelmed and inadequate when comparing achievements and financial status to peers, leading to social avoidance and low self-worth.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Li's tendency to equate self-worth with financial status and perceives a cycle of avoidance due to feelings of inferiority, yet Li demonstrates insight and openness to exploring these beliefs.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive-behavioral strategies to help Li challenge and reframe self-worth beliefs influenced by external factors. Begin promoting self-compassion and developing practical steps towards financial independence.
