# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang (pseudonym) is a single mother facing significant stress related to interactions with social services concerning the welfare of her daughter. She struggles with feelings of inadequacy as a parent and is currently dealing with substance use issues, specifically heroin. Zhang desires stability and a positive environment for her daughter, aspiring to be a reliable and sober role model.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem, primarily triggered by fear of losing custody of her daughter. She shows signs of substance use, which further complicates her emotional and psychological well-being. No formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang is overwhelmed and hopeless, feeling inadequate and fearful regarding her parenting abilities and the constant scrutiny from social services.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's deep-rooted fear of not being a good mother, yet identifies moments of insight and self-reflection where she recognizes her capacity for positive interactions with her daughter.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang identify and challenge negative beliefs about her parenting. Develop strategies to improve self-esteem and manage overwhelming emotions, with an emphasis on mindfulness and self-care practices.
