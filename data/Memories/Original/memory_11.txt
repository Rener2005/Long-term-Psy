# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Li is a university class monitor who experiences anxiety and self-doubt, particularly in response to others' perceptions and reactions. He aims to improve his management of worries related to his responsibilities and interactions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Li exhibits symptoms of anxiety and frequent self-doubt related to performance and social relationships, though there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li feels anxious and restless, experiencing sleep disturbances due to persistent worries about his role as class monitor and classmates' perceptions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Li's tendency to overthink and misinterpret minor social cues as personal failures, yet he shows openness to exploring alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Li challenge and reframe his negative thoughts, focusing on recognizing and consolidating his achievements as a leader. Introduce relaxation techniques to improve sleep quality.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li continues to feel anxious and insecure, particularly concerned about his efficacy and social acceptance in his role.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Xiao Li's propensity for negative interpretations of social interactions, but recognizes his awareness and acknowledgment of positive feedback from peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Xiao Li to actively recall positive feedback and accomplishments to counterbalance negative self-perceptions. Implement strategies to shift focus from self-doubt to self-assurance by revisiting evidence of personal success.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li feels frustrated with ongoing self-doubt, which leads to avoiding social interactions that could reaffirm his leadership abilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Li's struggle with exaggerated negative thoughts during decision-making but perceives his potential for change through the exploration of balanced viewpoints.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Xiao Li in developing the ability to recognize and reinterpret negative thoughts as they arise, and practice maintaining a positive internal dialogue based on past successes.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Li experiences heightened anxiety and self-questioning related to expectations from himself and peers, causing sleep issues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Li's tendency to internalize others' behaviors as negative judgments on his leadership, but notes his willingness to attempt new strategies to improve social confidence.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Xiao Li's engagement in social interactions to enhance confidence and provide incentives for regular self-reflection and journaling to track progress in reframing perceptions and reducing anxiety.
