# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang is a student who often feels lost and unmotivated in academic pursuits, describing a lack of joy in both studies and personal activities. Zhang feels pressure to perform academically and struggles to find meaningful direction beyond education.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang experiences symptoms of low motivation and a sense of emptiness, leading to feelings of inadequacy and self-doubt when not engaged in school-related activities.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels disengaged from studies and describes a pervasive sense of emptiness and lack of motivation towards academics.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang’s routine-driven approach to academics without emotional engagement and notes an openness to exploring interest-driven learning as a means to reignite passion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang identify personal interests that enhance motivation. Set small, achievable goals that reconnect Zhang with the joy of learning and potentially explore peer interactions to enhance emotional engagement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels lonely and disconnected from classmates, with underlying anxiety about social interactions, including fear of judgment and inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang's awareness of self-doubt impacting group participation, but also Zhang's willingness to challenge isolation by exploring personal value and connection within academic settings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang in identifying self-doubt triggers and implementing cognitive reframing to enhance self-worth and social interaction. Set goals to actively engage in class discussions, thereby fostering a sense of belonging.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang feels a mix of excitement and anxiety regarding public speaking opportunities, battling fears of judgment and performance-related pressures.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang’s tendency to overthink performances, yet recognizes a readiness to embrace more positive, outcome-independent experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang to adopt reframed mindsets that emphasize enjoyment over performance. Use cognitive restructuring to build confidence, focusing on preparation strategies and reinforcing the discovery of passion through practice.
