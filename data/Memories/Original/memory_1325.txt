# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a student who is experiencing stress and anxiety related to academic performance, particularly about upcoming entrance exams. Li is concerned about future prospects and the pressure of meeting expectations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li reports feelings of anxiety influenced by high personal and familial expectations, but there's no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels stressed and anxious about the prospect of failing the entrance exams, with worries about academic and familial expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that Li's anxiety stems from negative thinking patterns and rigid self-expectations, manifesting in avoidance behaviors such as procrastination.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Li break down study schedules into manageable timeframes and introduce a relaxation strategy, like deep breathing, to cope with exam-related anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels better but still experiences doubts about fitting in at a new school, linked to self-comparison and anxiety about peer abilities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes Li's tendency to focus on perceived inadequacies compared to peers, though there's a recognition of previous successes which is promising for growth.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Li in focusing on past academic achievements and practicing self-compassion to counter negative self-comparisons. Encourage participation in school activities to build confidence and social networks.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li shows improvement in self-perception, though anxiety persists concerning exam performance and parental expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes Li’s increased awareness of personal achievements, yet notes persistent worry about failing to meet expectations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Li to identify and challenge irrational fear of failure using evidence from past success. Use CBT strategies to reinforce a positive and balanced self-assessment.

# Sixth Consultation
