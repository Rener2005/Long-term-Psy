# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li Ming is an adult who recently lost a job, leading to struggles with anxiety and feelings of inadequacy. Li Ming often questions their abilities and feels isolated from others, particularly in professional settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li Ming experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming feels anxious and overwhelmed, particularly struggling with self-doubt during job interviews and social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Li Ming's negative self-talk is a barrier to connecting with others and overcoming the sense of inadequacy, yet there's a willingness to explore kinder self-perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to help Li Ming challenge negative thoughts, develop self-compassion, and build confidence in social and professional interactions. Encourage positive reinforcement through self-affirmations and share experiences for additional support.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming continues to experience anxiety and low mood, particularly after unsatisfactory job interviews, feeling increasingly doubtful about their career path.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes deep-seated negative self-perceptions, but Li Ming is open to reframing these thoughts and recognizing past successes as evidence of capability.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Li Ming's ability to challenge negative thought patterns and reinforce self-worth by acknowledging past achievements. Encourage consistency in practicing communication skills and anxiety management strategies.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming continues to feel anxious and isolated in social settings, experiencing increased feelings of not belonging and frequent negative self-comparisons.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Li Ming's anxiety is exacerbated by assumptions of social judgment, but there is emerging self-awareness and willingness to challenge these assumptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Promote more adaptive responses to perceived social judgment through structured exposure to social interactions. Encourage Li Ming to adopt a mindset focused on curiosity and connection rather than fear of judgment.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li Ming is anxious about keeping up with a skills retraining course, feeling overwhelmed by the volume of new information.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li Ming's high self-imposed expectations contributing to stress and motivation loss, yet there's an openness to restructuring learning strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Li Ming establish achievable learning goals and prioritize tasks to manage stress. Reinforce the importance of self-compassion and peer support throughout the learning process.

# Fifth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li Ming's improved mood with social support, though there's lingering anxiety about initiating and maintaining these connections.

[Consultation Goals]

# Sixth Consultation

Li Ming is feeling optimistic and more confident after starting a new job, with supportive colleagues and engaging work helping to alleviate prior anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]
