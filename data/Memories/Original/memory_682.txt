# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wu is a student who is experiencing anxiety and stress related to academic performance and the pressure to succeed in exams. They are driven by high self-expectations, particularly in context of family expectations, and seek to manage these pressures better.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wu experiences symptoms of anxiety linked to academic stress, but there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder reported.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wu feels overwhelmed and anxious about upcoming exams, with a fear of negative academic outcomes and disappointing family expectations.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wu struggles with self-criticism and fear of failure but is open to exploring self-compassion and alternative strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral approaches to help Wu challenge negative self-perceptions and develop coping strategies for managing academic pressure and anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wu continues to experience tension, particularly in regards to familial expectations, which exacerbates anxiety about academic performance.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Wu exhibits patterns of defensiveness in family interactions, which impacts academic focus and exacerbates stress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Wu in communicating more effectively with family using assertive but empathetic communication methods, such as “I” statements, to reduce conflict and manage anxiety.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wu is making slight improvements in academic performance with new study methods but remains anxious about impending exams.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Wu's entrenched high expectations for perfection, contributing to ongoing anxiety but acknowledges Wu’s willingness to engage in reframing thought patterns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Help Wu set small, attainable study goals and track achievements to bolster confidence and challenge the binary outlook on academic success.
