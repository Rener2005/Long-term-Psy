# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Susan (pseudonym) is a young adult experiencing deep feelings of overwhelm and hopelessness, marked by a recent encounter with suicidal thoughts. Susan is navigating family dynamics and is often caught in self-critical thought patterns that contribute to her mental distress.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Susan appears to be struggling with symptoms of depression and anxiety but has not been formally diagnosed with any specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Susan feels overwhelmed by hopeless thoughts, perceiving life as a dark place devoid of joy and comfort.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a significant struggle with perceived hopelessness and inability to engage in previously enjoyable activities, demonstrating signs of deep distress but also a willingness to seek alternative coping mechanisms.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral strategies to aid Susan in identifying and challenging the thoughts contributing to hopelessness. Introduce small, manageable activities to promote engagement and relieve mental fatigue.
