# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Melany is a young adult who recently lost her job. She experiences feelings of inadequacy and anxiety, often isolating herself and lacking motivation to engage in productive activities.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Melany shows symptoms of anxiety and depression, characterized by feelings of worthlessness and low energy, though no formal diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Melany feels lost and anxious after her job loss, struggling with a lack of motivation and overwhelming fear of job searching and failure.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Melany's cycle of guilt and avoidance, driven by a significant fear of failure, but notes her openness to identifying her strengths.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT techniques to help Melany identify and challenge negative thoughts, fostering self-compassion, and set small, achievable goals to gradually re-engage in job searching and social activities.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Melany continues to feel worthlessness and experiences discouragement related to daily tasks and inactivity.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Melany is caught in self-comparison and high self-expectations but sees potential in acknowledging small achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Melany to celebrate small accomplishments, challenge high standards, and establish a routine incorporating manageable, anxiety-reducing activities.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Melany feels a sense of accomplishment from volunteer work but remains anxious about career prospects, fearing past failures.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Melany's increased engagement in positive activities yet persistent self-doubt regarding her professional potential.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Melany in reframing her volunteer skills for employment contexts and explore broader interpretations of her competencies.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Melany is striving to establish daily routines but faces paralyzing fear of failure and decision-making in career planning.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Melany's progress in striving for structure but recognizes her fear of failure impeding full commitment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Melany in recognizing external factors impacting job search anxiety, develop constructive thought patterns, and create a balanced daily routine.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Melany gains hope from her interview experience, despite rejection, and reflects on improvements she could make.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Melany's resilience and ability to treat the interview as a learning opportunity, while still feeling daunted by networking.

[Consultation Goals]  
Enhance Melany's interview skills with structured story-telling practices and reduce networking anxiety by reframing it as a learning exchange.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Melany feels more confident with her new job and improved social engagement but occasionally faces performance anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Melany's successful transition into employment and social connection, marking significant progress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Melany to challenge lingering negative thoughts, journal positive feedback, and maintain her social and professional growth momentum.
