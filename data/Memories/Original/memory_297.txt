# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Dong (pseudonym) is a university student experiencing anxiety, particularly regarding social interactions and eye contact with girls in class. Dong is motivated to overcome these challenges by actively participating in discussions and seeking to connect with others.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Dong displays symptoms of social anxiety, particularly in situations involving potential judgment. There is no disclosed formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Dong feels anxious and embarrassed about making eye contact with girls, often fearing judgment and ridicule.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes that Dong struggles with a cycle of negative thoughts, yet demonstrates insight and willingness to adopt new perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist Dong in challenging negative beliefs and fears concerning judgment. Encourage gradual exposure to social scenarios to reduce anxiety over time.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Dong continues to experience anxiety and embarrassment about eye contact, especially with girls in class, but expresses a desire to improve.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that although Dong's beliefs are deeply ingrained, he is open to reframing them and moving forward with practical strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Aid Dong in identifying and challenging invalid thoughts with cognitive behavioral tactics. Foster reassuring self-talk and gradual exposure to eye-contact scenarios starting with friends.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Dong notices progress in managing social anxiety, yet still encounters moments of awkwardness and second-guessing during interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Dong's growing awareness of the gap between his fears and reality, noting his initiative to tackle anxiety.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Dong’s ability to challenge negative predictions through cognitive restructuring, promote positive affirmations, and employ role-play to increase comfort in social settings.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Dong feels increased confidence yet continues to struggle with the fear of judgment during social interactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Dong is making progress in confidence-building but notes persistent underlying anxiety around negative judgment.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce “thought replacement” strategies to reduce anxiety and set small social interaction tasks to foster connection and practice new thought patterns.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices Dong’s growing excitement about social involvement, although anxiety about judgment persists in some interactions.

[Consultation Goals]

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Dong feels substantially more confident in social settings and less anxious when interacting with others.

[Observational Impressions]
