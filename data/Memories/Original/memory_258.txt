# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, referred to as C, has been struggling with social interactions, particularly with feelings of rejection and embarrassment from a recent incident involving their friends. C is eager to improve communication skills and gain a better understanding of how to navigate social situations effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
C experiences social anxiety and demonstrates a tendency towards negative self-assessment, though no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder has been mentioned.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
C feels upset and confused after an incident with friends, fearing judgment and misunderstanding, which has led to feelings of rejection and a preoccupation with the event.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that C is reflective and thoughtful about their behaviors but struggles with replaying negative interactions in their mind, indicating a need for addressing underlying fears of social rejection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to assist C in reducing anxiety by challenging negative thoughts and improving self-perception. Establish foundational strategies for effective communication and handling social conflict.
