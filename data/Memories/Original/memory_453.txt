# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is an individual who has recently lost his father and is experiencing mixed emotions of sadness and freedom. He feels overwhelmed about his future and family dynamics in the absence of his father's guidance, striving to find a balance between grief and personal growth.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei shows symptoms of grief and emotional conflict, struggling with feelings of inadequacy and guilt concerning familial roles and future aspirations, without a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels a mixture of sadness and newfound responsibility following his father's passing, grappling with uncertainty about personal decisions and family relationships.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei's openness in expressing complex emotions of loss and uncertainty, with a sense of longing for guidance and self-discovery.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei process grief while exploring avenues for personal growth. Encourage him to develop plans that integrate his father's values, aiding in the reconciliation of his grief with aspirations for the future.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei struggles with feelings of heaviness and isolation, finding it difficult to share his grief and fears burdening others with his emotions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei’s hesitation to openly express his grief to others, highlighting a fear of negative judgment and self-imposed expectations for emotional resilience.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support Zhang Wei in initiating open conversations about his feelings with trusted friends, and apply CBT strategies to challenge his perceptions of burdening others. Encourage self-compassion and gradual vulnerability in sharing emotions.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei remains hesitant to engage deeply in new social interactions, struggling with a fear of vulnerability and judgment, despite attending social activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's internal conflict between a desire for connection and the fear of misunderstanding, noting his inclination to anticipate worst-case scenarios.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide Zhang Wei in applying cognitive restructuring to reframe negative thought patterns during social interactions. Encourage incremental sharing of emotions to foster deeper connections and boost confidence in openness.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels increased pressure at work and struggles with feelings of inadequacy as he juggles new responsibilities against his personal grief.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's self-critical tendencies and the heightened standard of self-expectation, along with difficulty acknowledging personal successes amidst pressure.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement CBT strategies to challenge Zhang Wei’s negative self-perception and emphasize growth through mistakes. Promote effective task prioritization and open communication with colleagues to alleviate work-related stress.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei experiences a conflict between celebrating his work achievements and a lingering sadness from his father's loss, feeling guilty about his happiness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei’s internalized belief that happiness diminishes the memory of his father, alongside his difficulty in reconciling these conflicting emotions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Zhang Wei to explore balancing remembrance and joy by planning a celebration that honors his father. Use CBT to challenge guilt-related thoughts, fostering a supportive environment for experiencing happiness.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei is engaged in self-reflection concerning his life values and priorities, contemplating potential career changes that align more closely with his evolving sense of purpose.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Zhang Wei's reflective nature and emerging desire for a meaningful career change, though marked by apprehension due to fear of failure and external judgments.

[Consultation Goals]
