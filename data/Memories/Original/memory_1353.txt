# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student experiencing feelings of isolation and disconnection from classmates. Recently developed a strong interest in Qigong, which has led to skipping classes and social withdrawal.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits signs of social withdrawal and anxiety without indicating a formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels isolated and uncertain about returning to social interactions with classmates after focusing intensely on personal interests.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's regret over social withdrawal and a hesitance to reconnect, yet acknowledges the user's willingness to take steps toward re-engagement.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge assumptions about social rejection and encourage gradual re-entry into social circles. Foster understanding of the impact of personal interests on social well-being.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated by continued feelings of social misunderstanding and isolation despite attempts to reconnect with peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes the user's internal struggle with self-worth and misinterpretations of social feedback, but the user shows the ability to consider evidence against negative self-assumptions.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in preparing for social interactions by planning contributions and promoting current self-awareness to counter negative self-talk. Encourage self-compassion and persistence in social engagement efforts.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s entrenched beliefs about being unworthy of social connection but sees potential for recognizing external factors affecting peer responses.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize CBT strategies to help the user identify cognitive distortions and promote incremental social exposure, taking steps to understand and learn from social setbacks.

# Fourth Consultation
