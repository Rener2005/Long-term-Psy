# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Xiao Hua is a student experiencing significant social anxiety, with particular stress regarding interactions with peers and authority figures. Recent challenges include being reprimanded for late homework and feelings of inadequacy in social settings.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Xiao Hua shows symptoms of social anxiety but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a specific mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hua feels nervous and plagued by thoughts of inadequacy, especially after incidents where mistakes are noticed by others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Xiao Hua to be overwhelmed by self-criticism and worries about social interactions, but open to exploring supportive perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement cognitive behavioral techniques to help Xiao Hua shift focus from negative self-assessment to personal interests during social interactions. Develop strategies to build comfort in social settings using gradual exposure and positive reinforcement.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hua feels discouraged about not engaging in social activities, attributing it to self-doubt and perceived judgment from others.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Xiao Hua tends to magnify negative possibilities but is beginning to recognize positive interactions when prompted.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Xiao Hua to counter negative thoughts with evidence-based positive perspectives. Reinforce a gradual approach to social engagement to enhance self-assurance incrementally.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hua feels upset about not consistently keeping a diary but gains insights into personal emotions and behaviors.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees Xiao Hua as committed to personal growth, exhibiting both frustration with setbacks and moments of self-discovery.

[Consultation Goals]  
Strengthen Xiao Hua’s commitment to self-reflection through rewarding practices. Use CBT techniques to reinforce understanding and managing of social anxiety triggers.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hua experiences improvement in social interactions but continues to battle self-doubt during expressions of personal views.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Xiao Hua's growing attentiveness in listening and willingness to experiment with communication, despite underlying caution.

[Consultation Goals]  
Further develop Xiao Hua’s expressive confidence using role-play and reflective exercises. Encourage the application of “I” statements to foster effective communication.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Xiao Hua feels proud after a successful performance but is anxious about trying new activities or joining groups.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives Xiao Hua's increased self-esteem post-performance, though anticipatory anxiety continues to be a barrier.

[Consultation Goals]  
Motivate Xiao Hua to leverage recent confidence into exploring new group activities by framing them as low-pressure opportunities. Begin planning first visits to club meetings to reduce pressure and build familiarity.
