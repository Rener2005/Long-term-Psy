# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Ms. Zhang is a project manager who experiences feelings of anger and frustration, particularly in workplace settings when facing conflicts with colleagues. She desires to improve her emotional responses and manage stress effectively, especially in high-pressure project management situations.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Ms. Zhang exhibits symptoms of stress and difficulty managing anger in conflict scenarios at work. Her self-reported tendencies suggest potential benefits from thematically focusing on emotional regulation and communication skills improvement, though no formal mental health diagnosis has been disclosed.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Ms. Zhang reports feeling overwhelmed and helpless due to work pressures and conflicts with colleagues, experiencing difficulty in controlling her emotional reactions.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes Ms. Zhang's struggles with perceived challenges to her authority and competence, resulting in exaggerated emotional responses, but notes her willingness to explore triggers and improve emotional regulation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on identifying cognitive and physiological triggers of anger and developing cognitive behavioral strategies to challenge and replace negative thoughts. Introduce techniques such as emotional diary-keeping to foster deeper self-awareness and support emotional management in workplace interactions.
