# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Lin (pseudonym) is a student dealing with anxiety related to academic performance and social interactions, particularly after a recent physical altercation with a classmate. Lin is actively seeking ways to navigate and mend these social challenges.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Lin exhibits symptoms of social anxiety and low self-confidence but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Lin feels anxious and isolated after a recent conflict with a classmate, fearing social rejection and struggling with self-perception as a "troublemaker."

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Lin's tendency towards negative self-comparison and absolute thinking, yet notes Lin's openness to reconsider self-perceptions and rebuild social connections.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Lin reframe negative and absolute thinking patterns, and develop incremental strategies for re-engaging with classmates to reduce social anxiety.
