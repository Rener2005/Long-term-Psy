# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Yang (pseudonym) is an adult who struggles with feelings of emotional instability, often experiencing sudden shifts between laughter and crying. He describes recent challenges in adjusting to his current situation, reflecting on past family experiences that elicit both positive and negative emotions.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Yang experiences symptoms of emotional distress and struggles with self-worth, particularly influenced by his past experiences and family reflections. There is no formal diagnosis mentioned.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Yang feels overwhelmed by rapid emotional shifts and is weighed down by memories of family and past experiences.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Yang's difficulty in managing emotional turbulence and perceives a strong longing for connection and understanding, underlined by self-directed anger and sadness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to aid Yang in identifying and reframing negative thoughts and increase his ability to express emotions constructively. Encourage him to consider potential support systems for sharing his feelings.
