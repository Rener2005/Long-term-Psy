# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a grieving parent dealing with the loss of a child due to an accident, resulting in profound feelings of loss and guilt. The user describes struggling with daily activities and is in the process of physical rehabilitation.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user exhibits symptoms of depression and complex grief, notably marked by overwhelming guilt and self-blame. There is no mention of a formal diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels lost and burdened by grief, experiencing a lack of joy and pervasive feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes intense self-blame and hopelessness, with the user demonstrating a significant struggle to engage in self-forgiveness and self-compassion.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user recognize and challenge guilt-related thoughts and encourage the expression of emotions to alleviate some of the burdens of grief.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel overwhelmed by guilt and experiences intense emotional reactions when recalling memories of their deceased child.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes a persistent cycle of guilt and sorrow but observes the user's openness to challenging guilt-induced thoughts with alternative perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in developing a practice of self-compassion and using affirmations to counter guilt. Encourage exploration of activities that offer emotional relief.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels heavy and burdened by recurring memories and emotions when alone, leading to sadness and anger.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a struggle with avoidance behaviors and the user's discomfort in processing traumatic memories. Nonetheless, there is acknowledgment of the user’s emotional awareness.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate the user in facing distressing memories in a controlled manner and explore small, manageable steps towards rediscovering joy in past interests.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's engagement in the rehabilitation process while grappling with self-doubt and anxiety regarding physical recovery.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in acknowledging small rehabilitation achievements and motivate using attainable goals and self-kindness tactics to counter negative self-talk.

# Fifth Consultation

[Observational Impressions]

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to celebrate small accomplishments and engage family support to achieve rehabilitation goals, emphasizing gradual adaptation and self-acceptance.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling slightly better, with a cautious approach towards future goals but still grapples with motivation and acceptance of their current situation.

[Observational Impressions]
