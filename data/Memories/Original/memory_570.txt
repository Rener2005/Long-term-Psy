# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is experiencing hand tremors and numbness, impacting daily activities and leading to feelings of frustration and helplessness. They are concerned about their ability to participate in family activities and seek solutions to improve hand function.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user acknowledges a potential psychosomatic component to their hand issues and experiences anxiety related to future work prospects.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated and anxious due to the physical limitations caused by hand tremors and numbness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user's negative thoughts about their hand condition contribute to feelings of helplessness and withdrawal from social and professional activities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to challenge negative thought patterns and develop strategies for engaging in enjoyable activities, reducing anxiety, and reclaiming control over their life.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reflects on new ideas but feels uncertain about implementing them due to past disappointments with treatments.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's skepticism toward new exercises but perceives a willingness to explore them if properly guided.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage experimentation with small, manageable exercises to identify effective strategies while simultaneously addressing underlying skepticism using cognitive restructuring.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels optimistic after gaining new insights and support, and expresses a proactive attitude towards handling their hand condition.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a shift from skepticism to cautious optimism as the user becomes more open to integrating exercises into their routine.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce the user's proactive mindset by establishing a consistent exercise routine and setting realistic, achievable goals, while challenging lingering doubts about efficacy.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more confident as improvements in hand function motivate positive outlooks and engagement in daily activities.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user’s increased confidence and motivation, but recognizes challenges maintaining consistency amidst personal responsibilities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Implement strategies for creating consistent routines and enhancing motivation, such as linking exercises to daily habits and seeking social support, to sustain progress.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels greater confidence and a sense of accomplishment through engaging in fine motor skill exercises.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist perceives a significant positive shift in self-perception and determination to tackle previous limitations.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage continued development of hand skills by setting new challenges, fostering self-compassion, and celebrating small achievements to reinforce motivation and progress.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is feeling optimistic and active, excited to engage in social and work activities due to enhanced hand function.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist acknowledges the user’s significant progress and enthusiasm for maintaining improvements and setting new goals.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in sustaining progress through setting long-term goals, maintaining social connections, and using self-monitoring tools like journaling to track achievements and motivation.
