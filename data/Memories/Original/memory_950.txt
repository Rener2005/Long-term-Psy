# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Lao Qin is an elderly individual experiencing frustration with a slow recovery process following a medical procedure. He worries about being a burden to his family and feels anxious about his ability to become self-sufficient again.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Lao Qin experiences symptoms of anxiety and low mood related to his recovery, but no formal mental health disorder diagnosis has been provided.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Lao Qin feels frustrated, anxious, and worried about being a burden to his family, which affects his motivation for rehabilitation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Lao Qin is weighed down by negative thoughts concerning his recovery speed and familial burden, yet he is open to recognizing his progress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive behavioral techniques to help Lao Qin focus on small achievements to alleviate frustration and anxiety, fostering a sense of hope and encouraging self-compassion.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Lao Qin feels frustrated and anxious about perceived slow recovery and expresses concerns about self-worth tied to recovery speed.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that Lao Qin struggles with self-comparison and feelings of inadequacy but acknowledges family support and is open to reframing negative thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage Lao Qin to set realistic, small goals in his recovery to foster a sense of accomplishment, and help him focus on individualized progress.

# Third Consultation

[Mental State]

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Lao Qin feels burdened by guilt from relying on family but is open to communicative strategies for expressing feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate Lao Qin in expressing his feelings of guilt to his family to strengthen bonds and alleviate emotional burden, using role-playing exercises.
