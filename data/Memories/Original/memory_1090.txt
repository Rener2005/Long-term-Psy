# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Wang (pseudonym) is an adult male who is currently unemployed and struggles with feelings of inadequacy after not meeting familial expectations. He has recently been using alcohol and smoking as coping mechanisms and is experiencing significant stress and uncertainty about his future.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Wang experiences symptoms of anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Wang feels overwhelmed and lack of motivation, experiencing anxiety and shame due to beliefs of personal failure and disappointing his parents.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Wang has internalized familial expectations, leading to self-judgment and harmful coping mechanisms. He shows a willingness to explore healthier coping strategies and self-reflection.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Wang identify and challenge negative thought patterns. Encourage healthier coping mechanisms and foster an understanding of the discrepancies between self-expectations and reality.
