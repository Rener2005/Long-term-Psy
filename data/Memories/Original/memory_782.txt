# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user, a university student, feels overwhelmed by academic pressures and interpersonal conflicts. Struggles with self-expectations and seeks support in managing these pressures and emotions effectively.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms of anxiety tied to performance expectations and interpersonal relations but has not reported a formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels anxious and restless, struggling with a persistent fear of failing academically and not meeting self-imposed standards of perfection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes a pattern of negative self-talk and comparison with peers, coupled with an aversion to showing vulnerability, yet willingness to consider more self-compassionate perspectives.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user identify and challenge negative thoughts, foster self-compassion, and develop realistic academic and interpersonal expectations.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels frustrated and misunderstood in attempts to resolve conflicts with classmates, exacerbating feelings of anxiety and social isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's negative perceptions of communication skills, despite openness to considering situational factors in misunderstandings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user in reframing beliefs about personal communication abilities and employ CBT strategies to approach conversations with curiosity and clarity, emphasizing self-compassion.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and disheartened by recent academic setbacks, leading to self-doubt and heightened anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes difficulty in overcoming negative automatic thoughts about academic capabilities, alongside the challenge of maintaining motivation under stress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in breaking down negative thinking patterns using CBT, encourage small, manageable study objectives, and reinforce successes to build confidence.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user's steps toward engagement in school activities bring some relief to anxiety, yet struggles remain with self-doubt and social comparison.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes the user's difficulty in maintaining progress amidst lingering self-critical thoughts but acknowledges their growing willingness to engage with supportive peers.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage more active participation in group activities and develop strategies for positive thinking and self-acceptance, leveraging peer support to mitigate isolation.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user begins to experience improved confidence and social integration at school yet remains concerned about ongoing academic and relational challenges.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes increased self-awareness and adaptability in managing social dynamics, alongside the user's continued vigilance in balancing academic commitments.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce positive cognitive restructuring and maintain engagement in valued activities, using CBT to address lingering anxieties around interpersonal conflicts and academic performance.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels significantly more positive and connected to classmates, leading to better academic motivation and reduced isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes meaningful improvements in the user's social interactions and academic collaboration, alongside a supported, empowered outlook on potential challenges. 

[Consultation Goals]  
Sustain open communication and collaborative strategies developed, encouraging proactive stress management techniques during exam periods to support both academic and social growth.
