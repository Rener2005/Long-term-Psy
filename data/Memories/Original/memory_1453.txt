# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is a student who is struggling with academic performance and parental expectations, leading to feelings of inadequacy and anxiety. The user is trying to navigate these pressures while seeking methods to improve personal well-being.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user displays symptoms consistent with anxiety and low self-esteem. There has been no formal diagnosis, but present struggles suggest significant stress related to familial and academic pressures.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and frustrated by academic challenges and perceives strong parental expectations as a source of anxiety.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes that the user is trapped in a cycle of negative self-evaluation but shows openness to explore supportive, self-compassionate strategies.

[Consultation Goals]  
Employ cognitive behavioral techniques to help the user challenge pervasive negative thoughts and recognize personal achievements. Encourage the use of a journal to record positive experiences to counterbalance anxiety.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports feelings of boredom and aimlessness after attempting to reduce phone usage as a distracting mechanism.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that the user struggles with maintaining motivation for activities and experiences social isolation, but displays curiosity about re-engaging with old hobbies like drawing.

[Consultation Goals]  
Facilitate exploration of new and old leisure activities to enrich daily life. Utilize CBT methods to reframe perceptions about social interaction, promoting gradual, manageable exposure to social situations.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Anxiety persists for the user, particularly in social situations, generating fears of judgment and inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notices ingrained negative assumptions about self-worth in social interactions, yet the user is starting to acknowledge unsupported beliefs, suggesting potential for cognitive reappraisal.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to recognize and challenge unsupported negative assumptions. Foster gradual social exposure, using CBT techniques to build more constructive social viewpoints and interactions.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Feelings of uncertainty and doubt accompany the user's attempts to improve academic performance amid parental expectations, leading to frustration and hopelessness.

[Observational Impressions]  
The user displays a tendency toward all-or-nothing thinking and struggles to recognize incremental successes but shows openness to acknowledging even small achievements.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in setting realistic, incremental academic goals. Use CBT techniques to encourage recognition of any progress and facilitate open communication with parents about anxiety towards expectations.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user grapples with pressure from parental expectations, driven by a fear of disappointment, affecting self-esteem and emotional connection.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist sees potential in the user's growing insight into how familial pressure impacts them. The user expresses a willingness to communicate openly with parents about these feelings.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user through initiating open dialogues with parents to express feelings about expectations. Develop strategies to reframe and manage thoughts of self-worth related to academic results.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels more confident with improved scholastic performance and relationships, showing an optimistic outlook towards sustaining these changes.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes increased self-efficacy and positivity in the user. Continued exploration and communication with parents and peers contribute to this progress.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to maintain positive habits and strategies for self-improvement and anxiety management. Reinforce the benefits of open communication and structured study plans alongside supportive social engagements.
