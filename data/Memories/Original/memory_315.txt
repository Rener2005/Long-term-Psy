# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Zhang Wei is a professional experiencing self-doubt and anxiety regarding work performance and peer comparisons. Despite recent achievements, Zhang Wei often questions personal worth and contribution in a competitive work environment.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Zhang Wei experiences symptoms of anxiety and low self-esteem but has not disclosed any formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei feels unsure and anxious about work performance, doubting personal worth despite completing an important project.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes that Zhang Wei struggles with feelings of inadequacy and self-comparison, often feeling like an imposter despite positive feedback.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral techniques to help Zhang Wei challenge negative, self-limiting thoughts and recognize individual achievements. Encourage strategies for maintaining self-esteem during feedback.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Zhang Wei continues to experience intense self-criticism, fixating on perceived shortcomings despite acknowledgment from colleagues.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Zhang Wei’s tendency to dwell on negative incidents, often minimizing personal successes.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist Zhang Wei in redirecting focus from failures to achievements, employing CBT to balance self-criticism with affirmations of competence.
