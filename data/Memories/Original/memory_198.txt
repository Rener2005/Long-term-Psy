# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
Li is a university student dealing with significant anxiety, driven by recent familial stress due to his father's health diagnosis. This anxiety impacts his studies and overall mental well-being, causing him to feel overwhelmed and responsible for his family's support.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
Li experiences symptoms of anxiety and stress related to family health issues, although there is no formal diagnosis of a mental health disorder.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is experiencing high levels of anxiety and a sense of being overwhelmed, particularly concerning his father's health and the accompanying responsibility.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li’s sense of obligation towards his family and his internal struggle with anxiety-driven thoughts about the future.

[Consultation Goals]  
Introduce cognitive behavioral techniques to manage anxiety, focusing on relaxation methods and reaching out for social support to prevent isolation.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels slightly relieved due to a scholarship alleviating financial strain but continues to be anxious about his father's health, with added feelings of guilt.

[Observational Impressions]  
Li's worry and guilt are tied to family expectations and personal pressures, affecting his ability to focus on academic responsibilities.

[Consultation Goals]  
Use cognitive restructuring to address guilt and anxiety, and promote balanced self-care strategies, including self-compassion and open family communication.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li is still anxious and feels overwhelmed, with racing thoughts about academics and family obligations hindering his concentration.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist notes Li's cycle of avoidance in response to stress and anxiety, indicating deeper-rooted fears of failure.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage identification of negative automatic thoughts and develop practical time management strategies to reduce academic pressure.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels slightly improved and finds purpose in volunteering, yet remains anxious when isolated due to his father’s condition.

[Observational Impressions]  
Li’s engagement in community activities suggests increased social connectivity but demonstrates episodic anxiety related to family concerns.

[Consultation Goals]  
Reinforce involvement in social and community activities to enhance emotional resilience and develop positive self-talk for anxiety management.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
Li feels more hopeful with recent positive family developments, leading to a renewed focus on personal goals and academic interests.

[Observational Impressions]  
The therapist observes Li's increased optimism and goal orientation, as well as a shift toward proactive involvement in academic and social contexts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support continued use of cognitive behavioral strategies to maintain optimism, promote social connections through academic clubs, and encourage ongoing self-reflection and goal setting.
