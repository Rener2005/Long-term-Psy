# First Consultation

## User Background
[Personal Profile]  
The user is currently undergoing military service, where they feel pressured and struggle with adapting to the strict environment. They often experience loneliness and feel overwhelmed by the circumstances.

[Assessment and Diagnosis]  
The user experiences symptoms related to anxiety and low mood, but has not disclosed any formal mental health diagnosis.

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels overwhelmed and lonely due to the pressure of military training and struggles with feelings of inadequacy.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes that the user is highly self-critical, comparing their performance to peers, and doubting their abilities, but they show openness to challenging these thoughts.

[Consultation Goals]  
Utilize cognitive behavioral therapy to help the user identify and challenge negative self-perceptions, recognize their achievements, and develop a focus on personal progress and stress management.

# Second Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user remains low and anxious, facing challenges in social interactions with misunderstandings deepening feelings of isolation.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes the user’s struggle with perceiving social exclusion and their tendency to assume negative views from others but sees potential in the user’s ability to look at situations from a different perspective.

[Consultation Goals]  
Assist the user using CBT techniques to reframe negative social beliefs and encourage engagement in small-group discussions to improve social skills.

# Third Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user is experiencing emotional swings and lacks sleep, largely due to stress about performance and fear of failure impacting their studies and daily life.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes patterns of all-or-nothing thinking and notes the user’s tendency to feel paralyzed by fear of failure.

[Consultation Goals]  
Encourage the user to establish small, manageable study goals and integrate relaxation techniques to break the cycle of anxiety and improve sleep.

# Fourth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user continues to feel a sense of loneliness despite understanding the importance of family support and struggles with reaching out to peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant sees potential for connection in the user’s recent efforts but notes recurring assumptions that may not reflect reality, contributing to their sense of isolation.

[Consultation Goals]  
Guide the user in challenging negative assumptions and encourage participation in group activities to foster a sense of belonging and connection.

# Fifth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user reports a positive shift after engaging in group activities, finding relief from anxiety and enjoying a sense of camaraderie.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant observes an increase in the user’s openness and willingness to engage socially, signaling a positive change in their interpersonal dynamics.

[Consultation Goals]  
Support the user in maintaining and expanding positive social interactions through regular activities and reinforce visualization techniques to manage social anxiety.

# Sixth Consultation

## Conversation Memory
[Mental State]  
The user feels optimistic after successfully adjusting to military life, experiencing improved communication and reduced anxiety with their peers.

[Observational Impressions]  
The consultant notes a significant transformation in the user’s proactive approach to challenges and their ability to reflect on and learn from past experiences.

[Consultation Goals]  
Focus on building the user’s confidence to express themselves in larger group settings by setting small goals, practicing self-confidence techniques, and seeking supportive feedback.
